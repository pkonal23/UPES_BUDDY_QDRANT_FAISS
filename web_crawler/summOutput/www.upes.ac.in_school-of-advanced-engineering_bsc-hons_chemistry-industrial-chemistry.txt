The document outlines the Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) program in Chemistry with a specialization in Industrial Chemistry offered by the UPES School of Advanced Engineering. The program is structured over eight semesters, integrating both theoretical knowledge and practical skills essential for careers in various sectors such as pharmaceuticals, petrochemicals, and materials science.

### Program Overview:
- **Curriculum:** 
  - The program includes core subjects like Organic Chemistry, Inorganic Chemistry, Physical Chemistry, and Analytical Techniques, with specialized courses in Industrial Chemistry. 
  - Key courses across semesters include Advanced Organic Chemistry, Chemical Thermodynamics, Quantum Chemistry, and various laboratory courses focusing on practical applications.
  - Emphasis is placed on green chemical synthesis, energy-efficient processes, and analytical techniques to address environmental concerns.

- **Research and Industry Collaboration:** 
  - The program encourages meaningful research collaborations with international institutions, providing students with valuable networking opportunities and exposure to real-world applications.
  
- **Career Opportunities:**
  - Graduates can pursue roles such as Research Scientists, Quality Managers, Consultant Chemists, and Chemical Analysts, among others.
  - The program prepares students for higher studies and competitive exams like JAM, enhancing their career prospects.

### Admission and Eligibility:
- **Eligibility Criteria:** 
  - Candidates must have a minimum of 50% marks in Class X and XII with a focus on Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM/B).
  
- **Selection Process:** 
  - Admission is determined through a Personal Interview assessing the candidate's performance.

### Additional Program Features:
- **Hands-on Learning:** 
  - Access to advanced facilities for chemical synthesis and characterization allows for practical skill development.
  
- **Skill Enhancement:** 
  - The curriculum includes Skill Enhancement Electives and focuses on critical thinking, leadership, teamwork, and scientific communication.

- **Exit Options:** 
  - As per the National Education Policy (NEP), multiple exit options are available, allowing students to earn certificates, diplomas, or degrees based on the credits secured (40, 80, 120, 160).
