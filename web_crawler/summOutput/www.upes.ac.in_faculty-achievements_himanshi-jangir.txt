Dr. Himanshi Jangir, an Assistant Professor at the School of Health Sciences and Technology at UPES, has achieved a significant milestone by receiving the Sree Ramakrishna Paramahamsa Research Grants 2024 for Agriculture. This prestigious grant is provided by the Sree Padmavathi Venkateswara Foundation (Sree PVF) based in Vijayawada, Andhra Pradesh. 

Her awarded project is titled “A Novel Eco-Friendly Nano-Agriculture Strategy for Small-Marginal Farmers of Higher Himalayas: A Nano-Pyrite Based Seed/Root/Shoot Treatment Approach for Improving Potato Yield and Dairy Green Fodder Production.” The primary objective of this project is to enhance agricultural practices specifically tailored for small and marginal farmers in the Higher Himalayas. 

The Sree PVF foundation will support Dr. Jangir's research with funding amounting to INR 47,96,000 over a two-year period, which will facilitate the implementation of her innovative agricultural strategies.
