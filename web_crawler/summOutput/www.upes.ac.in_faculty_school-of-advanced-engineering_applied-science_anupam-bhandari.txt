Dr. Anupam Bhandari is an esteemed academic and researcher, currently serving as an Associate Professor at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES). He has garnered recognition for his outstanding research in ferrofluids, which earned him a research award from the university. His contributions extend beyond research; he has actively participated as a group leader in the National Children's Science Congress representing Uttarakhand, where he showcased his project "Traditional Seeds," receiving a certificate from the Government of Uttarakhand.

Dr. Bhandari's research interests primarily focus on Ferrohydrodynamics, a specialized area within fluid mechanics. He has welcomed Dr. Tristan Partridge from the University of California, Santa Barbara, as a visiting researcher for joint studies. His commitment to social movements is evident through his invitation to speak on the Beej Bachao Andolan by the Human Rights Law Network in New Delhi in 2008. He has dedicated time to educating international students about significant social movements like the Chipko Movement and Beej Bachao Movement prevalent in the Garhwal Himalayan region.

In collaboration with Dr. Partridge, Dr. Bhandari co-authored an article discussing the history and origins of the Chipko Movement, along with two additional pieces on environmental issues. He also actively participated in the Aarakot-Askot foot march to gather traditional paddy seeds in Uttarakhand and delivered a lecture on "Advanced Computational Techniques in Science and Engineering" at NIT Uttarakhand.

Prior to his tenure at UPES, Dr. Bhandari completed his Ph.D. at NIT Kurukshetra, where he received a full academic scholarship from the Ministry of Human Resource Development (MHRD) and demonstrated exceptional performance throughout his studies. He teaches a variety of courses, including Engineering Mathematics, Differential Equations, Complex Analysis, Group Theory, Numerical Analysis, and Multivariable Calculus.

Dr. Bhandari is not only recognized for his academic prowess but also for his active involvement in environmental campaigns, such as the Chipko Movement, Beej Bachao Andolan, and the Anti-Mining Movement, particularly in Henvalghati, Tehri Garhwal. His teaching philosophy emphasizes building a strong foundation of ideas and learning techniques, fostering critical thinking, effective communication, and problem-solving skills among students to prepare them for future academic and professional endeavors.
