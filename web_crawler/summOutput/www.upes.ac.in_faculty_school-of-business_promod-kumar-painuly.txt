Promod Kumar Painuly is an esteemed professional in the oil and gas sector with over 30 years of diverse industry experience, including significant roles such as Chief Geophysicist at ONGC, Exploration Manager for Reliance Industries, and Exploration Manager for Repsol in West Africa and Latin America. He joined the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) in July 2017 as an Industry Fellow in Oil & Gas. His research interests encompass oil equity acquisition, asset allocation, exploration risk assessment, portfolio management, natural gas markets, unconventional resources, and digitization in oil and gas projects.

Painuly has organized and executed geological expeditions, notably for Spanish geoscientists, with published results. He is recognized both nationally and internationally as a leading oil and gas professional and has contributed to various professional societies, including the American Association of Petroleum Geologists (AAPG), the European Association of Geoscientists & Engineers (EAGE), and the Society of Petroleum Geophysicists (SPG). He has also played a vital role as a task force member for the Ministry of Petroleum & Natural Gas (MOPNG) in India, contributing to the Digital Exploration and Production (E&P) Roadmap and providing technical audits for government public sector undertakings (PSUs).

His collaborations extend to international energy agencies such as the International Energy Agency (IEA) and NITI Aayog, along with partnerships with Indian companies like Essar Oil, ONGC, and Cairn India, as well as the University of Adelaide. Painuly's teaching philosophy integrates theory with project-based learning, focusing on contemporary industry issues and problem-solving. He enhances student exposure to the industry through visits to prominent institutions and labs in Dehradun, and he incorporates certification programs in assessments.

In addition to his teaching role, Painuly has served as a distinguished educator and trainer for various professionals, including those at ONGC and Accenture, and has taught under the EU-Erasmus grant at the University of Maribor, Slovenia. He has presented technical papers at international conferences, contributing to discussions on smart city development and deepwater exploration risks.
