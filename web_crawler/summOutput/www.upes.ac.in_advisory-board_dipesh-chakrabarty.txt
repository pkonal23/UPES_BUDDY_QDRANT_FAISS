Dipesh Chakrabarty holds the position of Lawrence A. Kimpton Distinguished Service Professor in History, South Asian Languages and Civilizations, and the College at the University of Chicago. He serves as the Faculty Director of the University of Chicago Centre in Delhi, is a faculty fellow at the Chicago Centre for Contemporary Theory, and is affiliated with the Department of English and the Law School by courtesy. His academic credentials include a BSc (Physics Honours) from Presidency College, University of Calcutta, a postgraduate Diploma in Management from the Indian Institute of Management, Calcutta, and a PhD in History from the Australian National University.

Chakrabarty is a prominent figure in academia, being a founding member of the editorial collective of Subaltern Studies, a consulting editor for Critical Inquiry, and a founding editor of Postcolonial Studies. He has contributed to editorial boards for prestigious journals such as the American Historical Review and Public Culture. Additionally, he co-founded the series South Asia Across the Disciplines with Sheldon Pollock and Sanjay Subrahmanyam, published by a consortium of university presses including Chicago, Columbia, and California.

His international academic engagement is extensive, having been a visiting scholar and distinguished lecturer at renowned institutions such as Harvard, Yale, Princeton, University of Toronto, University College London, and various others across Europe and Australia.

Chakrabarty has received numerous accolades, including the 2014 Toynbee Foundation Prize for contributions to global history and the 2019 Tagore Memorial Prize from the Government of West Bengal for his book *The Crises of Civilization*. He has been awarded honorary doctorates from the École Normale Supérieure in Paris (2021), the University of London (2010), and the University of Antwerp (2011). In 2011, he also received the Distinguished Alumnus Award from the Indian Institute of Management, Calcutta. His fellowships include being an honorary fellow of the Australian Academy of the Humanities (2006) and a fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences (2004).

Prof. Chakrabarty has authored and edited numerous influential works, including *Rethinking Working-Class History: Bengal 1890-1940*, *Provincializing Europe: Postcolonial Thought and Historical Difference*, *Habitations of Modernity*, *The Calling of History*, *The Crises of Civilization*, and *The Climate of History in a Planetary Age*. His writings have been translated into multiple languages, including German, French, Spanish, Russian, Turkish, Korean, Portuguese, Chinese, and Arabic.
