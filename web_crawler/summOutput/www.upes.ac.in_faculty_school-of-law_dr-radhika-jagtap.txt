Dr. Radhika Jagtap, an Assistant Professor at the School of Law (SOL) at UPES, Dehradun, has a rich academic background and extensive experience in public international law and gender studies. She holds an M.Phil and Ph.D. in International Legal Studies from the Centre for International Legal Studies at Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU), New Delhi, and is a graduate of the Institute of Law, Nirma University, Ahmedabad. Before her tenure at UPES, she taught for approximately 2.6 years at Symbiosis Law School, Pune, where she was actively involved in the Gender Championsâ€™ cell, promoting gender sensitization and awareness.

Dr. Jagtap's research interests focus on the intersections of gender justice, feminist jurisprudence, public international law, and labor laws. Her doctoral thesis, defended in 2021, examined the relationship between social movements and public international law, highlighting the overlooked narratives of non-western histories in the discipline. She has published and presented her work in various prestigious national and international journals, including those by Oxford University Press and Duncker & Humblot, and has participated in significant academic forums such as the Max Planck Institute for Comparative Public Law and International Law in Germany.

In her teaching, Dr. Jagtap emphasizes a collaborative, student-centric approach, believing in the importance of education that fosters autonomy and critical thinking. She teaches courses in international law, including International Criminal Law, Humanitarian and Refugee Law, International Organizations Law, Labor Laws, Gender Justice, and Feminist Jurisprudence. Currently, she is also teaching Insurance Law.

Dr. Jagtap has been recognized for her academic excellence, having been a gold medalist in her LLM batch and receiving foreign travel grants for her research presentations at notable institutions in Germany. She is committed to continuous learning and engagement in academia, both for herself and her students.
