**Pharmacovigilance:**
- The field of pharmacovigilance (PV) is increasingly important in India, given the country's significant role in global pharmaceutical manufacturing and the need for drug safety monitoring. It involves the detection, assessment, and prevention of adverse drug reactions (ADRs).
- B.Pharma graduates are well-positioned for careers in pharmacovigilance due to their foundational knowledge in pharmacology. Entry-level roles such as Drug Safety Associate typically offer salaries ranging from INR 3-5 lakhs annually, while mid-level positions can command INR 10-15 lakhs.
- Certifications from recognized bodies like the Indian Society for Clinical Research (ISCR) and courses in Good Pharmacovigilance Practices (GVP) are recommended for enhancing employability and career advancement.
- As regulatory scrutiny increases, particularly from agencies like the Central Drugs Standard Control Organization (CDSCO), the demand for skilled professionals in this field is expected to rise, especially with the integration of technologies like AI and big data analytics.
