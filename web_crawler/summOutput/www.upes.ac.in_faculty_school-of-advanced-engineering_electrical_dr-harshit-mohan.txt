Dr. Harshit Mohan is a notable entrepreneur and researcher focused on fostering innovation and entrepreneurship among youth. He graduated with a degree in Electrical Engineering from Aligarh Muslim University (AMU) in 2014 and subsequently earned his master's and doctoral degrees from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Roorkee in 2017 and 2022, respectively. His achievements include receiving the DST Nidhi Prayas 2021 scheme, which provided him with a product development grant of INR 1 million. Dr. Mohan has authored over 20 scholarly articles in reputable journals, conferences, and book chapters, and he holds three patents in India, reflecting his significant contributions to the field.

His research interests are diverse, covering Power Electronics, Electrical Machines, Industrial Drives, Fuzzy Neural Systems, Internet of Things (IoT), Embedded Systems, Entrepreneurship, as well as practical applications of MATLAB and Simulink. Dr. Mohan has been recognized for his work on the "PAANI Drives" project, which focuses on Smart Variable Frequency Drives used in irrigation, earning him appreciation from the National Water Development Agency and THDC India Limited.

As an Assistant Professor at the School of Advanced Engineering, he teaches various courses including Electrical Machines, Electrical Drives, Power Electronics, Microprocessors, PLC & SCADA, MATLAB, Simulink, UAVs, and Electric Vehicles (EVs). His teaching philosophy emphasizes not only knowledge transfer but also the development of problem-solving skills among students, encouraging them to consider the societal implications of their work. He integrates hands-on learning experiences with theoretical concepts, particularly in electrical and electronic laboratories, and utilizes software tools for enhanced learning.

Dr. Mohan is also an active member of IEEE and other professional societies, having reviewed over 35 articles for national and international journals and conferences. His research work has earned him several "Best Paper Awards" at various conferences. Before his current role at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES), he successfully incubated his startup, HMV Drives Pvt Ltd, at TIDES, IIT Roorkee.
