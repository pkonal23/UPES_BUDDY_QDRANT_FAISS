**Bachelor of Science (Hons.) Mathematics Program at University of Petroleum and Energy Studies School of Advanced Engineering**

**Program Overview**

The Bachelor of Science (Hons.) Mathematics program at University of Petroleum and Energy Studies School of Advanced Engineering is a comprehensive four-year undergraduate program aligned with the National Education Policy 2020 framework. It provides students with a flexible and dynamic curriculum, enabling them to tailor their education to their interests and career aspirations.

**Program Highlights**

* **Multiple Exit and Entry Options:** Students have the flexibility to exit with a certificate (40 credits), diploma (80 credits), or undergraduate degree (120 credits) depending on their preferences and progress.
* **Specializations:** Students can specialize in Data Science and Computational Mathematics through electives and additional courses.
* **Research-Oriented Curriculum:** The program includes research-specific courses to prepare students for their dissertations in the final year.
* **Global Learning Environment:** The program fosters a stimulating and diverse learning environment that nurtures creativity and global perspectives.
* **Advanced Research Labs:** State-of-the-art research labs provide students with hands-on experience and cutting-edge resources for their research.

**Career Opportunities**

Graduates of the Bachelor of Science (Hons.) Mathematics program have a wide range of career opportunities, including:

* **Higher Studies:** Master of Science, doctorate, or PhD in various fields
* **Industrial Employment:** Research and Development, data science, financial analysis, actuarial science, operations research, quantitative analysis
* **Research Institutions and Academia:** Research roles in academia or government organizations

**Curriculum**

The program consists of eight semesters with a maximum of 20 credits per semester. The curriculum includes courses in linear algebra, differential calculus, real analysis, ordinary differential equations, mechanics, complex analysis, partial differential equations, numerical analysis, and abstract algebra. Students also take courses in skill enhancement electives, minors, and research methodology.

**Eligibility and Selection Criteria**

* **Eligibility:** Minimum 50% marks in Class X and XII (PCM/B)
* **Selection Criteria:** Performance in a personal interview
