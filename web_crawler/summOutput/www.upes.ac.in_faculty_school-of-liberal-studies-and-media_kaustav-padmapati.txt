Dr. Kaustav Padmapati is an accomplished scholar and assistant professor specializing in political science, international relations, American studies, and gender studies. His primary research interests encompass global politics, particularly focusing on China, Taiwan, East and Southeast Asia, US politics, foreign policy, queer and gender studies, and cultural studies. He completed his PhD in the American Studies Programme at the School of International Studies, Jawaharlal Nehru University, New Delhi. Dr. Padmapati has a rich professional background, having worked at various esteemed institutions including Sahitya Akademi, Thought Arbitrage Research Institution, the Foundation for National Security Research, and Macmillan Publishing Solutions.

Dr. Padmapati's teaching philosophy is interdisciplinary, emphasizing interactive learning and employing pedagogical tools inspired by intersectionality. He strives to create a positive educational environment through group discussions, assignments, and class activities that address critical social issues such as caste, class, gender, race, and ethnicity. He teaches a range of undergraduate and graduate courses, including “Why Media Matters?”, “Media Laws – Regulation, Governance, Ethics”, “Global Media and Politics”, and “Media and Gender Studies”. He also offers skill enhancement courses on blogging and panel discussions.

He actively engages in scholarly activities, presenting papers at numerous national and international seminars. Dr. Padmapati's forthcoming edited volume, “Queer Voices from the Periphery: A Collection of Perspectives from Northeast India,” aims to highlight the challenges faced by the queer community in Northeast India, fostering critical discussions among students on relevant global issues such as China's rise and its implications for India.

Dr. Padmapati has received several prestigious awards and fellowships, including the Fulbright Nehru Doctoral Research Fellowship, which allowed him to work as a Visiting Fulbright Research Scholar at the Center for Asian Studies, American University, Washington, D.C. He was also recognized for his research performance by the Royal Global University, Guwahati, in 2021, and has been involved in various international projects, including collaborations with Japan and strategic dialogues with countries like Korea and China.
