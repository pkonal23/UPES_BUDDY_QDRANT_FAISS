**Research Interests and Scholarly Activities**

Ms. Yadav's research interests are diverse, encompassing constitutional law, media law, and legal theory. She is particularly focused on examining various legal frameworks and their implications on economic and social systems. Her multidisciplinary approach allows her to explore the intricate dynamics between law, economics, media, and society, analyzing how these fields influence one another.

In addition to her research contributions in esteemed academic journals and law reviews, she has authored two books that address critical issues within media law, including freedom of speech, media regulation, privacy concerns, and the legal implications of emerging technologies. These publications contribute significantly to the scholarly discourse surrounding media law.

**Teaching Philosophy and Courses Taught**

Ms. Yadav adopts a student-centered teaching philosophy, recognizing the diverse backgrounds and learning styles of her students. She aims to create an inclusive classroom environment that fosters open dialogue, mutual respect, and intellectual curiosity. Her teaching methodology emphasizes active student engagement through interactive discussions, case studies, and practical exercises, which promote critical thinking and the application of legal principles in real-world contexts.

She has taught a wide range of courses, including:
- Constitutional Law
- Environmental Law
- Family Law
- Sociology
- Taxation Law
- International Trade Law

Through her engaging curriculum design, she ensures a comprehensive understanding of these subjects.
