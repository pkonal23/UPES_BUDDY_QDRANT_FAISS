The content outlines a comprehensive curriculum and program details for a Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech.) in Civil Engineering with a specialization in Construction Management offered by the UPES School of Advanced Engineering. This program is structured to provide students with essential knowledge and skills to effectively manage construction projects while emphasizing environmental sustainability.

### Program Overview:
- **Core Focus Areas**: The curriculum integrates project management techniques, general management practices, and technology-related subjects. Students learn to plan, schedule, and control construction projects within defined scope, quality, time, and cost constraints.
- **Practical Exposure**: The program includes hands-on learning experiences through labs and projects, enhancing practical application of theoretical knowledge in real-world scenarios.

### Course Structure:
- **Semesters**: The program spans eight semesters, covering various subjects including:
  - Environmental Engineering
  - Concrete Technology
  - Structural Engineering
  - Transportation Engineering
  - Building Information Modelling
  - Disaster Management
  - Advanced Geotechnical Engineering
- **Specialization Courses**: Students can choose from several specialization courses, including Energy Efficiency, Forensic Engineering, and more.

### Industry Relevance:
- **Future Scope**: The program prepares students for a rapidly evolving construction industry, which is witnessing a surge in demand for modern infrastructure, including green buildings and advanced construction technologies like 3-D printing.
- **Career Opportunities**: Graduates can pursue roles such as Project Manager, Construction Manager, Site Engineer, Quantity Surveyor, and Planning Engineer across various sectors including real estate, infrastructure, and government agencies.

### Admission Criteria:
- **Eligibility**: Candidates must have a minimum of 50% marks in Classes X and XII, with 50% in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics in Class XII. Admission relies on performance in entrance exams like UPESEAT, JEE, Board Merit, SAT, or CUET.
