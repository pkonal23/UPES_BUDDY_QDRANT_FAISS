Dr. Surbhi Saraswat is an Assistant Professor at the School of Computer Science, having joined UPES in January 2022. She specializes in several cutting-edge fields including Big Data Processing and Analytics, Data Visualization, Artificial Intelligence, Fog/Cloud Computing, the Internet of Things (IoT), and Ubiquitous Computing. 

Dr. Saraswat is dedicated to fostering an interactive learning environment where students engage in discussions, collaborate, and tackle real-world challenges. She employs a range of formative and summative assessment methods aimed at providing timely and meaningful feedback, ensuring that assessments are authentic and aligned with learning objectives. This approach allows students to showcase their understanding and skills in varied ways. Furthermore, Dr. Saraswat is committed to her professional development, consistently seeking to enhance her teaching strategies and methodologies.

Her academic credentials include a PhD in Computer Science & Engineering from IIT (BHU) Varanasi, India, completed in 2020, an M.Tech. in Information Technology from IIIT Allahabad, India, and a B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering from Jaipur National University, Jaipur. Dr. Saraswat has over five years of experience in Big Data, during which she has delivered seminars and conducted workshops. Prior to her current role, she was a Post-Doctoral Researcher at University College Cork, Ireland, from June 2020 to November 2021, where she contributed to the European Union's BRAINE Project, focusing on energy-efficient hardware and AI-empowered software systems for processing Big Data at the Edge.

In terms of scholarly contributions, Dr. Saraswat has a granted patent and has published extensively, with four journals in IEEE Transactions and twelve conference papers indexed in Scopus. She teaches a variety of courses beyond Big Data, including Introduction to IoT, Microprocessors, Computer Architectures, Digital Logic, and Computer Organization. Her teaching emphasizes hands-on learning and project-based activities, particularly in Big Data courses covering ingestion, search, analytics, and stream data processing, to ensure students can apply their knowledge in practical contexts.
