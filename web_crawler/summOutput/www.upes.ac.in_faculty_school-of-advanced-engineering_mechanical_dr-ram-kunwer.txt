Dr. Ram Kunwer is an accomplished academic in the field of mechanical engineering, specializing in various subjects including Engineering Thermodynamics, Heat Transfer, Engineering Mechanics, Automotive Thermal Management, Aircraft Materials and Manufacturing Processes, Applied Machine Learning, and Solar Thermal Systems. He joined the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) in 2016, after serving as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Lovely Professional University from 2012 to 2016.

Dr. Kunwer holds a Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) in Mechanical Engineering from Krishna Institute of Engineering and Technology (KIET), completed in 2010. He further advanced his education with a Master of Technology (M.Tech) degree in Industrial Tribology from the Indian Institute of Technology, Delhi (IITD) in 2012. He achieved his Ph.D. in 2022, focusing on Packed Bed Thermal Energy Storage Systems for Concentrated Solar Power Applications at UPES.

His research interests encompass Thermal Energy Storage, Solar Thermal Systems, Refrigeration, Heat Exchangers, and Multi-Physics modeling of solar thermal systems. Dr. Kunwer has made significant contributions to the academic community, having published approximately 19 articles in reputable international journals. He also holds three design patents and one utility patent, and has authored a book on thermal energy storage systems tailored for concentrated solar power applications. Additionally, he has developed a laboratory-scale test facility aimed at assessing the performance of sensible-based thermal energy storage systems.

Dr. Kunwer's teaching philosophy centers on creating a stimulating learning environment that promotes critical thinking and curiosity among students. He emphasizes the importance of establishing a strong foundational knowledge, which he believes is essential for both academic and personal growth. His teaching approach focuses on fundamental concepts while drawing connections to practical, real-life applications.
