The M. Tech. in Petroleum Engineering program offered by the UPES School of Advanced Engineering is a specialized 2-year course designed to address the talent shortage in the oil and gas sector, particularly in light of the "Great Crew Change" as many experienced professionals retire. This program aims to equip students with essential knowledge and skills to meet industry challenges effectively and safely. 

### Program Structure
- **Credits**: 80 credits over two years.
- **Curriculum**: The first year focuses on core subjects such as petroleum geology, drilling, formation evaluation, reservoir engineering, and HSE (Health, Safety, and Environment) issues. The second year emphasizes industry-focused internships and dissertations, blending academic knowledge with practical experience.
- **Programming Training**: The program includes training in programming and exposure to advanced digital technologies, preparing students for the evolving petroleum industry landscape.

### Selection Criteria
- **Eligibility**: Candidates must have a B.Tech/B.E. in fields such as Petroleum, Geo-Science, Gas Engineering, or equivalent, with a minimum of 60% marks at the Higher Secondary level (10th and 12th). GATE or NET qualification is preferred.
- **Admission Process**: Selection is based on individual performance during a personal interview.

### Career Opportunities
Upon graduation, students can pursue various roles in the petroleum industry, including:
- **Exploration and Production (E&P)**: Opportunities with companies like Shell, Reliance, and Adani.
- **Service Companies**: Positions at Schlumberger, Weatherford, Halliburton, and Baker Hughes.
- **Consultancy Services**: Roles with firms such as KPMG, McKinsey & Company, and Deloitte.
- **Data Processing**: Opportunities with companies like Petrolink and Infosys.
- **Academic and Research Positions**: Potential roles in universities and IITs.

### Industry Trends
The program addresses the ongoing need for skilled professionals in the oil and gas sector, emphasizing the importance of soft skills alongside technical knowledge. The industry is evolving with advancements in IoT and AI, necessitating a workforce trained in these technologies. Additionally, there is a growing focus on minimizing carbon emissions and exploring biofuels.
