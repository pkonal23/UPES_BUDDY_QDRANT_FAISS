The University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) offers a Master of Science (M.Sc.) program in Medical & Pharmaceutical Microbiology through its School of Medical Sciences & Technology. This program is designed to equip students with a comprehensive understanding of the relationship between microorganisms and human health, as well as the pharmaceutical advancements related to infectious diseases.

### Program Overview
- **Curriculum**: The program includes a diverse range of subjects such as medical bacteriology, virology, mycology, immunology, pharmaceutical microbiology, molecular biology, and more. Students engage in hands-on laboratory work, research projects, and practical training to enhance their analytical and problem-solving skills.
- **Facilities**: Students have access to state-of-the-art facilities, including a BSL-1 Aseptic Room and advanced microbiology equipment like BOD incubators, CO2 incubators, freezers, and centrifuges.

### Career Opportunities
Graduates can pursue various roles, including:
- Microbiologists and Research Scientists in pharmaceutical companies and research institutions
- Laboratory Managers in biotechnology firms
- Positions in vaccine development, quality control, clinical research, and public health
- Regulatory affairs and contract research organizations (CROs)

The demand for experts in microbial testing and quality control is increasing, particularly for roles that ensure the safety and efficacy of drugs and medical products.

### Admission Criteria
To be eligible for the M.Sc. program, applicants must have completed their X and XII with a minimum of 50% and possess a relevant undergraduate degree (B.Sc. in Biology/Biotechnology/B.Sc. Pharmacy/B.Sc. Microbiology, etc.) with at least 50% marks. 

### Placement Success
The program has a dedicated placement cell that has successfully secured lucrative job opportunities for graduates in leading pharmaceutical companies, research institutions, and healthcare organizations. The university's strong industry connections play a crucial role in these placements.
