Rodolphe Gelin, born in 1965, is an AI expert currently associated with Renault in Paris. He began his professional journey at the French Atomic Energy Commission (CEA), where he focused on robot control systems for industrial uses as well as rehabilitation robotics. Throughout his career, Gelin has managed various teams specializing in robotics, virtual reality, and cognitics. 

In 2009, he transitioned to SoftBank Robotics, where he took the lead on the ROMEO project, aimed at creating a human-sized humanoid robot designed to assist the elderly. Since 2019, Gelin has been applying his expertise in deep learning to the development of autonomous and connected vehicles at Renault, where he is responsible for initiatives related to trustworthy AI.
