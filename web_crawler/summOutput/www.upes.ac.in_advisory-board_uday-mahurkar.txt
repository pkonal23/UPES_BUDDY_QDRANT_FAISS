Uday Mahurkar serves as the Information Commissioner for the Government of India and has a distinguished background as a former Senior Deputy Editor at India Today magazine, where he dedicated 33 years of his career. His extensive experience encompasses coverage of politics, social issues, religious conflicts, and both rural and urban development, resulting in a substantial portfolio of articles on these topics.

Mahurkar is particularly recognized for his expertise on radical Islamic movements and their societal impacts, contributing numerous articles on this theme through the India Today platform. Additionally, he has conducted in-depth research on revolutionary figure Veer Savarkar, portraying him as a defender of India’s national security, and has published a variety of articles reflecting this perspective.

His journalistic excellence has earned him several accolades, including the prestigious Narad Samman in 2018, awarded by Indrapastha Samvad Kendra. Mahurkar is also noted for his insights into the governance model of Prime Minister Narendra Modi. He is the author of two significant works: "Marching with a Billion," published by Penguin in 2017, and "Centrestage," published by Random House in 2014. These books encapsulate Modi’s vision for administration and have received praise from notable figures such as Klaus Schwab, founder of the World Economic Forum; Sri Sri Ravi Shankar; former French President Francois Hollande; Bangladesh Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina Wajed; and Infosys founder NR Narayana Murthy.
