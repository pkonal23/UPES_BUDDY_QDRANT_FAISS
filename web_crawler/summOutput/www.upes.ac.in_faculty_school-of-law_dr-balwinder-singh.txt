Dr. Balwinder Singh is an accomplished academic with over 11 years of teaching and research experience, currently serving as an Associate Professor at the School of Law, UPES. He specializes in various legal fields, including Family Laws, Constitutional Law, Public International Law, and Legal Methods. His teaching philosophy emphasizes creating a positive learning environment, critical reflection, and professional growth. He believes in the importance of practical applicability in legal education.

Dr. Singh has a robust publication record, contributing significantly to various legal journals. Some of his notable works include:

1. **"Hunger Amid Plenty: Realizing Human Right to Food in India"** - Kurukshetra Law Journal, Vol. IV, 2014, pp. 303-313, ISSN No. 2231-5519.
2. **"Rights of LGBTQ Community: Legislative and Judicial Trends in the United States, United Kingdom & India"** - Journal of Education Rabindra Bharti University, Vol. XXIII, Issue No 3, 2021, pp.518-526, ISSN No. 0972-7175.
3. **"Regulation of Refugee Influx in India: Need for National Legal Regime"** - NIU International Journal of Human Rights, Vol 8, Issue III, 2021, pp.359-368, ISSN No-2394-0298.
4. **"Child’s Right to Food: Addressing Malnutrition through Law and Policy in India"** - Design Engineering, Issue 9, 2021, pp. 6600-6612, ISSN No. 0011-9342.
5. **"Law Relating to Settlement of Spousal Property in India: A Need for Reforms"** - RGNUL Law Review, Vol. IV, 2014, pp. 73-87, ISSN No. 2231-4938.
6. **"Development of Forest & Biodiversity Conservation Laws in India: Analysis from Vedic Period to Present Scenario"** - Vol. LXV, Issue-2, No. 12, 2022.
7. **"Viability of the Use of Artificial Intelligence in the Legal Domain: A Critical Analysis"** - Neuro Quantology, June 2022, Volume 20, Issue 6.
8. **"Wife’s Right to Employment vis-a-vis Husband’s Conjugal Rights under Hindu Law: A Critique"** - NUALS Law Journal, Vol. 8, 2014, pp. 87-100, ISSN No.2319-8273.
9. **"Food Security and Public Distribution System: Response and Policy Initiative against Hunger in India"** - Chanakya National Law University Journal, Vol.7, 2017-18, pp. 80-95, ISSN No. 0976-805X.
10. **"Law relating to maintenance with special reference to Aged parents: An Analysis"** - MDU Law Journal, Vol. XIX, July 2017, pp.89-100, ISSN No. 2230-746X.

Dr. Singh has also authored books on critical topics such as the intersection of science, technology, and human rights, and the evolving dimensions of family law in India. He has received accolades for his contributions, including the Surana and Surana International Essay Writing Competition award in 2013 and the Best Paper Award at a national conference on the Right to Food in 2017. His commitment to environmental issues has been recognized with a certificate of appreciation from the National Green Tribunal for his contributions to an international conference on the environment.

Dr. Singh has previously worked at NorthCap University and Lovely Professional University, and he has experience representing cases in civil and criminal matters in Jammu and Kashmir. His research interests focus on contemporary challenges in personal laws, food rights, AI in legal education, and biodiversity conservation.
