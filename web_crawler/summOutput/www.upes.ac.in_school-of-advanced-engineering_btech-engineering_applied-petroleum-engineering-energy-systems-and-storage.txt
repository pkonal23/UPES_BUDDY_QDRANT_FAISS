The UPES School of Advanced Engineering offers a B.Tech program in Applied Petroleum Engineering with a specialization in Energy Systems and Storage. This cutting-edge curriculum is designed to equip students with essential skills and knowledge for thriving in the rapidly evolving energy sector, particularly in petroleum engineering applications. The program covers critical areas such as exploration, production, processing, and storage of petroleum resources while integrating the latest advancements in energy systems and storage technologies.

**Program Highlights:**
- Comprehensive understanding of energy industry dynamics and environmentally friendly practices.
- Emphasis on practical skills through hands-on experience in labs and industrial internships.
- Strong industry connections that facilitate excellent placement opportunities in top-tier organizations within the energy sector.
- Focus on sustainable energy practices, including renewable energy technologies, energy policies, and hydrogen energy.

**Curriculum Overview:**
The curriculum spans eight semesters, including courses such as:
- Advanced Drilling Engineering
- Reservoir Modelling
- Natural Gas Engineering
- Oil and Gas Well Testing
- Enhanced Oil Recovery
- Data Analytics and AI in Petroleum Engineering

Students will also engage in major projects and internships to apply their theoretical knowledge in real-world scenarios. The program prepares graduates for diverse roles, including Reservoir Engineers, Drilling Engineers, Production Engineers, Process Engineers, and Pipeline Engineers.

**Future Scope and Industry Trends:**
The program addresses the future demands of the energy sector, including the development of Carbon Capture, Utilisation, and Storage (CCUS) technologies, which are essential for reducing greenhouse gas emissions. Graduates will be expected to adapt to the evolving landscape of international energy markets and geopolitical influences, ensuring they are well-prepared to tackle the challenges of the energy transition and contribute to sustainable practices.

**Eligibility Criteria:**
To qualify for admission, candidates must have:
- Minimum 50% marks in Classes X and XII, with at least 50% in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics in Class XII.
- Performance in entrance exams such as UPESEAT, JEE, Board Merit, SAT, or CUET.
