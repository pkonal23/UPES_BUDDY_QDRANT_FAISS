The program at UPES School of Law is meticulously designed to provide students with a critical understanding of banking and insolvency laws, emphasizing both theoretical concepts and practical applications. This comprehensive curriculum prepares students for real-world challenges in the banking and financial sectors.

### Academics
The program covers essential topics such as:
- **Banking Laws and Practices**
- **Negotiable Instruments Laws** (including cheque bouncing)
- **Financial Market Regulation**
- **Regulatory Compliance**
- **Anti-Money Laundering Regulations**
- **Consumer Protection Laws**
- **Liability under Tort**
- **Debt Recovery Laws**
- **Insolvency and Bankruptcy Laws**
- **Insurance Laws**
- **International Banking and Financial Regulation**

The faculty comprises experienced professionals with extensive knowledge in these areas, ensuring students receive high-quality education and mentorship.

### Industry Trends and Future Scope
The program aligns with current industry trends, focusing on:
- Historical, political, and legal aspects of business and financial markets.
- Expertise in debt recovery, insurance laws, compliance, and customer relations.
- The impact of fintech on banking, including digital banking, data protection, and cybersecurity.
- Strategies for resolving non-performing and stressed assets, emphasizing debt restructuring and asset reconstruction.
- The evolving regulatory framework post-Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code.
- Understanding international banking laws and compliance for cross-border transactions.
- Collaboration and strategic alliances in non-banking financial companies to enhance operational efficiency.

Graduates are well-positioned for careers in various sectors, including:
- **Banking and Financial Institutions**
- **Corporate Law Firms**
- **Regulatory Bodies**
- **Government Agencies**

They can pursue roles as banking lawyers, legal consultants, compliance officers, or in-house counsels, with opportunities in insolvency and restructuring practices.

### Admission and Eligibility
To apply for the program, candidates must meet the following eligibility criteria:
- A minimum of 50% marks at Higher Secondary (XII) level.
- Admission is based on performance in ULSAT, CLAT, Board Marks, or CUET.

- **Practical Learning**: Students engage in case studies, moot courts, internships, and industry interactions to enhance their practical understanding.
- **Comprehensive Knowledge**: The program equips students with skills in legal research, analytical thinking, problem-solving, and effective communication.
