Dr. Souradeep Roy is an accomplished Nanotechnologist with significant expertise in biomolecular sensor development for healthcare and environmental applications. He currently serves as an Assistant Professor in the School of Health Sciences and Technology at UPES, Dehradun, India. Dr. Roy holds a B.Tech degree in Electronics and Instrumentation Engineering (2015) from the Institute of Technical Education and Research (ITER), Bhubaneswar, an M.Tech in Nanotechnology (2017) from Amity Institute of Nanotechnology, Amity University Uttar Pradesh (AUUP), and a PhD (2021) from AUUP, supported by a DST-UKIERI project. His doctoral research included a stint as a visiting researcher at the Nanotechnology and Integrated Bio-Engineering Centre (NIBEC) in Northern Ireland, UK.

Dr. Roy has been recognized for his research excellence, having received the CSIR Senior Research Fellowship (SRF Direct) in November 2020, and has recently secured a SEED grant worth Rs. 7.6 Lakhs from UPES as a Co-PI. His research output includes over 30 publications in high-quality journals, 4 book chapters, and 4 national patents, with a citation count exceeding 380 and an h-index of 12 and i-10 index of 16 as of December 2023. He has also presented his work at numerous national and international conferences, winning awards for best oral presentations in categories related to healthcare, AI, materials, and nanoscience.

Dr. Roy's research interests encompass a wide range of topics including nano-electrochemistry, biomolecular sensors, microfluidics, lab-on-chip technologies, connected health, biomechanics, research methodology, biomedical transport, nanoscience and technology, biosensors, and instrumentation techniques. Notably, during his postdoctoral tenure at the Centre for Interdisciplinary Research and Innovation (CIDRI) at UPES, he developed a groundbreaking sensor prototype for intraoperative leveling in patients with Hirschsprung’s Disease in collaboration with AIIMS Bhubaneswar, and worked on piezoelectric sensors for detecting Hirschsprung’s Disease and COVID-19 during his time as a visiting researcher in Thailand.

In terms of teaching philosophy, Dr. Roy emphasizes the importance of a solid understanding of fundamentals, gradually progressing to advanced topics. He employs real-world examples to illustrate complex concepts, fostering an engaging learning environment that encourages student interaction and participation. Dr. Roy advocates for a multidisciplinary approach, highlighting the relevance of cross-discipline learning in contemporary and future contexts.
