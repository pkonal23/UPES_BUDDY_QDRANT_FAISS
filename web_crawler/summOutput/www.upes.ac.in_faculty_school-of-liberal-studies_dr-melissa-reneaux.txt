**Personal Information**

Dr. Melissa Reneaux is an Assistant Professor at the School of Liberal Studies at University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES). Prior to joining UPES, Dr. Melissa Reneaux held postdoctoral research positions at the Clopath Lab at Imperial College London and the Pinotsis Lab at City-University of London.

**Academic Background**

Dr. Melissa Reneaux earned her undergraduate and postgraduate degrees in physics from St. Stephen's College, University of Delhi. Dr. Reneaux completed her doctoral research in computational neuroscience at Jawaharlal Nehru University, Delhi, under the supervision of Professor Karmeshu and Dr. A. Krishnamachari.

**Research Interests**

Dr. Melissa Reneaux's research focuses on neuropsychiatric disorders, particularly depression, working memory, suicidal ideation, and gender violence. Dr. Reneaux employs mathematical models to investigate the mechanistic underpinnings of these conditions.

**Research Collaborations**

Dr. Melissa Reneaux has collaborated with prominent neuroscientists, including Professor Karl Friston. Dr. Melissa Reneaux has received funding from the Council of Scientific & Industrial Research and UK Research and Innovation to support her research endeavors.

**Teaching Philosophy**

Dr. Melissa Reneaux employs problem-solving-based teaching, connecting classroom concepts to real-life scenarios. Dr. Melissa Reneaux simplifies complex topics and utilizes innovative assessment methods to promote critical thinking and problem-solving skills. Additionally, Dr. Reneaux integrates her research expertise into her teaching, ensuring students have access to the latest knowledge in their field.

**Courses Taught**

Dr. Melissa Reneaux has taught undergraduate courses in Statistical Methods in Psychology, Brain and Behaviour, and Developmental Cognitive Neuroscience. Dr. Reneaux has also supervised postgraduate students' dissertations.

**Awards and Achievements**

Throughout her academic career, Dr. Melissa Reneaux has received numerous awards and honors, including the Meera Memorial Scholarship, Mukarji Memorial Prize, Meritorious Student Award, Virendra Kumar Memorial Scholarship, and Meera Memorial Award. Dr. Reneaux was also awarded a research fellowship from the Council of Scientific and Industrial Research, Government of India, for her doctoral research.

**Scholarly Activities**

Dr. Melissa Reneaux's involvement in scholarly activities began during her undergraduate years with internships at the Harish Chandra Institute and IIT Madras. Her subsequent research endeavors have led to publications in reputable journals such as Journal of Theoretical Biology, Cell Reports, and Journal of Neuroinflammation. Dr. Melissa Reneaux has actively disseminated her research findings through public lectures on mental health and anxiety.
