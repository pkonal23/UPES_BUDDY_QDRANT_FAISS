**Research Interests and Projects:**
Dr. Anil Kumar is leading a significant project titled "Development of an Integrated Intelligent Disaster Management System for Mountain Communities in India," recognized as a project of "National Importance" and funded by the Department of Science and Technology (DST), Government of India. This project aims to create an intelligent system that integrates a comprehensive knowledge database on disasters, traditional mountain community knowledge, real-time monitoring sensors, spatial information systems, and auxiliary data sources. The focus is on multi-hazard early warning and management in hilly regions of India.

Additionally, he has been involved in a project related to the "Semantic segmentation of PolSAR image data using advanced deep learning models," which is part of the NISAR (NASA-ISRO Synthetic Aperture Radar) mission. His research also includes the automatic identification of lunar craters and ejecta using deep learning models, and in healthcare, he is applying machine learning algorithms to assess breast cancer proliferation rates through the analysis of whole slide images.

**Professional Recognition:**
Dr. Anil Kumar has received the prestigious Governor Research Award from the Governor of Uttarakhand, India. He was selected as the Lead Speaker for the NISAR Science Workshop in 2015, conducted at the Space Applications Centre (SAC), ISRO in Ahmedabad. He is also a member of the Executive Committee of the WInSAR (Western North America Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar Consortium) as an Adjunct II part of UNAVCO.

**Academic Background and Experience:**
Dr. Anil Kumar serves as an Associate Professor at the School of Computer Science and has previously worked at DIT University and Cognizant Technology Solutions. He is also a guest faculty member at the Indian Institute of Remote Sensing, ISRO, in Dehradun, India. He completed his master's thesis at IIT Kharagpur.
