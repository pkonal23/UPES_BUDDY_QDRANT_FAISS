The University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) offers a specialized LL.M. program in Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) through its School of Law, which spans two semesters and consists of 25 total credits. The program is designed to equip students with a comprehensive understanding of legal aspects related to intellectual property, including key concepts such as copyrights, trademarks, patents, and trade secrets. 

**Program Structure:**
- **Electives:** Students can choose four electives, each worth 2 credits. Options include "Future Scope / Industry Trends," "Intellectual Property Rights," "Law of Industrial Designs and ICL," "Law of Copyrights and Related Rights," "Law of Patents," "Law of Trademark and Practice," and more.
- **Curriculum:** The curriculum covers essential topics like Biotechnology, Industrial Designs, Trade Secrets, and Geographical Indications, focusing on both theoretical knowledge and practical skills necessary for legal practice in IPR.
- **Practical Training:** The program emphasizes practical exercises, case studies, and the development of critical thinking and legal analysis skills, preparing students for real-world challenges in the field.

**Career Opportunities:**
Graduates of the program have access to a wide range of career paths, including roles as IP attorneys, patent agents, trademark analysts, IP consultants, and legal advisors in law firms, corporations, research organizations, and government bodies. The program's strong industry connections facilitate internships and job placements, enhancing career prospects. Additionally, graduates may pursue academic careers in teaching or research.

**Industry Relevance:**
The demand for skilled professionals in IPR is on the rise due to technological advancements, globalization, and increased awareness of intellectual property protection. Emerging technologies such as artificial intelligence and blockchain are creating new complexities in the field, further driving the need for legal expertise.

**Placement Opportunities:**
UPES provides excellent placement opportunities for students, leveraging its corporate tie-ups with renowned IPR law firms. The dedicated IPR center at UPES fosters networking opportunities with industry experts, enhancing students' professional connections.

**Eligibility Criteria:**
To enroll in the LL.M. program, candidates must have a minimum of 50% marks at the Higher and Senior Secondary levels (X and XII) and in their Graduation with an LLB degree or equivalent. Selection is based on performance in ULSAT-LLM / CLAT-PG / CUET.
