The Ph.D. program in Biomedical Engineering at the UPES School of Health Science & Technology is set to commence with a July 2024 intake, offering both full-time and part-time study options. The program is designed to provide students with advanced knowledge and research skills in the rapidly evolving field of biomedical engineering, with a duration ranging from 3 to 6 years.

### Admission Requirements:
Candidates must hold a B.Tech./B.E. or M.Tech. in engineering disciplines, or an M.Sc. in relevant subjects, achieving a minimum of 60% marks or a 6.00 CGPA on a 10-point scale. Additionally, qualifications in GATE/CSIR/UGC NET/ICAR/ICMR/DST-INSPIRE fellowship are required. This ensures that students are adequately prepared for the program's academic rigor.

### Program Objectives:
The Ph.D. program aims to cultivate scholars who will significantly contribute to biomedical research and technology. Key educational objectives include:
- Developing a deep understanding of contemporary theories and methodologies in biomedical engineering.
- Fostering ethical and responsible research practices.
- Enhancing skills in interdisciplinary collaboration, critical thinking, and problem-solving.
- Preparing graduates for effective communication of research findings.

### Research Focus Areas:
The program emphasizes cutting-edge research in areas such as:
- Biomaterials
- Microfluidics
- Lab-on-Chip technology
- Biosensors
- Targeted Drug Delivery
- Soft Robotics

### Career Opportunities:
Graduates of the program can pursue a variety of career paths, including:
- Academia (professors or researchers)
- Biomedical Research Scientists in pharmaceuticals and biotechnology
- Clinical Engineers in healthcare settings
- Regulatory Affairs Specialists
- Opportunities in entrepreneurship, biomechanics, data science, healthcare IT, and more.

### Program Highlights:
- Access to advanced laboratories and research equipment in the Central Instrumentation Center.
- Strong industry connections and mentorship from a highly qualified faculty actively involved in research.
- Opportunities for collaboration with global researchers through seminars, workshops, and conferences.
