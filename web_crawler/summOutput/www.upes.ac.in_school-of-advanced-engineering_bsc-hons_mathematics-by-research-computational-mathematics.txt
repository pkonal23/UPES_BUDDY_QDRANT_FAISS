**University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) School of Advanced Engineering's Bachelor of Science (Hons.) Mathematics in Computational Mathematics Program**

**Program Overview**

The Bachelor of Science (Hons.) Mathematics in Computational Mathematics program, offered by University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) School of Advanced Engineering, provides students with an in-depth understanding of the intersection between mathematics and computational methods. Designed to empower individuals with the skills and knowledge to tackle complex challenges in diverse fields, this program emphasizes the application of mathematical and computational methodologies.

**Course Curriculum**

Through theoretical and practical components, the program's curriculum focuses on traditional mathematical disciplines such as calculus, linear algebra, differential equations, and numerical methods. Additionally, students delve into the world of mathematical software and programming languages, enabling them to create mathematical models, analyze data, and derive meaningful insights.

**Research Focus**

A distinctive feature of this program is its emphasis on research. Students engage in advanced topics in computational mathematics, conduct independent research projects, and collaborate with University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) School of Advanced Engineering faculty members on cutting-edge research initiatives. This hands-on experience fosters critical thinking, problem-solving abilities, and the application of mathematical prowess to real-world scenarios.

**Program Highlights**

* Cultivates practical problem-solving skills through computation-based methods, preparing students to tackle real-world challenges.
* Provides a strong foundation in scientific methodology and essential computational skills, aligning with industry demands and enhancing employability.
* Offers comprehensive placement assistance, ensuring a seamless transition from academia to the professional world.
* Supports students in preparing for competitive exams like JAM, empowering them to pursue advanced studies and research in mathematics and related fields.
* Engages students in research projects that bridge mathematics and computation, fostering innovation and critical thinking for solving complex mathematical problems.
* Integrates industry-relevant content, exposing students to practical applications of computational mathematics and enhancing their adaptability to evolving technological landscapes.
