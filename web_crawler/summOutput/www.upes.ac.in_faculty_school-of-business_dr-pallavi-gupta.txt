Dr. Pallavi Gupta is an accomplished academic with a strong focus on labor economics, gender studies, and time use research, particularly within the South Asian context. She holds a Ph.D. in Economics from IIT Roorkee and currently serves as an Assistant Professor in the Economics and Finance Cluster at the School of Business, UPES Dehradun.

Her research contributions, which have been published in peer-reviewed journals, tackle significant issues such as time poverty, unpaid work, and labor force participation. Dr. Gupta is an active member of the Indian Society of Labour Economics and has presented her findings at various national and international conferences, promoting discussions on economic inequalities and gender disparities.

Prior to her tenure at UPES, Dr. Gupta was engaged in extensive research projects at IIT Roorkee and collaborated with key stakeholders in economic policy analysis. Her interdisciplinary work has focused on addressing social inequalities and labor force dynamics.

In her teaching role, Dr. Pallavi emphasizes creating an engaging and inclusive learning environment. She encourages students to think critically and apply theoretical concepts to real-world scenarios through data-driven discussions, case studies, and collaborative assignments. Her teaching philosophy aims to empower students to become independent thinkers capable of tackling complex economic challenges with creativity and confidence.

Dr. Gupta has been recognized for her academic excellence with the prestigious UGC Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) from the University Grants Commission, which facilitated her advanced research in economics. She teaches courses in Microeconomics, International Economics, and various Business courses, focusing on analytical techniques and data interpretation to prepare students for both academic and professional success.
