Amit Gurung is an Assistant Professor - Selection Grade at the School of Computer Science, with a robust academic background and extensive experience in teaching and research. He earned his Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from the National Institute of Technology Meghalaya, India, in 2018. With over 15 years in the field, he has published numerous research papers in prestigious SCI/Scopus-indexed journals and conferences, and holds a filed patent in Kyoto, Japan.

His teaching portfolio includes a variety of undergraduate and postgraduate courses such as Computer Networks, Network Security, Data Structures and Algorithms, Software Engineering, and Object-Oriented Programming Languages. His research interests are diverse, focusing on formal methods, particularly in reachability analysis and safety verification of Hybrid Systems, as well as High-Performance Computing. He is also engaged in exploring digital image processing and machine learning.

Currently, Amit Gurung is delving into research that involves learning models of Cyber-Physical Systems from simulation data, with applications for property verification. He is exploring innovative surveillance systems utilizing drone technology.

Before his tenure at UPES, he was a Program-specific Researcher (Postdoctoral) for the JST CREST project titled "CyPhAI: Formal Analysis and Design of AI-intensive Cyber-Physical Systems," funded by the Japan Science and Technology Agency at Kyoto University, Japan. His career includes roles at the National Institute of Informatics in Tokyo, Japan, and the Formal Methods Laboratory at the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur, India. He has also taught at various academic institutions, demonstrating a commitment to education.

Amit Gurung is dedicated to making learning engaging by connecting theoretical concepts to real-world applications and incorporating advanced software tools into his teaching methodology. His contact email is amit.gurung@ddn.upes.ac.in.
