Dr. Suchetana Sadhukhan is an accomplished academic currently associated with the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES). Prior to her tenure at UPES, she served as an Assistant Professor at VIT Bhopal University from 2021 to 2024, where she taught a range of courses in Physics and Computational Science. Her teaching portfolio included subjects such as Computational Physics, Game Physics, and foundational courses like Engineering Physics, as well as specialized courses like Energy and Environmental Physics. She was also instrumental in developing an interdisciplinary course on Econophysics, which applies principles of Statistical Physics to financial systems.

In her role at VIT Bhopal, Dr. Sadhukhan was not only engaged in teaching but also took on leadership responsibilities as the School Research Coordinator, overseeing research initiatives for both students and faculty. Additionally, she was the Faculty Coordinator for the Music Club, promoting student involvement in extracurricular activities.

Her research during this time focused on Random Matrix Theory and Complex Systems, and she was recognized as a National Research candidate for two consecutive years due to her contributions in these fields.

Dr. Sadhukhan's research interests are diverse and include Random Matrix Theory, Econophysics, and studies related to Air Pollution. She employs Statistical Physics and Time Series Analysis to investigate complex systems, uncovering patterns and behaviors within these intricate domains. Her scholarly work has resulted in numerous publications in high-ranking international journals, including contributions to SCI and SCOPUS indexed journals, book chapters, and conference papers. She has also been granted three patents, highlighting her innovative contributions to technology. Additionally, she serves as a reviewer for esteemed academic journals.

Dr. Sadhukhan holds a B.Sc. in Physics from Vidyasagar College, University of Calcutta, an M.Sc. in Physics from the National Institute of Technology Rourkela, and a Ph.D. in Physics from the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur, where her research concentrated on the statistical analysis of complex systems through Random Matrix Theory.

Her teaching philosophy emphasizes an interactive and interdisciplinary learning environment. Dr. Sadhukhan is committed to fostering curiosity, encouraging collaborative learning, and developing problem-solving skills among students, which she believes are essential for their academic and professional success.
