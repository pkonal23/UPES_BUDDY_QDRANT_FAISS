Dr. Himanshi Jangir is a distinguished bioengineer and nature-inspired bio-designer with a robust academic and research background. Her expertise spans across various innovative fields, including multi-organ human-on-a-chip platforms, micro-physiological systems, 3D organ printing, bio-hybrid devices, green electrode development for bioenergy applications, and nano agriculture.

### Academic Background:
- **Undergraduate Studies**: Dr. Jangir completed her BTech in Biological Sciences and Bioengineering at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kanpur from 2013 to 2017, focusing her research on carbon and cerium-based bio-electrodes. She was awarded the Academic Excellence Award for her achievements.
- **Masterâ€™s Research**: She continued her studies at IIT Kanpur, completing her MTech in 2018 under an ISRO-funded project that investigated bioinspired lithium-sulfide cathode materials for next-generation lithium-ion batteries.
- **Doctoral Research**: Dr. Jangir pursued her PhD in the Department of Design at IIT Kanpur (2018-2020), concentrating on nano agriculture and green electrode materials. Her doctoral thesis received the Outstanding Doctoral Thesis Award during the 53rd Convocation of IIT Kanpur.

### Professional Experience:
- In 2020, Dr. Jangir joined the University of Central Florida as a postdoctoral fellow under the Preeminent Postdoctoral Program (P3) Fellowship. Her work included developing human-on-a-chip systems, modeling sarcopenia, and neuromuscular modeling related to ALS mutations.
- As of April 2024, she has taken on the role of Assistant Professor at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES).

### Research Contributions:
Dr. Jangir has contributed significantly to the field through various peer-reviewed journal publications and conference papers. Notable research includes:
- Development of a multi-organ human-on-a-chip system to model chronic opioid overdose effects.
- Exploration of nano pyrite as a sustainable agricultural solution to enhance crop yields in nutrient-deficient soils.
- Innovations in bio-batteries and bioelectrodes for medical applications.

### Awards and Recognition:

- 'Preeminent Postdoctoral Program (P3) Fellowship', University of Central Florida.

### Teaching and Scholarly Activities:
Dr. Jangir is involved in teaching sustainable bio-design, emphasizing hands-on learning through experimental work. She has served as a teaching assistant for the National Program on Technology Enhanced Learning (NPTEL) in India from 2018 to 2024 and has developed courses focusing on biological designs and sustainable manufacturing practices.
