Dr. Rakesh Kumar Mishra is an Assistant Professor at the School of Health Sciences and Technology, specializing in pharmacology and drug delivery systems. His research interests include the assessment of drugs for idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis, the development of herbal formulations, and the exploration of various drug delivery methods and their physicochemical properties. Dr. Mishra has a robust background in conducting efficacy and safety studies, particularly focusing on conditions such as rheumatoid arthritis and inflammatory diseases. He has experience in animal studies, toxicity assessments, and has developed a DSS-induced colitis mice model.

Dr. Mishra completed his Ph.D. from the Institute of Nano Science and Technology, Mohali, and the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research, Mohali. He has seven years of interdisciplinary research training that encompasses chemical biology, cell culture techniques, animal handling, histopathology, immunohistochemistry, nanotechnology, and biophysical characterization of nanoparticles. His postdoctoral research was conducted at St. John’s University in New York, where he focused on novel drug delivery systems, lipid nanoparticles, and drug repurposing.

In terms of teaching philosophy, Dr. Mishra emphasizes active student involvement and dynamic learning environments. He adapts his teaching methods to meet the diverse needs of students, ensuring regular assessments to monitor individual progress and foster a collaborative academic journey.

Dr. Mishra has received notable recognitions, including a doctoral fellowship from the Department of Science and Technology, Government of India, and a postdoctoral fellowship from St. John’s University. Before joining UPES, he served as an associate professor at Dattakala College of Pharmacy in Pune.
