Shatavisha Mustafi is an Assistant Professor at the School of Liberal Studies and Media at UPES, with a robust academic background that includes an undergraduate degree in Comparative Literature, a Master's, and an M.Phil in Art History. Her scholarly contributions have been published in notable journals such as the Bengal Art Journal, Sahapedia, and Eyeline. Recognized by the Indian Council of Social Science Research under the Ministry of Education, her doctoral research focuses on the intersection of new media, materiality, and their consequences in the art world.

Shatavisha was a Fulbright-Nehru Doctoral Fellow (2019-2020) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), where her research examined the art collection of Chester and Davida Herwitz, investigating the influence of foreign patronage on the promotion of Indian art internationally. Her work critically analyzes the economic structures that shape the valuation and circulation of art.

Throughout her career, she has received several accolades, including the Young Curators Curatorial Award from the Kochi Biennale Foundation (2016-2017) and the Godrej Cultural Lab National Fellowship (2020). She also obtained a travel grant from the Nehru Trust for the Indian Collection at the Victoria and Albert Museum (2018-2019) and participated in workshops at the International Museum Expo organized by the Ministry of Culture in 2023.

Shatavisha is actively involved in teaching and mentoring, offering training sessions for students applying for grants like the Fulbright, focusing on university selection and research area identification. Her teaching philosophy emphasizes critical thinking and open questioning, often utilizing real-life examples to facilitate understanding of societal dynamics. She conducts a variety of courses, including "A Survey Course on the Iconography and Material Culture of India" and "Construction and Deconstruction: A Seminar Course on Transmedia Adaptation."

In addition to her academic pursuits, Shatavisha is a trained Bharatnatyam dancer and painter, engaging deeply with the intersections of literary studies and the culture industry as a means of exploring diplomacy and soft power.
