Dr. Udai Pratap Singh serves as an Assistant Professor (Senior Scale) at the School of Law, UPES Dehradun. He holds a doctorate in International Law from Dr. Ram Manohar Lohiya National Law University (RMLNLU) in Lucknow, and possesses a Master of Laws (LLM) with dual specializations in Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) and Corporate Law from the Indian Law Institute, Delhi. He completed his undergraduate law degree at RMLNLU as well.

Dr. Singh's academic interests encompass International Law, Human Rights, Humanitarian Law, and Constitutional Law. He has been recognized as a UGC-JRF awardee and has published and presented numerous scholarly papers on topics such as the use of force in public international law, dispute resolution, and alternative dispute resolution (ADR) in the criminal justice system. His research has appeared in various prestigious UGC CARE and peer-reviewed journals. Additionally, he has been invited to serve as a resource person at numerous national and international conferences, including chairing a technical session at the 6th World Congress on Disaster Management in 2023, organized by the Government of Uttarakhand and the National Disaster Management Authority.

With over six years of teaching experience, Dr. Singh previously held positions at IMS Unison University, Dehradun, where he also served as Assistant Dean of the Internal Quality Assurance Cell (IQAC). He has worked at Amity Institute of Advanced Legal Studies (AIALS) at Amity University, Noida, and Sree Guru Gobind Singh Tricentenary (SGT) University in Gurugram.

Dr. Singh has taught a diverse range of subjects to both undergraduate and postgraduate students, including Public International Law, Comparative Public Law, Legal Aid, Insurance Law, Intellectual Property Rights, Securities & Investment Law, Human Rights, and Research Methodology.

His teaching philosophy emphasizes continuous learning and the enhancement of teaching methodologies. Dr. Singh aims to cultivate curiosity and critical thinking in his students through a blend of traditional note-taking, peer discussions, and multimedia resources. He employs the Socratic method to promote open dialogue and encourages questions and answers during his sessions.
