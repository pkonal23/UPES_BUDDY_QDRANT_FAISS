Dr. Suvendu Manna is an accomplished academic and researcher currently serving as an Assistant Professor (Senior Scale) in the School of Advanced Engineering at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES). He holds a PhD from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur and has amassed 9 years of post-PhD experience in research and teaching. His scholarly contributions are extensive, with 46 articles published in peer-reviewed international journals, 15 book chapters authored, 5 books edited, and 5 patents (one granted and four filed). He is actively involved in the academic community as a guest editor for various international journals and as a reviewer for numerous publications.

Dr. Manna leads a research team focusing on waste management and green energy generation. His group specializes in the characterization and utilization of solid and liquid wastes for various applications, including the preparation of composites, construction materials, and nanomaterials. They are also dedicated to developing affordable water filtration systems for rural communities and exploring the Himalayan regions for unique microbes that may yield novel compounds and energy-producing capabilities.

His research interests encompass a wide range of topics, including:
- Solid waste management, particularly in the context of construction materials and nanomaterials.
- Wastewater remediation using nanomaterials and nanocomposites.
- Green energy, with a focus on biofuels and biohydrogen.
- Environmental microbiology, particularly the isolation and screening of medically significant endophytic microbes.

In his teaching, Dr. Manna employs a research-oriented pedagogy, utilizing real-life problems to engage students in developing solutions that address environmental challenges. He promotes collaborative learning, encouraging students to work in groups to enhance their social networking, leadership, and time management skills. Open discussions are a key component of his teaching approach, allowing students to share insights and develop a comprehensive understanding of the material.

Dr. Manna has taught a variety of core courses in civil and engineering disciplines, including Environmental Studies, Immunology, Food Microbiology, Pharmaceutical Microbiology, and Eukaryotic Microbiology. His dedication to education is reflected in his successful supervision of several master's students and his current mentorship of 7 PhD candidates.

His contributions to the field have been recognized with prestigious awards and grants, including the National Post-Doctoral Fellowship Award from SERB-DST, Government of India, in 2016, and the DS Kothari Post-Doctoral Fellowship from UGC, Government of India, in 2015. He has also secured research funding from external agencies such as SERB-DST and Oil India Limited, leading projects with a total funding of approximately 50 lakhs.
