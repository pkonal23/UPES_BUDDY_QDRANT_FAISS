Dr. Vinay Kumar Singh is an Assistant Professor in the English Literature cluster at the School of Liberal Studies and Media. He holds a Masterâ€™s degree in English Literature from Banaras Hindu University, a Post Graduate Certificate in the Teaching of English (PGCTE), and an M.Phil in English (Linguistics and Phonetics) from The English and Foreign Languages University, Hyderabad (Lucknow campus). He completed his Ph.D. in English from the University of Lucknow.

Dr. Singh emphasizes the importance of continuous learning in teaching and believes that a good teacher must first be a good learner. His passion for learning drives his teaching approach, which is centered around students. He employs a learner-centered methodology, incorporating practical activities such as live projects, debates, discussions, roleplays, and presentations to foster critical thinking and analytical skills among his students.

His teaching experience spans over 15 years, beginning in 2008, during which he has worked at various institutions including ICFAI National College in Kanpur, Galgotias College of Engineering and Technology in Greater Noida, Shri Ramswaroop Memorial University in Lucknow, and Dharmashastra National Law University in Jabalpur.

Dr. Singh's research interests encompass Applied Linguistics, methods for teaching literature, the decolonization of English teaching in India, English for Law, and life skills. He has presented twenty-five papers at national and international conferences and published fifteen research papers in international UGC care-listed journals, edited books, and conference proceedings.

His academic contributions include a Ph.D. thesis on "A Linguistic Analysis of the Syntactic Errors in the use of verb phrases made by the students of class 10th at Gonda (U.P.)" and an M.Phil. dissertation titled "A Comparative Study of Cultural, Communicative and Linguistic variations in the use of Englishes with special reference to Indian, American and British Englishes."
