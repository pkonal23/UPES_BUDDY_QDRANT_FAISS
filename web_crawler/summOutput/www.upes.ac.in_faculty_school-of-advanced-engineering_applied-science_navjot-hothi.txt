Dr. Navjot Hothi is an accomplished academic currently serving as an Assistant Professor at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES), where she teaches a range of Physics courses to both undergraduate and master's students. Her teaching portfolio includes subjects such as Nuclear and Particle Physics, Digital Systems, Quantum Mechanics, and Atomic and Molecular Spectroscopy. Dr. Hothi is also involved in conducting laboratory classes across various Physics disciplines and has a strong background in Material Science, having taught topics related to the Structure and Properties of Materials.

Her research interests are diverse, focusing on Regge Trajectories, Quantum Chromodynamics, Exotic Hadrons, and Environmental Sciences, with a particular emphasis on Physics Education Research. Dr. Hothi has made significant contributions to the field of Physics education by creating approximately 25 audio and video lectures aimed at enhancing student learning, especially during the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.

Dr. Hothi's academic credentials are notable; she was awarded the Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) and Senior Research Fellowship (SRF) from the University Grants Commission (UGC) as part of the Research Fellowship in Science for Meritorious Students (RFSMS) scheme during her Ph.D. studies. Additionally, she received a cultural fellowship from the Ministry of Culture for her work titled "Sitarwaden," which she held for three years.

Before her tenure at UPES, Dr. Hothi served as an Assistant Professor at Bahra University and Chitkara University, and she also taught at Kumaun University while being an RFSMS fellow. Her teaching philosophy emphasizes revealing the beauty of Physics and igniting students' passion for the subject. She employs various innovative teaching strategies, including the "Flipped Classroom" approach, quizzes, assignments, and tutorials, to foster cognitive skills and enhance problem-solving abilities among her students.
