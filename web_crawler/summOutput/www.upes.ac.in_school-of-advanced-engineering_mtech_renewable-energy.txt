The M. Tech. in Renewable Energy program at UPES School of Advanced Engineering is a comprehensive two-year postgraduate course designed to equip students with the knowledge and skills necessary for a successful career in the renewable energy sector. The program is structured into four semesters, each lasting six months, and emphasizes an industry-relevant curriculum developed in collaboration with energy experts from academia and industry.

### Key Features of the Program:
- **Eligibility Criteria**: Candidates must hold a B.Tech/B.E. degree in relevant engineering disciplines (Electrical, Electronics, Mechanical, Chemical, Instrumentation, Petroleum Engineering, or equivalent) with a minimum of 60% marks. Preference is given to those who have qualified GATE or NET. Additionally, candidates should have at least 60% marks in their Higher Secondary (12th grade) and Secondary (10th grade) examinations.
- **Industry Engagement**: The program fosters strong ties with the renewable energy industry, allowing students to participate in collaborative projects and gain practical insights into the field. Students have opportunities to work on funded research projects and consultancy assignments supported by government and funding agencies.
- **Hands-on Learning**: The program includes practical training through lab courses, field visits to relevant industries, and the chance to undertake research projects in collaboration with industry and research laboratories.
- **Career Opportunities**: Graduates can pursue diverse roles in research, consulting, project management, and implementation of renewable energy solutions across various sectors. The program prepares students for careers in solar, wind, and biomass energy companies, as well as opportunities in government agencies, consulting firms, and international organizations.

### Program Curriculum:
The curriculum covers a wide range of topics, including:
- Biomass Processing Technologies
- Energy Efficiency and Simulation Labs
- Renewable Energy Lab
- Hydrogen and Fuel Cell Technology
- Energy Storage Systems
- Smart Grid and Hybrid Power Generation Systems
- Life Cycle Assessment of Energy Systems
- Energy Policy, Audit, and Business Challenges

### Admission Process:
Interested candidates can apply online, and the last date for applications is February 28, 2025. Selection is based on personal interviews.
