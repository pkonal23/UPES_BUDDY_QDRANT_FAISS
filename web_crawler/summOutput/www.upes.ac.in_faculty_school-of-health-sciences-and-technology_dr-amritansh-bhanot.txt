Dr. Amritansh Bhanot is an Assistant Professor at the School of Health Sciences and Technology, UPES, having joined in January 2024. He holds a doctoral degree from the Department of Pharmacy at BITS, Pilani, and a master's degree in Pharmaceutical Chemistry from Punjabi University, Patiala. His academic credentials are further complemented by his success in the national level GPAT entrance exams in 2013 and 2014.

Dr. Bhanot's research interests primarily focus on Medicinal Chemistry, Pharmaceutical Chemistry, Cheminformatics, Drug Discovery, and Computer-Aided Drug Design. He is particularly engaged in drug discovery efforts targeting diseases such as Cancer, Malaria, and Tuberculosis. His scholarly activities include significant contributions to the understanding of antimalarial chemical space, as evidenced by his research on the physicochemical profiling of antiplasmodials and the discovery of small molecule inhibitors for Mycobacterium tuberculosis.

Before his current position, Dr. Bhanot served as an Assistant Professor at Lovely Professional University in Phagwara, Punjab, and previously at the Himalayan Institute of Pharmacy from April 2016 to August 2020. He is currently involved in the Rice Fortification Project, which is implemented by the Government of Uttarakhand and funded by the World Food Programme.

Dr. Bhanot has also applied for a patent concerning "Quinazoline based compounds and pharmaceutical compositions thereof," with application number 202311002668. His teaching philosophy emphasizes active engagement and critical thinking among students, encouraging them to question and apply their knowledge rather than passively absorb information.
