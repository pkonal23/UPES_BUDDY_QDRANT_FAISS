Shahina Anwarul is a highly qualified and dedicated professional in the field of Information Security, holding an M.Tech degree from NIT Allahabad (2016) and a B.Tech degree in Computer Science and Engineering from Uttar Pradesh Technical University, Lucknow (2013). She has also successfully qualified for the National Eligibility Test (NET) and Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) in 2022. Shahina has participated in the women empowerment program ‘Shakti’ conducted by UPES and is a certified Quick Heal Cyber Security Professional (QCSP) as well as a certified Ethical Hacker from the EC Council.

Currently, Shahina serves as an Assistant Professor-Selection Grade in the Systemics Cluster at the School of Computer Science, UPES, Dehradun, where she is also pursuing her PhD degree. With over 7 years of experience in academia and research, her research interests encompass Deep Learning, Image Processing, Computer Vision, and Information Security.

Shahina’s teaching philosophy emphasizes active learning and critical thinking, aiming to create a collaborative educational environment. She facilitates students' holistic development and encourages them to engage actively in their learning process. This approach fosters respect, open communication, and intellectual curiosity among her students.

Throughout her career, Shahina has taught various core and domain-specific subjects, including Data Structures using C, IT Systems Security, Principles of Programming Languages, and Fundamentals of Computer Science. Her scholarly contributions include several published research papers in SCI/Scopus-indexed journals and conference proceedings, focusing on topics like Information Security, Deep Learning, IoT, and Image Processing. Her research has been recognized with awards, including the Best Faculty Award from the School of Computer Science at UPES in 2022 and a nomination for the same award in 2021. Additionally, she received the Best Paper Award for her research on an automated video password-based authentication system using electrocardiogram at an international conference.
