Dr. Shanthi Johnson is a distinguished academic and leader with a robust commitment to international cooperation, equity, and inclusive diversity, which shape her approach to enhancing the human condition. She has a strong academic background and leadership experience, primarily in Canada, but also internationally, originating from India.

Currently, Dr. Johnson holds the position of Professor and Vice President of Research and Innovation at the University of Windsor. Prior to this role, she was the Dean of the School of Public Health at the University of Alberta. Her research focuses on critical issues such as falls and injury prevention, aging in place in rural communities, and social isolation among immigrant and refugee seniors. Her research initiatives are bolstered by her extensive international experience, which includes serving as a Fulbright Scholar in the United States, an Endeavour Executive Fellow in Australia, a DAAD Scholar in Germany, a GIAN visiting professor in India, and an Understanding China fellow.

In addition to her academic contributions, Dr. Johnson actively participates on various boards at both local and international levels, including the Eastern Kings Community Health Board, Annapolis Valley Health, Saskatchewan Health Research Foundation, National Seniors Council, and Shastri Indo-Canadian Institute. Her professional accolades include fellowships with the Dietitians of Canada, the American College of Sports Medicine, and the Gerontological Society of America.
