Dr. Anand Gaurav is a prominent researcher and educator in the field of medicinal and pharmaceutical chemistry, currently affiliated with the School of Health Sciences and Technology. He has made significant contributions to drug design and discovery, particularly focusing on phosphodiesterase (PDE) inhibitors for treating central nervous system disorders and respiratory issues.

**Publications and Research Contributions:**
Dr. Gaurav has authored over 50 publications in reputable journals, including:
- "Identification of Dual Inhibitor of Phosphodiesterase 1B/10A using Structure-Based Drug Design Approach" in *Journal of Molecular Liquids* (2021).
- "Discovery of Natural Product Inhibitors of Phosphodiesterase 10A as Novel Therapeutic Drug for Schizophrenia" in *Computational Biology and Chemistry* (2018).
- "An overview on synthetic methodologies and biological activities of pyrazoloquinolines" in *Mini-Reviews in Medicinal Chemistry* (2010).

He is also involved in editorial roles for international journals like *PLOS One* and *Frontiers in Drug Discovery* and serves as a reviewer for numerous high-impact journals.

**Grants and Awards:**
Dr. Gaurav has secured several research grants totaling over RM 300,000 from various institutions, including:
- RM 24,000 from CERVIE for a project on xylitol-starch base coprocessed adjuvants.
- RM 139,700 from the Ministry of Higher Education for research on ginger compounds.
- Recognition for innovations, including a Silver Award at the 13th MPS-USM Pharmacy Scientific Conference and multiple prizes at the Malaysian Technology Expo for his work on PDE inhibitors.
