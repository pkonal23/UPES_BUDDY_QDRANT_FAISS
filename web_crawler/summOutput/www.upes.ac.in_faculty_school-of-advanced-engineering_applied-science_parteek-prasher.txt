Dr. Parteek Prasher is an Associate Professor at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES), where he is a key member of the multidisciplinary Respiratory and Inflammatory Research (RAIR) team. This international team focuses on developing innovative technologies for managing airway diseases. Dr. Prasher's significant contributions include creating tablet formulations utilizing naturally occurring polysaccharides for the delivery of ulcerogenic Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs (NSAIDs). He is also researching multitargeting small molecule inhibitors aimed at adenocarcinoma, particularly those that address the abnormal galactosylation of mucins, highlighting his commitment to impactful scientific research.

In the academic sphere, Dr. Prasher teaches Organic Chemistry at both undergraduate and master's levels, covering courses such as Photochemistry and Pericyclic Reactions, Asymmetric Synthesis, and Organic Synthesis Lab, among others. His teaching philosophy emphasizes the integration of research with education, aiming to familiarize students with the latest advancements in their fields to inspire their interest and participation. He promotes group activities to enhance teamwork and prepare students for real-world challenges.

Dr. Prasher has received numerous accolades throughout his career, including the Junior Research Fellowship (2012), Senior Research Fellowship (2014), and Lectureship Award (2012) from the Council of Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR). He has been honored with the Research Excellence Award from UPES for three consecutive years (2020-2022) and was awarded the TARE fellowship and a research grant from the Science and Engineering Research Board (DST-SERB) recently. He was also recognized as one of the World's Top 2% Scientists for the year 2021 and serves as an Associate Editor for the journal Frontiers in Drug Delivery.

Before joining UPES in 2016, Dr. Prasher was an Assistant Professor at Lovely Professional University and currently holds the position of SERB-TARE fellow at the National Institute of Pharmaceutical Education and Research (NIPER) Punjab since December 2021.
