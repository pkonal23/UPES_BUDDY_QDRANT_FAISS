**Program Overview**

The Bachelor of Technology in Biotechnology (Pharmaceutical Biotechnology) program offered by the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) School of Health Sciences & Technology aims to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of biotechnology techniques and their application in the pharmaceutical industry. The program covers a wide range of subjects, including pharmacology, medicinal chemistry, rational drug discovery, stem cell technology, and discovery and development of biologics, enabling students to explore various aspects of research, innovation, development, and manufacturing of pharmaceutical products.

**Program Features**

The program features a world-class biotechnology laboratory equipped with advanced instruments and technologies for students' experiments. Students receive hands-on experience in diverse techniques using state-of-the-art tools like real-time PCR machines, fermenters, and spectrophotometers. Dedicated areas for molecular biology, protein analysis, cell culture, genomics, and bioinformatics foster an optimal environment for conducting experiments and encouraging collaboration. The lab promotes teamwork, the exchange of ideas, and the sharing of workstations. Experienced staff provides guidance on instrument operation, experimental design, data analysis, and troubleshooting, empowering students through workshops and hands-on training sessions.

**Career Opportunities**

Graduates can explore diverse career opportunities in the pharmaceutical and biotechnology industries. With a strong foundation in advanced biotechnological concepts and pharmaceutical research, they can pursue roles such as:

- Pharmaceutical Research Scientist
- Bioprocess Engineer
- Drug Development Specialist
- Regulatory Affairs Manager
- Bioinformatics Analyst

**Eligibility and Selection Criteria**

Interested candidates must meet the minimum eligibility criteria of a minimum of 50% marks at X & XII with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Mathematics, and English as major subjects in XII. The selection criteria are based on performance in the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies Engineering Aptitude Test (UPESEAT)/Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Mains/Board Merit/Scholastic Assessment Test (SAT)/Central Universities Common Entrance Test (CUET).
