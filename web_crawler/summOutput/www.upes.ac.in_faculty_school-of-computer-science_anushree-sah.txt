Anushree Sah is a seasoned IT professional with over 16 years of experience in both the IT industry and education. She has held significant roles in prominent companies such as Oracle Financial Services & Software Ltd., Western Union, and Dencare Ltd. Anushree holds a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science and Engineering and a Master's degree from the University of Greenwich, London, UK. She has been associated with the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) for seven years, where she has taken on various academic and administrative roles including subject coordinator, course coordinator, mentor, proctor, conference reviewer, and member of the curriculum design team.

Currently, Anushree serves as an Assistant Professor (Selection Grade) in the School of Computer Science at UPES. Her academic qualifications include a Bachelor of Technology in Computer Science, a Master of Science in Web Technology, and a PhD in Computer Science Engineering. She is known for her strong analytical, problem-solving, and communication skills, which she utilizes to drive organizational growth.

Anushree's teaching philosophy emphasizes enabling students to become independent, critical thinkers who actively engage in their education. She believes in creating a supportive and inclusive learning environment that nurtures students' unique strengths and talents, fostering their growth and passion for learning. Her goal is to empower students to become lifelong learners and positively impact the world around them.

In terms of scholarly activities, Anushree has served as the lead author on multiple research papers indexed in Scopus, focusing on enterprise applications, cloud computing, and blockchain applications. She has encouraged her students to publish quality research in reputable journals and conferences. Anushree has also reviewed and authored various articles for Web of Science journals and international conferences.

The courses she teaches encompass a blend of core and specialized subjects, including Programming with Java, Cloud Computing, Service-Oriented Computing, Python Programming, Discrete Mathematics, Programming Languages, Web Technologies, Fundamentals of Cloud Computing, Cloud Computing Architecture, Open Source and Open Standards, XML Programming, and CBNST.
