Mr. Gaurav Mittal is an Assistant Professor at the School of Law, University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES), Dehradun, where he specializes in Contract Law and Family Law, with additional expertise in Constitutional Law and interdisciplinary interests in Criminal Law. He has a rich academic background, having previously taught at the ICFAI University, Dehradun, for approximately 3.5 years before joining UPES in 2018. 

Mr. Mittal holds a B.Com. (Hons.) from Delhi University, as well as LL.B. and LL.M. degrees from the Faculty of Law, University of Delhi. He is currently pursuing a Ph.D. at UPES. His teaching philosophy emphasizes creating a supportive environment that encourages student inquiry and exploration of legal concepts. He employs innovative "flipping the classroom" techniques, which include assigning pre-reading tasks and utilizing quizzes to inform in-class activities, thereby enabling students to apply their knowledge to real-world scenarios.

His scholarly work includes several publications in esteemed law journals, such as:
1. "From fault to no-fault: irretrievable breakdown of marriages as a ground of divorce," published in the NIU International Journal of Human Rights, Volume 9(XI), 2022, pages 174–184.
2. "Same-sex relationships in India: An Emerging Jurisprudence," featured in Anvesak, Volume 52, No. 9(1), July–December 2022, pages 49–57.
3. "Comparative Analysis of Layoff and Retrenchment Law with Respect to the Evolving Ambit of the Industrial Relations Code, 2020," a book chapter in "Labour Reforms in India: Issues and Challenges in Current Legal Perspectives," published by Iterative International Publishers.

Mr. Mittal has received accolades for his academic excellence, including the Hiralal Daga Memorial Medal and the University Law Union Prize for Law, recognizing him as the top candidate in the LL.B. examinations of 2011.
