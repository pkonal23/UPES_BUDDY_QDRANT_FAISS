Chinta Jayanth Srinivas, a student from the School of Health Sciences & Technology, achieved notable recognition by securing the Second Prize for his poster presentation at an International Conference. His project, funded under the "SHODH" scheme by UPES, is specifically designed to support undergraduate research. 

The focus of his research was on the physicochemical properties of dried purple passion fruit peel, which he utilized to create a value-added functional food product. This innovative work highlights both his research capabilities and the potential applications of his findings in the food technology sector.

Dr. Abhishek Chandra, an Assistant Professor (Senior Scale), is associated with this project, underscoring the academic support provided to Chinta during his research endeavors.
