Dr. Kanak Pal Singh Parmar is an Associate Professor in the Physics Department at UPES Dehradun, India, with a strong focus on experimental material physics. His research interests encompass Photocatalytic and Photoelectrochemical Water Splitting, Solar Energy Devices, and the Rheology of Colloids. Dr. Parmar specializes in colloidal and nanosized photocatalysts for energy harvesting applications, emphasizing light-driven catalysis for energy-related technologies.

In his academic career, Dr. Parmar has taught a diverse array of courses at both undergraduate and postgraduate levels, including Engineering Physics, Digital Electronics, Lasers and Applications, Optics, Quantum Mechanics, Electrodynamics, Solid State Physics, and various laboratory courses. His teaching philosophy is centered around learner-centricity, aiming to create an environment conducive to progressive learning and instructional methods.

Dr. Parmar is an active member of the Indian Physics Association and has made significant contributions to the field, authoring over 25 articles in prestigious international journals and contributing to several book chapters. He has also delivered more than 35 keynote and plenary presentations at global conferences, showcasing his expertise. Furthermore, he is involved in developing e-content for multiple universities.

Dr. Parmar's academic qualifications include master's degrees in Optics and Physics from the Indian Institutes of Technology in New Delhi and Roorkee, respectively. He obtained his PhD from the Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU) and completed a postdoctoral fellowship at Pohang University of Science and Technology (POSTECH). His accolades include the GATE Fellowship at IIT Delhi, a PhD scholarship at NTNU, and a KOSEF Postdoctoral fellowship at POSTECH.
