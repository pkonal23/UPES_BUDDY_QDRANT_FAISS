Dr. Roohi Sille is a dedicated faculty member at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES), where she has contributed for nine years in various significant roles, including subject coordinator, course coordinator, mentor, conference reviewer, and member of the curriculum design team. She has taught foundational courses in computer science, such as Operating Systems, Computer Graphics, Microprocessors and Embedded Systems, Programming Languages, and Web Technologies, along with specialized subjects like Introduction to Mainframes, Mainframe Operating System, Linux on Mainframes, Open Source and Open Standards, and Licensing in Open Source Software (OSS).

As the Head of Internationality at the School of Computer Science, Dr. Sille collaborates extensively with the Department of International Affairs. She has played a pivotal role in guiding students through projects that have been presented and published at international conferences indexed by Scopus. Her research primarily focuses on the intersection of artificial intelligence and healthcare, particularly in deep learning and medical image segmentation, where she has been the lead author on several Scopus-indexed publications.

Dr. Sille is also active in scholarly activities, having reviewed articles for journals indexed in Web of Science and international conferences. She encourages her students to publish their work in reputable journals and conferences, fostering a research-oriented environment. Her teaching philosophy emphasizes creating an engaging learning atmosphere, utilizing methods such as "flipping the classroom," project-based learning, and interactive Q&A sessions to enhance student understanding.

Educationally, Dr. Sille holds a B.Tech in Biomedical Engineering (2012), an M.Tech in Artificial Intelligence and Neural Networks (2014), and a Ph.D. in Computer Science Engineering (2022). Her research interests include Artificial Intelligence, Deep Learning, and Medical Image Segmentation. She has contributed to numerous articles and book chapters in SCI and Scopus indexed journals, participated in international conference planning committees, and is involved in mentoring students across various domains.
