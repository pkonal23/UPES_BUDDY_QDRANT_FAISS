Dr. Rahul Kumar is an Associate Professor at the School of Advanced Engineering, holding an M. Tech from the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay and a PhD from Jaypee University of Engineering & Technology, Madhya Pradesh. With 13 years of experience in academia, he has significantly contributed to the field of particle engineering, particularly through crystallization. His research has led to numerous publications in both national and international journals, and he has presented at various conferences.

Currently, Dr. Kumar is working on a funded project titled "Solubility & dissolution rate enhancement of herbal compounds," supported by UPES with a funding of approximately 6 lakhs. He has received two SEED grants from UPES and is involved in various research activities, including antisolvent crystallization, co-crystallization, encapsulation, and the design of novel drug delivery systems for pharmaceutical and herbal compounds. His research also includes the extraction of bioactive compounds using supercritical CO2, and he is exploring energy-efficient methods for producing micron/nanoparticles.

Dr. Kumar has taught a range of courses, including Heat Transfer, Mass Transfer, Chemical Engineering Thermodynamics, Fluid Mechanics, Process Dynamics and Control, and Mechanical Operation. He emphasizes clarity in course objectives and outcomes, utilizing Bloom's Taxonomy to define learning goals. He encourages active learning through student presentations and collaborative projects in the departmental lab.

Before joining UPES, Dr. Kumar served as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Chemical Engineering at Jaypee University of Engineering & Technology from 2009 to 2013. He is also involved in setting question papers for state universities in Uttarakhand and Uttar Pradesh and collaborates with national and international researchers.
