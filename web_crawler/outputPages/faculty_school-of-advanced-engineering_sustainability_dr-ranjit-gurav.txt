Sustainability
He possesses extensive expertise in harnessing agriculture and food industry waste biomass, and by-products to produce microbial biodiesel, bioelectricity, bioplastics, exopolysaccharides, and biohydrogen. He has demonstrated proficiency in employing microbial, hydrogel, biochar, and self-activated carbon-based technologies for the remediation of organic contaminants and heavy metals. He has devised a novel process for converting poultry feather biomass into the amino acid cocktail, serving as a potent growth promoter, and chitin biomass as an antifungal agent. He has also designed a biologically active pervious structure for stormwater runoff quality improvement.

Scholarly Activities
Ranjit Gurav is an Associate Professor who ranks among the top 2% of global scientists in Biotechnology, Environmental Sciences, and Enabling & Strategic Technologies. Renowned for his impactful and award-winning contributions, his research centers on bioenergy, biochar, biopolymers, biomass valorization, water treatment, and bioelectrochemical systems. He has conducted extensive research across multiple countries, including the USA, South Korea, India, and China for a decade to propel advancements in his field. Dr. Gurav has effectively obtained research funding amounting to 100K USD from prestigious institutions including the NSF I-Corps of the USA, FIAP of Texas State University, USA, and the Fastrack YS of SERB, India. He has published 117 Scopus-indexed international articles and 4 international patents. He is also serving as associate editor, and topic editor to 8 international journals and a reviewer for 60 international journals. In his approach, he emphasizes the importance of integrating sustainable practices into technological innovation, ensuring that progress aligns with the principles of environmental conservation, social responsibility, and economic viability.
