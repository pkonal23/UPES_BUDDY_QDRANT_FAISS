Sr. Associate Professor
Prof. Kumar has taught various courses like Mechatronics Product Design, Control System Engineering, Robotics based Industrial automation, Hydraulic and Pneumatic Control, PLC and HMI, Analog Integrated Electronics, Instrumentation and Control Systems, Emerging Automotive Technology, Electric Vehicle Technology, Measurement and Instrumentation, Automotive Electrical and Electronics, and Vehicle Infotronics. These subjects cover a diverse range of topics in the field of engineering and technology, providing students with comprehensive knowledge and skills in various areas of study.
Dr. Roushan Kumar completed his bachelor’s degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering from Visvesvaraya Technological University, Belagavi, Karnataka in 2006. Subsequently, he pursued his master’s degree in automotive Electronics from Vellore Institute of Technology, Vellore, Tamil Nadu in 2008. Driven by his passion for research, he pursued Ph.D. from UPES, Dehradun in the year 2018.

Eligibility Criteria
Prof. Roushan Kumar recognizes the importance of practical applications of knowledge. He aims to equip students with the skills and knowledge necessary to succeed in their chosen fields. By integrating real-world examples, case studies, and hands-on experiences into his teaching methods, he helps students bridge the gap between theoretical concepts and practical implementation. Prof. Roushan Kumar's teaching philosophy revolves around a multidisciplinary, student-centric approach. His goal is to empower students by enhancing their technical, management, and interpersonal skills while also fostering personal and professional growth.
Before joining the University, Dr. Roushan Kumar gained valuable industry experience. He began his career as an intern for automotive software development at KPIT Cummins Infosystem Ltd. in Bangalore from 2007 to 2008. Following his internship, he continued working with the same organization as a Senior Software Engineer until 2010. He has published more than 30 research papers and has filed 12 Indian patents out of which 5 patents have been granted. These patents showcase his innovative thinking and practical application of his research findings.
