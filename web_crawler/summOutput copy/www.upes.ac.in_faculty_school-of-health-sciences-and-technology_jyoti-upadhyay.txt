Jyoti Upadhyay is an Associate Professor in the School of Health Sciences and Technology at UPES, holding an M. Pharm in Clinical Research and a Ph.D. in Pharmaceutical Sciences. Her doctoral research focused on Maternal and Child Health, specifically identifying cases of congenital disorders and examining the influence of persistent organic pollutants on congenital disabilities. 

In addition to her academic role, she serves as the coordinator of the Adverse Drug Reaction (ADR) Monitoring Centre at UPES, which is recognized by the National Coordination Centre (NCC) of the Pharmacovigilance Program of India (PvPI) based in Ghaziabad, India. Her research interests encompass a broad range of topics including Maternal and Child health disorders, Neuroscience, Pharmacology, and Pharmacotherapeutics.

Currently, Dr. Upadhyay is engaged in a project funded by the Department of Science and Technology (DST) titled “Establishment of Community Covid Resilience Resource Centre (CCRRC)” in the Doiwala, Vikas Nagar, and Sahaspur blocks of Dehradun District, Uttarakhand. Her work also extends to prenatal care, postpartum depression, and the investigation of causes behind stillbirths and neonatal diseases. 

Dr. Upadhyay has an extensive publication record with over 40 research and review papers in scientific journals indexed in Sci and Scopus. She is recognized as a domain expert in Clinical Research and also serves as the program coordinator for Clinical Research at UPES.
