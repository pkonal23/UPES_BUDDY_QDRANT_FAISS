Dr. Divya Tripathi is an accomplished researcher and Assistant Professor at the School of Health Sciences and Technology, with a focus on biomedical engineering and healthcare innovations. She has recently been awarded a research grant from Harvard Medical School, USA, further emphasizing her contributions to the field. Dr. Tripathi's research interests encompass a wide range of topics, including biomedical devices, immunology, biostatistics, and molecular cancer therapy.

Her significant publications include:
- "Multicancer Early Detection Tests for Cancer Diagnosis" co-authored with Kasturee Hajra and Dipak Maity, DOI: 10.14218/JERP.2023.00007.
- "Therapeutic Techniques for Cancerous Tumor Cells and Future Aspects of Molecular Cancer Therapy," co-authored with Kartikay Singh Nayal, DOI: 10.26717/BJSTR.2023.53.008407.
- "Current Development of Bio-inspired Nanoparticles in Cancer Theragnostics," DOI: 10.20944/preprints202306.0058.v1.

Dr. Tripathi's prior experience includes working as a Postdoctoral Researcher at the World Health Organization's Central for Disease Control in the USA, focusing on point-of-care testing for Measles and Rubella, and at the Institute of Nuclear Medicine and Allied Sciences, where she contributed to biomedical devices and formulations for combat casualty management.

Her teaching philosophy emphasizes individualized learning experiences and the importance of understanding science as a dynamic process. She has developed a core curriculum designed to enhance knowledge in bioinstrumentation, biomedical engineering, and clinical monitoring for undergraduate and graduate students.

Dr. Tripathi holds a Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology and is recognized for her logical and conceptual intelligence, extensive research experience, and numerous publications. She is also skilled in various experimental techniques, including small-animal imaging modalities and statistical analysis for research.

In terms of accolades, she received the AWSAR Award from the Department of Science & Technology (DST) for Best Popular Science Stories in 2021. Additionally, she was awarded Best Presentation at the International Conference on Advances in Polymeric Materials & Human Health Care (APA STERMI) in October 2019.

For inquiries, Dr. Tripathi can be contacted via email at Divya.tripathi@ddn.upes.ac.in. She is currently engaged in a Senior Research Fellowship at the Institute of Nuclear Medicine and Allied Sciences, Defence Research and Development Organization, since March 2020, following her Junior Research Fellowship from February 2018 to March 2020.
