Dr. Mitali Chugh is an accomplished academic with over 22 years of teaching experience, currently serving as an Assistant Professor (SG) at the Cybernetics Cluster of the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) in Dehradun, India. She has a strong background in programming languages, including C, C++, Python, SQL, and PL/SQL, alongside core subjects like Operating Systems, Advanced Database Management Systems, and Software Engineering. Her research interests encompass Software Process Improvement, Knowledge Management, Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning (ML), Deep Learning (DL), and Data Analytics.

Before her tenure at UPES, which began in 2016, Dr. Chugh held significant positions at various institutions, including Tula’s Institute of Engineering and Management (Head of Department from 2014 to 2016), Uttaranchal University (2010 to 2013), and ICFAI University (2007 to 2010). She has been actively involved in scholarly activities, having authored over 40 research papers and participated as a Technical Program Committee (TPC) member and session chair at numerous conferences both in India and internationally.

Dr. Chugh has been recognized for her research contributions, receiving the Best Researcher Award in 2020 from UPES and the Research Excellence Award in 2019. She has also been awarded grants for her research, including a sanctioned fund of $25,000 from the 2019 Laureate Grant for Inter-Institutional Research and the 2019 David Wilson Award. Her research projects include the AI-based solar-powered seawater desalination apparatus and a study titled "InsighTD – Investigating Causes and Implications of Technical Debt in Software Projects Globally Distributed through a Family of Surveys." 

In addition to her academic roles, Dr. Chugh is a lifetime member of the International Association of Engineers (IAENG) and the Computer Society of India (CSI). She has filed patents and received copyrights related to her research work. Her teaching philosophy emphasizes enthusiasm and experiential learning, using team-based long-term projects and fostering a competitive yet supportive classroom environment through innovative rewards like leaderboards and digital badges.
