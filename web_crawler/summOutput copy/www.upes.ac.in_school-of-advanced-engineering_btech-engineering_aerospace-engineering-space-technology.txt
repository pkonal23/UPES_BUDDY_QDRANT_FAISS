The B.Tech. program in Aerospace Engineering with a specialization in Space Technology at UPES School of Advanced Engineering offers a comprehensive curriculum designed to equip students with essential knowledge and skills for the evolving space industry. The program spans multiple semesters, covering a wide range of subjects including Propulsion, Aerodynamics, Aircraft Systems, and Aerospace Vehicle Design, among others. 

### Key Highlights of the Program:
- **Industry Trends**: The space industry is rapidly expanding with trends such as commercialization, satellite mega-constellations, asteroid mining, lunar colonization, and the rise of reusable rockets, making space access more affordable. The focus on sustainability is crucial to minimize environmental impacts and space debris.
- **Hands-On Learning**: Students engage in practical training through state-of-the-art laboratories and simulation tools, gaining experience in spacecraft design, propulsion systems, and satellite technology. Internships with leading aerospace organizations provide valuable industry exposure.
- **Research and Innovation**: The program emphasizes research in propulsion systems, life support systems, and advanced robotics, preparing students to contribute to future space missions.
- **Career Opportunities**: Graduates can pursue various roles including Space Mission Design Engineers, Propulsion Engineers, Aircraft Design & Analysis Engineers, and positions in esteemed research organizations like the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and National Aerospace Laboratories (NAL).

### Admission Criteria:
- **Eligibility**: Candidates must have a minimum of 50% marks in Classes X and XII, with at least 50% in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics in Class XII.
- **Selection Process**: Admission is based on performance in UPESEAT, JEE Mains, Board Merit, SAT, or CUET.
