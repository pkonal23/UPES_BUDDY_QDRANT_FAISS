Dr. Giampaolo Zuccheri is a permanent Staff Researcher and Assistant/Adjunct Professor at the University of Bologna, Italy, affiliated with the Department of Pharmacy and Biotechnologies as well as the Interdepartmental Center for Industrial Research on Life Science and Technologies. He teaches a course in Nanobiotechnology for the degree programs in Molecular Biotechnology and Pharmaceutical Biotechnology. Additionally, he has experience as a visiting scientist at the École polytechnique fédérale de Lausanne in Switzerland.

His research focuses on utilizing atomic force microscopy, through which he has made significant advancements in macromolecular and nanoscale characterization techniques. Dr. Zuccheri has pioneered single-molecule approaches that facilitate the study of nucleic acid structures and protein unfolding processes.

Throughout his academic career, Dr. Zuccheri has received numerous awards and has published extensively in his field. He holds a Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) and a Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Industrial Chemistry from the University of Bologna. He also participated as an exchange student and research associate at the Lawrence Berkeley Laboratory, University of California, Berkeley. Dr. Zuccheri earned his Ph.D. from the University of Calabria, Italy, and completed his postdoctoral studies at the University of Bologna.
