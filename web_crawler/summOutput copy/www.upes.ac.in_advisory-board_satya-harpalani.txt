Satya Harpalani is currently the Associate Dean of the College of Engineering, Computing, Technology and Mathematics at Southern Illinois University (SIU), USA. He earned his PhD from the University of California, Berkeley, his MS from Virginia Tech, and his BS from the Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur. Following the completion of his PhD, Dr. Harpalani joined the University of Arizona, where he served as a faculty member for sixteen years before transitioning to SIU in 2002 as the Department Chair.

Dr. Harpalani has over 30 years of experience in researching fluid flow in deep coal and shale, with a particular focus on coalbed methane recovery and modeling, as well as the geological sequestration of CO2. In 2015, he expanded his research interests to include coal-to-methane (CTM) conversion. Throughout his career, he has been the Principal Investigator for research projects amounting to approximately $15 million over the past fifteen years, securing funding from various U.S. government sources and private companies, including BP, ConocoPhillips, VICO Indonesia, Advanced Resources International, and Encana. Additionally, he has completed two projects in India, both financed by the U.S. government.
