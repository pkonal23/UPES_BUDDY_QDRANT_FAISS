The content outlines the curriculum and opportunities associated with the B.Tech. program in Civil Engineering with a specialization in Environmental Engineering at UPES School of Advanced Engineering. 

### Program Overview:
- **Curriculum Structure**: The program spans multiple semesters, each containing various specialized courses and labs. Key subjects include:
  - **Environmental Engineering**: Focus on urban infrastructure, water supply, sewage systems, water treatment, and waste management.
  - **Design and Structural Engineering**: Courses on concrete technology, steel structures, and structural design labs.
  - **Technological Integration**: Emphasis on integrating modern technologies like Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning into environmental modeling.

- **Hands-On Learning**: Students are encouraged to engage in practical applications, research projects, and internships to gain real-world experience. The program includes labs for physics, chemistry, concrete technology, structural design, and environmental engineering.

### Eligibility Criteria:
- **Academic Requirements**: Candidates must have a minimum of 50% marks in Classes X and XII, including 50% in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) in Class XII.
- **Selection Process**: Admission depends on performance in UPESEAT, JEE, Board Merit, SAT, or CUET.

### Career Opportunities:
Graduates of this program can pursue various roles, such as:
- Environmental Consultant
- Water Resources Engineer
- Air Quality Specialist
- Waste Management Engineer
- Sustainability Coordinator
- Opportunities in government agencies, research institutions, and the private sector are also available.
