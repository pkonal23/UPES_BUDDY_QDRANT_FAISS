1. **Economic Burden of Cancer**: A report by Simiao Chen et al., published in JAMA Oncology in February 2023, projects that the total economic cost of cancer will reach USD 25.2 trillion globally between 2020 and 2050. This figure is derived from an analysis of 29 types of cancer across 204 countries, using a value-of-life approach that quantifies the socio-economic impact of cancer beyond healthcare costs.

2. **Regional Economic Impact**: The economic impact of cancer varies significantly by region, with North America experiencing the highest burden at USD 3.9 trillion in 2020, followed by Europe (USD 3.5 trillion) and Asia (USD 3.1 trillion). The report highlights that high-income countries tend to experience a greater economic impact per cancer case compared to low- and middle-income countries.
