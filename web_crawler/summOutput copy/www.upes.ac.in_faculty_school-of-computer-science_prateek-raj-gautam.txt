Dr. Prateek Raj Gautam is an Assistant Professor at the School of Computer Science (SoCS) at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES). With approximately six years of teaching experience, he has instructed both undergraduate (B.Tech.) and postgraduate (M.Tech.) courses, and has also conducted short courses on a variety of topics. His research interests encompass a broad range of fields including Code Division Multiple Access (CDMA), Interleave Division Multiple Access (IDMA), Wireless Sensor Networks (WSN), Internet of Things (IoT), Artificial Intelligence/Machine Learning (AI/ML), Computer Vision, Deep Learning, Brain Wave Mapping, and Digital Twin technologies.

Dr. Gautam has a robust academic background, having been awarded fellowships from the Ministry of Human Resource and Development (MHRD) during his M.Tech. at Harcourt Butler Technological Institute (HBTI) in Kanpur, and from the University Grants Commission (UGC) during his Ph.D. studies at Motilal Nehru National Institute of Technology Allahabad (MNNIT). His scholarly contributions include publications in reputable journals and transactions, focusing on topics such as Optical Orthogonal Codes of OCDMA, Interleaver design for IDMA, and routing and localization in WSNs and IoT. He has actively presented his research at various international conferences and contributed to multiple book chapters.

Prior to his current position at UPES, Dr. Gautam was affiliated with the Computer Science and Engineering Department at the Centre for Advanced Studies, Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam Technical University in Lucknow, UP. He has also worked as an Assistant Professor in the Electronics and Communication Engineering Department at Allenhouse Institute of Technology in Kanpur, and at Naraina College of Engineering and Technology in Ratanpur, Kanpur. Additionally, he served as a Guest Faculty member in the Electronics and Communication Engineering Department at the Institute of Engineering and Technology, CSJMU Kanpur.

His teaching philosophy emphasizes the importance of connecting theoretical concepts with practical applications, using real-world examples to enhance student understanding of core engineering subjects. He integrates contemporary technologies into his teaching, utilizing programming languages such as Python and JavaScript, as well as various web applications, to help students analyze real-world engineering challenges effectively.
