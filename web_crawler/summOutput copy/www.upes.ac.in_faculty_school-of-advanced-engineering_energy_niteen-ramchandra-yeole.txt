Mr. Niteen Ramchandra Yeole is an accomplished academic and researcher, recognized for his presentation titled "Newer insights into green and low-cost zeolitic adsorbents for adsorptive desulfurization of liquid transportation fuels," which earned him the First Prize Award at the International Conference, SuschemE 2015, organized by the Institute of Chemical Technology (ICT).

**Research Interests and Teaching Philosophy:**
Mr. Yeole's teaching philosophy emphasizes engaging students through creative and innovative learning methods. He utilizes animations, video demonstrations, and research papers to enhance student understanding and assessments. He promotes open-ended, industry-based assignments that enable students to identify real-world industrial problems, select relevant variables, and design their own equipment based on specific assumptions.

**Professional Experience:**
Before joining the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES), Mr. Yeole worked at the Maharashtra Institute of Technology (MIT) in Pune, India. He also served as a visiting faculty member at the International Institute of Information Technology (I2IT) in Pune, where he taught "Fundamentals of Refining" to MBA students specializing in Oil & Gas. Mr. Yeole led a successful 5-day training program on "Fluid Catalytic Cracking Units in Refineries" in Dubai, organized by TUV Middle East for professionals in the petroleum refining sector. Additionally, he provided training on various petroleum refinery topics to L & T employees on the MIT campus, covering areas such as Atmospheric & Vacuum Distillation, Fluid Catalytic Cracking, Thermal Cracking, Catalytic Reforming, Isomerization, and Lube Oil Processing.

**Student Engagement and Scholarly Activities:**
As an activity coordinator, Mr. Yeole has inspired students to present their project work publicly and encouraged them to publish their research in esteemed journals. His students have participated in national and international conferences, receiving constructive feedback from experts. He is dedicated to harnessing the potential of youth by providing them with the knowledge and direction needed to achieve remarkable success.

**Research Focus:**
Mr. Yeole's research interests encompass adsorptive separations, solid-state ion exchange, regeneration and reuse of spent adsorbents, forest fire management through biomass utilization, cold energy utilization at LNG regasification terminals, statistical data analysis, and experimental design for energy conservation. His work primarily addresses issues related to energy, environment, and sustainability.

**Courses and Subjects Taught:**
Mr. Yeole has instructed a variety of Chemical Engineering subjects, including Novel Separation Processes, and specialized topics across upstream, midstream, and downstream sectors, such as Petroleum Refining, Gas Processing, LNG & Storage of Natural Gas, and Petroleum Production System Design. He encourages students to engage with current research literature, fostering a deeper interest in their studies and keeping them informed about the latest advancements in their fields.

In summary, Mr. Yeole is committed to advancing education in chemical engineering while actively contributing to research that supports energy efficiency and environmental sustainability.
