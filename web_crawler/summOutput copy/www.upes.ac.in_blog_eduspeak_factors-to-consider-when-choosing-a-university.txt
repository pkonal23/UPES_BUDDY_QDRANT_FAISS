The University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) offers a Global Pathway Programme that includes international collaborations and semester exchange programs aimed at preparing students to become global citizens. Choosing a university is a critical decision that requires careful consideration of various factors, including academic quality, location, fees, and campus safety. The cost of education encompasses tuition, accommodation, travel, meals, textbooks, and other incidental expenses, making it crucial for students to assess the total investment in their education against potential returns.

UPES is a UGC-recognized institution, accredited with an 'A' grade by NAAC, and has received high ratings from QS for employability, academic development, program strength, and campus facilities. It offers specialized undergraduate and postgraduate programs through its seven schools: School of Engineering, School of Computer Science, School of Design, School of Law, School of Business, School of Health Sciences, and School of Modern Media.

When selecting a university, students should consider factors such as the potential need to relocate, living arrangements, and the costs associated with living in different locations. It's also essential to ensure that universities are accredited, as this affects the recognition of degrees by employers and other educational institutions.

UPES promotes a holistic educational experience through its 'School for Life' initiative, which emphasizes trans-disciplinary learning and the development of 21st-century competencies. The university's campus is located in Dehradun, spanning 44 acres at the foothills of the Himalayas, providing a pollution-free and serene environment conducive to learning.
