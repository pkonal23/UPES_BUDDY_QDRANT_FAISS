Dr. Laxmi Kirola is an accomplished academic and researcher specializing in human genetic diseases, particularly neurodegenerative and neuromuscular disorders such as Parkinson’s disease and Alzheimer’s disease. She advocates for a multidisciplinary and holistic approach to education, emphasizing the importance of scientific explanations, innovative thinking, collaboration, and the development of soft skills among students. Dr. Kirola is committed to fostering good citizenship and societal contributions through education.

Her academic journey began with a Bachelor's and Master's degree from Kumaon University, Nainital, followed by a Ph.D. in Genetics from the University of Delhi. She has held various roles in academia and research, including a postdoctoral fellowship at Greenwood Genetics Center in South Carolina, USA, and research at Washington University in St. Louis, where she utilized large genomic datasets to study Alzheimer's disease. Dr. Kirola has also worked as a senior scientist at Redcliffe Life Sciences, Noida, where she provided genetic counseling, and held an assistant professor position at Amity University, Noida.

Dr. Kirola's research achievements include leading a project funded by the Institute of Eminence at Banaras Hindu University, focused on identifying and characterizing novel genetic variants in families with neuromuscular disorders using next-generation sequencing. Her scholarly contributions extend to serving as a reviewer for numerous scientific journals and being on the editorial board of the Journal of International Genetics and Genomics. She has received several prestigious awards, including the Malaviya Postdoctoral Fellowship in 2021, and has ongoing research proposals under the Government of India.

In her teaching role at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES), she instructs courses such as Cell and Molecular Biology, Human Genetics, Medical Genomics, and Gene Therapy, while emphasizing practical engagement through class activities, discussions, and assignments. Dr. Kirola is dedicated to mentoring students at various academic levels, having trained numerous Ph.D. candidates and over 50 undergraduate and postgraduate students.

Her future research interests include the application of machine learning and artificial intelligence in genomics, with a focus on early detection and DNA testing for genetic disorders. Dr. Kirola aims to continue her impactful research and teaching endeavors at UPES.
