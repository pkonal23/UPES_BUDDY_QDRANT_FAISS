Dr. Kamesh Rajendra Babu is an accomplished academic and researcher with a PhD in Molecular Medicine from the University of Heidelberg, Germany. His educational qualifications also include an MPhil in Biochemistry, an MSc in Biotechnology, and a BSc in Microbiology. Dr. Babu has made substantial contributions to molecular medicine, evidenced by his authorship of 7 published papers in reputable journals and presentations at 7 conferences, leading to a notable 345 citations of his work. His research primarily focuses on iron homeostasis in cancer, microRNAs, and RNA-binding proteins across various cancer types.

Dr. Babu has held prestigious positions, including a postdoctoral research fellowship at Duke-NUS Medical School and the Cancer Science Institute in Singapore, as well as a research fellowship at the University Hospital Heidelberg in Germany. He is also the founder of Samsara Biotech Pvt. Ltd., reflecting his entrepreneurial spirit. His contributions to the field have been recognized through various honors, including travel awards and grants for international conferences. He is an active member of the European Iron Club and was awarded the HBIGS Ph.D. graduate fellowship during his tenure in Heidelberg.

In addition to his scientific pursuits, Dr. Babu has interests in entrepreneurship, economics, and finance, and enjoys watching documentaries, which contribute to his well-rounded personality.
