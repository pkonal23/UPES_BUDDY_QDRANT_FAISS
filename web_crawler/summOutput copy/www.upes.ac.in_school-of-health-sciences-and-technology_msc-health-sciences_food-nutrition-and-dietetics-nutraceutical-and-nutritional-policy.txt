The M.Sc. in Nutrition & Dietetics with a specialization in Nutraceutical and Nutritional Policy at UPES School of Health Sciences & Technology offers a comprehensive curriculum designed to equip students with essential skills and knowledge for addressing contemporary public health challenges through nutrition. The program emphasizes the importance of nutrition policy in improving community health and prepares graduates to contribute to the development and implementation of effective nutrition policies addressing issues such as malnutrition, obesity, and chronic diseases.

**Program Structure:**
- The program spans multiple semesters, including courses such as Human Anatomy and Physiology, Nutritional Biochemistry, and various specialized labs (e.g., Therapeutic Nutrition Lab, Food Microbiology Lab).
- Students engage in extensive laboratory sessions, workshops, and internships to gain hands-on experience in the field.
- The curriculum includes elective courses that allow students to tailor their education to their interests, such as Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare, Nutrigenomics, and Pharmacogenomics.

**Career Opportunities:**
Graduates can pursue diverse roles in the nutrition and healthcare sectors, including:
- Nutraceutical Product Development Specialists
- Clinical Dietitians
- Nutritional Policy Analysts
- Food Regulatory Consultants
- Research Scientists

The program also fosters collaboration with prestigious international organizations, offering global learning and networking opportunities.

**Eligibility Criteria:**
To apply for the program, candidates must meet the following minimum eligibility criteria:
- A minimum of 50% marks in X, XII, and graduation (B.Sc. in relevant fields such as Biology, Biotechnology, Food Science, Microbiology, Nursing, etc.).

**Application Process:**
Interested students can apply by filling out an enquiry form, providing their first name, last name, email address, and mobile number. The selection process may involve a personal interview.

**Future Scope and Industry Trends:**
The field of nutraceuticals is evolving, driven by increasing consumer interest in functional foods, dietary supplements, and personalized nutrition. There is a growing demand for sustainable and ethically sourced products, alongside advancements in digital health technologies. Collaboration between industry and research institutions is fostering innovation, while governments focus on fortification initiatives to combat micronutrient deficiencies.

For more information or inquiries, prospective students are encouraged to contact the UPES School of Health Sciences & Technology directly.
