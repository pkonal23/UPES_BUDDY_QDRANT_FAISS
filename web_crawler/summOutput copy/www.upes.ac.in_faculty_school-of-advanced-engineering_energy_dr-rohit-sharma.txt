Dr. Rohit Sharma is an Assistant Professor in the Energy Cluster at UPES, where he emphasizes a two-way teaching process that fosters student engagement and participation. He utilizes traditional teaching methods alongside modern multimedia techniques, such as PowerPoint presentations and video lectures, to enhance students' understanding of complex subjects.

Dr. Sharma's research interests are diverse, focusing on critical areas such as flow assurance of crude oils, wax and asphaltene deposition, gas hydrates, enhanced oil recovery, carbon capture, utilization and storage, hydrogen transport and storage, synthetic fuel development, and drilling fluids. He has a robust academic background, having earned his B.Tech in Petroleum Engineering from Pandit Deendayal Petroleum University, Gujarat, and his M.Tech from IIT (ISM), Dhanbad. He completed his PhD in Petroleum Engineering and Chemical Engineering from IIT (ISM), Dhanbad, and Curtin University, Australia, specializing in flow assurance of crude oils.

Dr. Sharma's teaching portfolio includes courses such as Advanced Reservoir Engineering, Advanced Production Engineering, Reservoir Surveillance and Management, Geomechanics, and Drilling Fluid and Cementation Laboratory. He has been recognized for his academic excellence, receiving the Inder Mohan Thapar Foundation Research Award in 2020 for outstanding research in Petroleum Engineering, awarded jointly by IIT (ISM), Dhanbad, and the Karam Chand Thapar Group.

He has successfully secured research grants, including Rs. 60,000/- for projects focused on wax deposition in storage tanks and the development of vegetable oil-based drilling fluids for shale formations, both under the SHODH 2023 scheme by UPES Dehradun. Dr. Sharma has also demonstrated his scholarly impact through an extensive list of publications, including eight peer-reviewed journal articles, three full-length SPE conference articles, and numerous presentations at both national and international conferences, alongside two published Indian patents related to flow improvement of crude oils.
