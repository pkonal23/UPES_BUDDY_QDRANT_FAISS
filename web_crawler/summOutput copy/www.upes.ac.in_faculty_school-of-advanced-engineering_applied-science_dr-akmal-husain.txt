Dr. Akmal Husain is a faculty member at the School of Advanced Engineering, specializing in the fields of mathematical and numerical solutions to hyperbolic partial differential equations, particularly in gas dynamics. He received his Ph.D. from the Indian Institute of Technology, Banaras Hindu University, Varanasi, and has extensive research interests that include Quasilinear Systems of Partial Differential Equations, Analytical and Numerical Solutions of Nonlinear Wave Problems in Gas Dynamics, Hyperbolic Systems of Conservation Laws, and Lie Group Analysis for Partial Differential Equations.

Dr. Husain's teaching portfolio encompasses various subjects vital to Science and Engineering undergraduates, such as Engineering Mathematics I and II, Discrete Mathematics, Integral Calculus, Ordinary and Partial Differential Equations, Linear Algebra, and Complex Analysis. He also conducts laboratory courses in Python programming and MATLAB. His teaching philosophy emphasizes motivating students through fundamental clarity, critical thinking, and adapting instruction pace to suit learners' needs, thereby fostering an engaging learning environment.

In terms of professional experience, Dr. Husain has previously worked as an Assistant Professor at the Ideal Institute of Technology in Ghaziabad for two years and served as a Guest Faculty at Aligarh Muslim University in the Department of Applied Mathematics for one year. He has received notable accolades, including a UGC research fellowship from 2007 to 2009 and a Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) from the Department of Science and Technology (DST), India, for a project at Banaras Hindu University during 2009-2010.

Dr. Husain has contributed to the academic community through various publications in respected national and international journals and has acted as a reviewer for several high-profile journals. He has also played a role in curriculum development at both undergraduate and postgraduate levels.
