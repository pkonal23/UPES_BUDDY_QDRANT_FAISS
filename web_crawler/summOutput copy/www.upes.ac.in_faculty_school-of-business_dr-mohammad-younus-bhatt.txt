Dr. Mohammad Younus Bhatt is an Assistant Professor - Selection Grade at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES), specializing in Environmental Economics, Energy Economics, and Climate Change. He holds a Ph.D. in Economics with a focus on Environmental Valuation and Choice Modelling. Dr. Bhatt's research is primarily concentrated on the Himalayan region, where he employs econometric valuation techniques to address biodiversity loss and develop conservation strategies.

Throughout his career, Dr. Bhatt has completed two significant research projects under the GoUK initiative and received an IMPRESS grant from the Ministry of Human Resource Development, Government of India, in 2018-19. This grant was aimed at evaluating the economic benefits of preserving Himalayan biodiversity. His recent scholarly output includes a book that discusses biodiversity loss and energy vulnerability, emphasizing the importance of safeguarding environmental biodiversity and enhancing renewable energy efficiency.

Prior to his tenure at UPES, Dr. Bhatt was affiliated with Jamia Millia Islamia and held visiting positions at various Amity schools, including Biotechnology, Food Technology, and Economics. He teaches a range of courses including Principles of Economics, Managerial Economics, Microeconomics, and Macroeconomics to undergraduate and MBA students.

Dr. Bhatt's teaching philosophy is student-centric, favoring collaborative learning environments over traditional lecture formats. He promotes classroom dynamics that encourage dialogue and interaction, allowing students to engage in smaller group discussions to enhance their learning experience.

His current research projects focus on the economic valuation of Himalayan biodiversity, including alpha, beta, and gamma ecological aspects, and examine the implications of climate change on biodiversity in National Parks and Wetlands. Additionally, Dr. Bhatt explores the relationship between energy-economic growth and the adoption of electric vehicles in India, as well as the potential of eco-tourism in the Himalayan regions. His work often seeks financial support from institutions such as MHRD, ICSSR, and DST, aiming to develop sustainable growth strategies for local Himalayan communities.
