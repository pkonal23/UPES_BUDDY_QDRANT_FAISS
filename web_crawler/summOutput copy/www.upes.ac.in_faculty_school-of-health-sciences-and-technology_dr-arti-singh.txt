Dr. Arti Singh is an accomplished Associate Professor at the School of Health Sciences & Technology, UPES, Dehradun, with a notable research profile. According to Google Scholar, her H-index is 16, I10 index is 27, and she has accumulated over 2700 citations across platforms like SCOPUS, PubMed, and Google Scholar. Her publication record includes 17 original articles, 42 reviews, 4 book chapters, and 4 design patents, along with over 15 conference presentations at both national and international levels.

**Research Interests:**
Dr. Singh's research focuses on several critical areas, including:
- Mitochondrial dysfunction in neurodegenerative diseases
- Zebrafish neurobehavioral studies
- Effects of sleep deprivation
- Chemobrain (cognitive impairments due to chemotherapy)
- Environmental neurotoxicity
- Nano neurotherapeutics
She is particularly interested in identifying epigenetic changes and their implications in the progression of neurological diseases.

**Awards and Grants:**
Dr. Singh has received numerous accolades and funding for her research:
- In 2018, she was awarded the IBRO award for participation in the IBRO-APRC Neurosciences School at AIIMS, New Delhi, India.
- She also received the IBRO-APRC Exchange Fellowship for research in France and the IBRO International Travel Award for attending the 38th Annual Scientific Meeting of the Australasian Neuroscience Society in Brisbane, Australia.
- Additionally, she was awarded the Rajiv Gandhi National Fellowship (RGNF) for her PhD studies in 2013.
- Dr. Singh completed a DST SERB research project (2020-2023) as Principal Investigator, focusing on the pharmacological exploration of mitochondrial metabolomics to treat chemotherapy-induced cognitive impairments, with a grant of 34.25 lacs from the Government of India.

**Teaching Philosophy:**
Dr. Singh views teaching as a bridge connecting students to existing knowledge. She emphasizes a solution-centric approach to education that encourages critical thinking and analysis among students. Her teaching methods include hands-on learning, cooperative projects, and individual work, aiming to engage students and activate their learning processes. She has taught various subjects at both undergraduate and postgraduate levels, including Pharmacology, Pharmacoepidemiology, and Advanced Pharmacology.

**Professional Affiliations:**
Dr. Singh is a member of several neuroscience societies, including:
- Indian Academy of Neuroscience (IAN)
- Society for Neurosciences (SfN)
- Society for Alternatives to Animal Experiments-India (SAAE-I)

**Profile Summary:**
Dr. Singh holds an M.S. in Pharmacology and Toxicology and a Ph.D. in Pharmaceutical Sciences with a specialization in neuroscience. She has previously served as an Associate Professor in the Department of Pharmacology at ISF College of Pharmacy in Moga, Punjab, from February 2019 to May 2024, and she has also conducted research at the National University of Singapore as part of her fellowship.

In summary, Dr. Arti Singh is a distinguished academic and researcher with a strong commitment to advancing the field of neuroscience through her teaching and research initiatives.
