Nadeem Yousuf Khanday is an accomplished academic currently serving as an Assistant Professor in the School of Computer Science at UPES. He has a robust educational background, holding a Bachelor of Engineering (B.E.) in Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) from VTU Belgaum, a Master of Technology (M.Tech) in CSE from VGU Jaipur, and a Ph.D. from NIT Srinagar, Jammu and Kashmir. His academic prowess is further demonstrated by his successful completion of several competitive examinations, including GATE, UGC-NET, and JK-SET, which are highly esteemed in the academic community.

Prior to his current role at UPES, Khanday accumulated 1.5 years of teaching experience as a contract Assistant Professor at the National Institute of Technology (NIT) Srinagar and also served at the Institute of Technology, University of Kashmir. Throughout these positions, he has taught undergraduate and postgraduate students, engaged in research activities, and contributed positively to the academic environments of these institutions, showcasing his commitment to education.

Khanday's teaching portfolio includes a variety of subjects in computer science, such as Data Structures and Algorithms, Operating Systems, Theory of Computation, Graph Theory, Computer Vision, and Machine Learning with real-world applications. His expertise is particularly focused on image processing, computer vision, machine learning, and visual learning, which involve applying computational methods to analyze and interpret images and visual data.

His contributions to the field are notable, with numerous research papers published in SCI and Scopus-indexed journals, indicating a significant impact within the academic community. Additionally, he holds a patent for his innovative work and has presented his findings at various conferences and in book chapters, further enriching the field of computer science and engineering.

Khanday's teaching philosophy encompasses a variety of educational theories, including essentialism, progressivism, constructivism, perennialism, behaviorism, humanism, and social constructivism, reflecting his belief in diverse learning methodologies to facilitate effective education.
