Mr. Santosh Anand is an Assistant Professor (Senior Scale) in the field of Production and Operation Management, with a strong emphasis on Multi-Criteria Decision-Making Analysis. He is actively involved in research focused on food loss and waste, collaborating internationally to understand its causes and implications. This issue affects not only resources like water, labor, and energy but also has detrimental effects on the triple bottom line, which encompasses societal, economic, and environmental aspects.

**Teaching Philosophy:**
Mr. Anand's teaching philosophy is centered on fostering a supportive learning environment. He employs innovative teaching methods, including elements of "flipping the classroom." This approach involves assigning pre-reading tasks that prepare students for in-class quizzes, which are then utilized as a foundation for engaging assignments involving real-world data and scenarios. This methodology aims to enhance students' critical thinking skills, transitioning them from novice to expert levels.
