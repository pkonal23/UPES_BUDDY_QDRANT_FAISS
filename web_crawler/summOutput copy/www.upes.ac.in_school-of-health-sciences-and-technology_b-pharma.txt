The document outlines the Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharm.) program offered by UPES School of Health Sciences and Technology, highlighting its curriculum, career opportunities, and industry trends. 

**Program Overview:**
- B.Pharm. is a four-year undergraduate program divided into eight semesters, focusing on pharmaceutical sciences, including medicinal chemistry, pharmacology, and pharmaceutics. 
- Students gain clinical and community exposure, enabling them to become proactive change-makers in healthcare. 
- The program emphasizes on-the-job training through internships and clinical rotations, preparing students for licensure exams to become registered pharmacists.

**Career Opportunities:**
- Graduates can pursue various roles such as Hospital Pharmacist, Pharmaceutical Sales Representative, Clinical Research Associate, Quality Control Analyst, and more.
- The demand for pharmacists is expected to grow, driven by advancements in technology and increased healthcare needs. 
- Potential employers include top pharmaceutical companies like Cipla, Sun Pharma, Ranbaxy, and GlaxoSmithKline, as well as mid-sized firms.

**Curriculum Highlights:**
- Core subjects include Pharmaceutical Microbiology, Medicinal Chemistry, Pharmacology, Quality Assurance, and Herbal Drug Technology.
- Students are encouraged to participate in research projects, seminars, and internships, with opportunities for student and faculty exchanges with international institutions.

**Eligibility Criteria:**
- Candidates must have completed their 10th and 12th grades with a minimum of 50% marks in the science stream, including Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Maths, and English.
- Entrance exams such as JEE Mains, CUET, NEET, or UPESPAT with a minimum score of 50% are also required for admission.

**Future Scope:**
- Graduates can further their education with M. Pharm. and Ph.D. programs and explore careers in biotechnology, nanotechnology, and biomedical engineering.
- The increasing reliance on technology in pharmaceuticals opens new avenues in pharmacoinformatics, pharmacovigilance, and clinical research.
