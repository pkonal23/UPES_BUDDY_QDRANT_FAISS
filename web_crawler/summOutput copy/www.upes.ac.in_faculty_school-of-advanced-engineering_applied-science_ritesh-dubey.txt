Dr. Ritesh Dubey is an esteemed expert in the fields of Crystal Engineering, Self-assembly, and Supramolecular Chemistry. He holds a PhD from the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore, and has amassed nearly seven years of research experience at prestigious institutions globally. Dr. Dubey has made significant contributions to the scientific community, authoring numerous articles in high-impact, peer-reviewed journals, including the Journal of the American Chemical Society (ACS Publications, USA), Angewandte Chemie International Edition (Wiley Publications, Germany), IUCrJ (IUCr Publications, UK), and Chemical Communications (RSC Publications, UK).

His research interests are broad and include Self-assemblies, Crystallization, Non-covalent interactions, Programmed solids, Molecular materials, Crystalline materials, and Molecular aggregation. Notably, Dr. Dubey has been a key member of an international team working on a National Project in Japan aimed at developing innovative methods for characterizing complex analytes in microgram quantities. This project has significantly advanced chemical analysis in both academic and industrial settings, enabling exploration of previously inaccessible chemical spaces.

In addition to his research, Dr. Dubey is an active scientific reviewer for various international journals, such as Crystal Growth & Design (ACS Publications), CrystEngComm (RSC Publications), and Polyhedron (Elsevier Publications). He has taught a range of courses, including Contemporary Research, Challenges, and Trends (PhD level at the School of Engineering, with over 100 scholars), Pesticide Chemistry (B.Sc), and topics related to s&p-Block Elements, States of Matter, and Chemical Kinetics (B.Sc).

Dr. Dubey has been recognized for his research excellence with accolades such as the Toulouse Medal from IISc Bangalore and the Carl Storm International Diversity Fellowship from GRC USA. Prior to his current position as an Assistant Professor-SG at the School of Advanced Engineering at UPES, he was affiliated with the Indian Institute of Chemical Technology (IICT), Hyderabad, and served as a postdoctoral scientist at the University of Tokyo, Japan, contributing to the ACCEL project. His earlier research experience includes roles at IISc Bangalore and IIT Kanpur.

Dr. Dubey is dedicated to not only imparting knowledge but also inspiring creativity, nurturing curiosity, and fostering critical thinking among his students, preparing them for the complexities of the world beyond academia.
