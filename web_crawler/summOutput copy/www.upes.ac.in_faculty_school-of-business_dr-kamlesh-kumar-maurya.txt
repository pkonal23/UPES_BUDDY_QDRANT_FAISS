Dr. Kamlesh Kumar Maurya is an Assistant Professor at the School of Business, currently associated with the Post Graduate Certificate in Academic Practice (PGCAP) program, which aims to enhance the academic practice and career development of faculty and academic staff. He specializes in organizational behaviour and human resource management, focusing on creating inclusive, student-centered, and experiential learning environments. His teaching philosophy emphasizes promoting critical thinking, ethical leadership, and lifelong learning, equipping students with essential skills and values for successful HR careers.

Dr. Kamlesh has a robust academic background, having participated in an Online Faculty Development Programme (FDP) on ‘Art and Science of Literature Review and References’, organized by Shri Jairambhai Patel Institute of Business Management and Computer Applications (SJPI) from August 12 to August 14, 2021. His research interests encompass various aspects of human resource management, including employer branding, talent management, employee engagement, work-life balance, organizational commitment, psychological contracts, and employee turnover.

He has a commendable track record in academia, having published over seven research articles in international peer-reviewed journals indexed in prominent databases such as ABDC, Web of Science, and Scopus. Dr. Kamlesh has also presented invited lectures and research papers at both international and national conferences. His contributions to the field have been recognized with two ‘Best Paper’ awards at international conferences, as well as an award for Best Academic Intern from International Internship Volunteering Projects (IIVP) in London.

Before joining the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES), Dr. Kamlesh was affiliated with VBSPU State University in Uttar Pradesh, India, and worked as an HR executive for three years, where he managed the full life cycle of staffing, induction training, employee engagement programs, team building, and recreational activities.
