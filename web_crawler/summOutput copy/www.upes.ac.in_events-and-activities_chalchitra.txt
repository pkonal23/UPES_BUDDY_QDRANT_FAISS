The **Chalchitra: An International Smartphone Film Festival 2025** is set to take place from **March 20-21, 2025**, at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) in Dehradun, India. This festival, organized annually by the School of Liberal Studies and Media, aims to promote and celebrate mobile filmmaking, particularly focusing on themes of sustainability and social relevance.

### Key Details:
- **Eligibility**: Films must be completed between **September 1, 2023, and March 10, 2025**, and should include English subtitles. The maximum duration for submissions is **15 minutes**.
- **Categories**: The festival includes various categories for awards, such as:
  - Best in India (Fiction & Documentary, under 15 minutes)
  - Best International Film (under 15 minutes)
  - Best Script, Best Direction, Best Cinematography, Best Editing, Best Sound Design, and more.
- **Prizes**: Winners will receive monetary awards along with trophies and certificates. For instance, the Best in India for Fiction under 15 minutes will receive ₹40,000, while the Best Editing in the Documentary Film Showcase will receive ₹15,000.
- **Additional Awards**: There are also additional awards available, with prizes in the form of gift vouchers or coupons.

### Schedule Highlights:
- **Inaugural Ceremony**: March 20, 2025, from 1:00 PM to 2:00 PM.
- **Short Documentary Showcase**: March 20, 2025, from 10:30 AM to 1:00 PM.
- **Fiction Short Film Showcase**: Various sessions throughout the festival.
- **Valedictory and Award Ceremony**: March 21, 2025.

### Objective:
Chalchitra aims to provide a global platform for mobile filmmakers, encouraging them to explore diverse film cultures and socially relevant narratives. The festival emphasizes the importance of storytelling as a tool for societal change, aligning with its theme for this year: **"Exploring Society, Politics, Psychology, and Cinema for Sustainable Futures."**

With its commitment to democratizing creative expression, Chalchitra continues to empower filmmakers to push the boundaries of their craft using accessible technology like smartphones.
