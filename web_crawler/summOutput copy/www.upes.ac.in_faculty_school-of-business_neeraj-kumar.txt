Mr. Neeraj Kumar is currently serving as an Assistant Professor (Senior Scale) at the School of Business, UPES Dehradun, starting from March 1, 2024. His research interests are primarily focused on Operations and Supply Chain Management, particularly in areas such as cold supply chain management, sustainable supply chain performance, and the integration of technology in supply chains. He has notably researched the impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic on cold supply chain operations and has developed a sustainable framework to enhance environmental performance in this sector.

His research contributions have garnered recognition, including the Outstanding Research Award from Dr. B.R. Ambedkar NIT Jalandhar for his work on the COVID-19 pandemic's effects. Additionally, he received the Best Paper Award at the 7th International Conference on Production and Industrial Engineering (CPIE-2023) for his article analyzing the dynamics of phase change materials in cold chain applications. Mr. Kumar has been awarded MHRD research fellowships from the Government of India for his master's and PhD programs, which he pursued from July 2017 until February 29, 2024.

His academic background includes a PhD thesis in Operations and Supply Chain Management submitted at Dr. B.R. Ambedkar NIT Jalandhar, an M.Tech in Industrial and Production Engineering from the same institution, and a B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering from Galgotias College of Engineering and Technology in Greater Noida. He has successfully qualified the Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE) in Mechanical Engineering and Production and Industrial Engineering four times between 2014 and 2019, achieving an All India Rank of 449 in 2019.

In terms of teaching, Mr. Kumar has experience as a Teaching Assistant at Dr. B.R. Ambedkar NIT Jalandhar from July 15, 2019, to February 29, 2024, and teaches a variety of subjects including Operations Management, Operations Research, Materials Management, Supply Chain Management, Production Planning and Control, and Statistics at both postgraduate and undergraduate levels. His teaching philosophy emphasizes creating holistic learning experiences that encourage critical thinking, effective communication, and ethical decision-making among students, utilizing real-world examples and interactive methods to foster engagement.

Mr. Kumar has authored twelve research papers, with nine published in reputable international journals and three included in high-impact books. He also contributes to the academic community as a reviewer for various journals, including Sustainable Production and Consumption and PLOS ONE.
