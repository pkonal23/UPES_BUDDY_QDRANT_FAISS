Prof. Phani Tetali is a seasoned animator, educator, and cartoonist with over 30 years of experience in the animation and gaming industries, particularly in India. Before transitioning into academia, he spent 18 years in the industry, where he made significant contributions, including founding his own animation studio, ‘Animatix,’ in Chennai in 1995. At Animatix, he created ‘Tara Ki Duniya,’ a pioneering 13-episode cartoon animation series in Hindi, marking a significant milestone as the first of its kind in India.

In his professional career, Prof. Tetali held prominent positions, including Vice President and CTO, where he played a crucial role in establishing and managing leading animation and gaming studios such as DQ Entertainment, Danlaw Technologies, and FXLabs in Hyderabad. For the past 13 years, he has been imparting knowledge in various subjects related to animation and design at the IDC School of Design and IIT Bombay. His teaching portfolio includes Drawing, Animation Processes, Animation Principles, Character Design, Game Design, and Sequential Storytelling.

Prof. Tetali completed his B.Tech. from IIT, BHU, Varanasi in 1988, followed by an M.Des. in Visual Communication from the IDC School of Design, IIT Bombay in 1991. He also served as the Head of the IDC School of Design at IIT Bombay and is currently a Distinguished Professor. Additionally, he is involved in academic governance as a member of the Board of Studies for several reputed universities in India.
