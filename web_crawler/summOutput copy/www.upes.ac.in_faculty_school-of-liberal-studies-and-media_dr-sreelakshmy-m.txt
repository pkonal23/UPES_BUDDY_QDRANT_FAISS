Dr. Sreelakshmy M holds a doctorate in English Literature from the University of Hyderabad, where her research focused on the speculative fiction works of Octavia E. Butler, analyzing them through the lenses of m/othering, reproduction, and multispecies communities. She is currently an Assistant Professor at the School of Liberal Studies and Media at UPES.

Her scholarly contributions include publications in notable journals such as the Journal of Posthuman Studies, the SFRA Review, and the Journal of Literary and Cultural Disability Studies (JLCDS). She has an upcoming book chapter in an edited collection by Lexington Books titled "Supernatural Mothers and Wombs of Discontent." Additionally, her recent article, "Rewriting the Sexual Contract in Octavia E. Butler’s ‘Bloodchild’," will be published in ANQ: A Quarterly Journal of Short Articles, Notes and Reviews in 2025.

Before her tenure at UPES, Dr. Sreelakshmy served as visiting faculty at the National Institute of Technology, Warangal (NITW). She has also engaged in copyediting and proofreading for IWEOnline, the first peer-reviewed Open Educational Resource for Indian Writing in English, and for the UNESCO Chair in Vulnerability Studies. 

Dr. Sreelakshmy has received Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) and Senior Research Fellowship (SRF) from the University Grants Commission during her PhD from 2018 to 2023. Her research interests encompass Butler studies, Afrofuturism, posthumanism, and speculative fiction and fantasy.

In her teaching philosophy, Dr. Sreelakshmy emphasizes the importance of English Literature in fostering critical thinking. She aims to engage students with both canonical and non-canonical texts, encouraging them to closely read and contextualize these works within their socio-cultural backgrounds. She teaches courses such as "Ecofeminism and Ecological Futures," "Gender, Literature and Culture," and "Evolution of Poetry." Additionally, she has previously taught English for Technical Communication at NITW and contributed to an NPTEL/SWAYAM course on Posthumanism.
