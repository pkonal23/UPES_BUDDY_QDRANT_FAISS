Dr. Pankaj Singh is an Assistant Professor at the UPES School of Liberal Studies and Media, specializing in various fields including the History of Psychology, Introduction to Psychology, Philosophy of Mind, Cognitive Science, Film Philosophy, Applied Ethics, Human Resource Management, Organizational Behaviour, and Philosophy of Management. His academic journey includes a Ph.D. in Philosophical Psychology from the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur, India. Prior to his academic career, he worked as an HR manager in a paper manufacturing unit.

Dr. Singh has a robust research background, with a strong track record of publications in reputable national and international journals. His research interests span a wide array of topics, including Theoretical Psychology, Cognitive Science, Popular Culture and Philosophy, Ethical Leadership and Management, Critical Thinking and Writing, Ethics of Technology, Philosophy of Learning, Philosophy of Cognitive Science, Philosophy of Mind, Philosophy of Science, Existentialism, Language and Cognition, Indian Philosophy, and Western Philosophy. He has also qualified for the UGC NET in two subjects: management and philosophy.

His teaching philosophy emphasizes contextual learning, where he integrates examples from popular culture—such as films, games, anime, and TV shows—to enhance student engagement and interest. Dr. Singh believes that student enthusiasm and participation are indicators of high-quality learning. He has trained over 1,000 students in creating e-portfolios, which have proven effective in fostering reflective writing and engagement with media.

Dr. Singh has participated in several international fellowships and research visits, including:
- A visiting RTG fellowship by the Research and Training Group (RTG) Situated Cognition at Ruhr-University Bochum and Osnabruck University, Germany, hosted by Dr. Beate Krickel and Prof. Albert Newen.
- A research visit to Jagiellonian University, Krakow, Poland, hosted by Prof. Bipin Indurkhya.
- A research visit to the Centre for Philosophical Psychology at the University of Antwerp, Belgium, hosted by Prof. Eric Myin and Dr. Zuzanna Aleksandra Rucinska.
- A research visit to the Department of Logic, History and Philosophy of Science at Universidad Nacional de Educación a Distancia, Madrid, Spain, hosted by Dr. Manuel Heras-Escribano.
