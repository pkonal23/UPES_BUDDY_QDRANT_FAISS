The M. Tech. Chemical Engineering program at UPES School of Advanced Engineering is designed for students holding a B.Tech/BE in Chemical Engineering, Chemical Technology, Petro Chemical Engineering, or Petroleum Engineering, with a minimum of 60% marks or equivalent. Preference is given to candidates who are Gate/NET qualified. The program emphasizes practical skills and a robust curriculum, preparing graduates for various roles in industries such as oil and gas, pharmaceuticals, renewable energy, and environmental engineering.

Key highlights of the program include:
- A strong focus on chemical process development and design.
- Hands-on experience with process simulators, enhancing technical skills.
- Collaboration with Grasim Industries (Aditya Birla Ltd.) through a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) for industrial projects and internships, providing exposure to cutting-edge technologies and industry best practices.
- Comprehensive training in process design, automation, and optimization, essential for modern chemical industries.
- Access to state-of-the-art interdisciplinary infrastructure at the Centre of Excellence for Energy Systems.
- A curriculum that integrates advanced topics such as sustainability, nanotechnology, biotechnology, and artificial intelligence.

The program includes a one-year mandatory industrial project, allowing students to apply theoretical knowledge in real-world scenarios. Graduates can expect diverse career opportunities as process engineers, research and development specialists, project managers, or technical consultants in esteemed organizations globally.

To apply, interested students must meet the eligibility criteria, which includes achieving a minimum of 60% marks at the Higher and Senior Secondary levels (10th and 12th grades). The selection process is based on performance in a personal interview.
