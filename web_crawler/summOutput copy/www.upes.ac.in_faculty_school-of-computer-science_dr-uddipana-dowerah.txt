Dr. Uddipana Dowerah is a faculty member at the School of Computer Science at UPES, having joined in November 2024. She specializes in Cryptography, focusing on areas such as computing on encrypted data and privacy-preserving cryptographic solutions. Dr. Dowerah holds a Ph.D. from IIT Guwahati and has extensive research experience, including a postdoctoral position at the Cybersecurity and Applied Cryptography lab at the University of St. Gallen, Switzerland. She has also been a visiting researcher at Chalmers University of Technology in Sweden.

Her notable research achievements include receiving the Hasler Foundation grant for her project, “Enhancing Data Privacy using Functional Encryption,” during her postdoctoral research. Additionally, she was awarded the Erasmus+ research fellowship to conduct research at the University of Pardubice in the Czech Republic during her Ph.D. studies.

Dr. Dowerah is an active contributor to the academic community, having authored numerous publications and served as a reviewer for various conferences and journals in the field of Cryptography. She is a life member of the Cryptology Research Society of India (CRSI) and holds memberships in both ACM and IEEE.

In terms of her teaching philosophy, Dr. Dowerah emphasizes empowering students to think critically and engage with complex problems while developing both technical and soft skills. She advocates for a learning environment that promotes conceptual understanding, active learning, and real-world applications, encouraging students to ask questions and explore beyond the standard curriculum.

For contact, Dr. Dowerah can be reached via email at uddipana.dowerah@ddn.upes.ac.in. Her research interests include Cryptography, Post-Quantum Cryptography, Fully Homomorphic Encryption, Functional Encryption, and Private Stream Aggregation.
