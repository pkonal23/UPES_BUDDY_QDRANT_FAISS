Dr. Pankaj Kundan Dadure is an Assistant Professor at the School of Computer Science at UPES, having joined in August 2022. He has a robust academic background, holding a Ph.D. from the National Institute of Technology Silchar and an M.Tech from the National Institute of Technology Arunachal Pradesh. His research interests encompass a variety of areas within Artificial Intelligence, with a particular focus on Natural Language Processing (NLP), Mathematical Information Retrieval, Visual Question Answering, and addressing Gender Bias in AI-NLP applications.

Dr. Dadure's scholarly work aims to enhance the way computers comprehend human language and the retrieval of information. He has made significant contributions to the field, including the design of formula embedding techniques for improved mathematical content retrieval, the generation of accurate image descriptions in low-resource languages, and advancements in automatic text summarization and translation quality for languages such as Khasi, Manipuri, and Assamese. His efforts are directed towards bridging language barriers and enhancing accessibility across various domains through innovative NLP methodologies.

In addition to his research, Dr. Dadure has an engaging teaching philosophy that emphasizes practical knowledge, critical thinking, and hands-on learning. He incorporates real-life examples and encourages active participation in his classes, fostering curiosity, collaboration, and effective communication among students. This approach is designed to help students understand complex concepts and develop a sustained interest in computer science.

Dr. Dadure has been recognized for his contributions to the field, winning the Lip-Sync Challenge-2021 organized by IIT Madras and CDAC Mumbai, and being a finalist at both Toycathon-2021 and Manthan 2021, events organized by the Government of India. He also received the Best Poster Award at the Student Research Convention - Anveshan 2.0, highlighting his expertise and impact in Artificial Intelligence and NLP.
