Mr. Abhijit Anand is an Assistant Professor at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES), where he employs the 'Socratic and Discussion method' of teaching to facilitate a deeper understanding of legal issues, their background, and relevant legal provisions among students. He has over five years of teaching experience, having previously held positions at Karnavati University in Gandhinagar and Glocal University.

Mr. Anand has a strong academic background, having completed his Bachelor of Arts and Bachelor of Laws (BALLB) from Dr. Ram Manohar Lohiya National Law University in Lucknow, followed by a Master of Laws (LL.M) from the Tata Institute of Social Sciences, specializing in Access to Justice. His expertise encompasses various subjects including Law of Crimes, Indian Evidence Law, Penology, Victimology, Law of Contracts, and Intellectual Property Laws. He is currently involved in teaching courses related to the implications of caste on law and public policy, education, and prisoners' rights.

In addition to his teaching responsibilities, Mr. Anand is active in scholarly activities, having published several articles in Scopus-indexed journals, National Law University (NLU) journals, and prominent newspapers. His contributions to legal education and research reflect his passion for the field and his commitment to enhancing the understanding of law and society.
