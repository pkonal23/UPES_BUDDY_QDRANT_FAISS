Dr. Neelu Jyothi Ahuja is a distinguished academic and researcher in the field of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and its applications, particularly in education and healthcare. Currently, she is working on innovative projects such as the development of AI-based tutoring tools for individuals with learning disabilities and an AI-driven smart snake trapper designed to capture large snakes safely without human involvement. Her research emphasizes the importance of making technological advancements accessible to the grassroots level of society.

Under her supervision, 11 scholars have successfully completed their PhDs, while 6 others are currently pursuing their doctoral studies. Dr. Ahuja is a recognized speaker at international forums, including her address at the United Nations during the 8th Multi-Stakeholder Forum for Science, Technology and Innovations for Sustainable Development Goals. She also serves on various committees, such as the WHO's Promotion of Assistive Products and Technologies and the DST Expert Committee for inspection and review of CORE projects.

Her research interests encompass AI and machine learning applications, intelligent tutoring systems, blockchain technology, and object-oriented development. She teaches several courses at the School of Computer Science, including Artificial Intelligence, Operating Systems, Object-Oriented Analysis and Design, Object-Oriented Programming, Cloud Computing Fundamentals, and Virtualization Basics, where she explores cloud delivery models and virtualization types.

Dr. Ahuja has received several accolades for her contributions to research and education, including the Himalayan Nari Shakti Award (2020) from the Uttarakhand Law Commission and the Uttarakhand Human Rights Commission, the IGEN Women Achievers Award (2021) from the Institute of Green Engineers, and the Leading Women Researcher Award from the Uttarakhand State Council for Science and Technology (UCOST) on International Women’s Day (2022). She has also garnered over ₹1.5 crore in grants for various research initiatives and has delivered multiple government-sponsored R&D projects since 2013.

With over 24 years of academic experience, including 17 years at UPES, Dr. Ahuja began her career in software development before transitioning to academia. She is known for her innovative teaching methodologies and her commitment to enhancing student engagement and learning experiences beyond traditional classroom settings.

Dr. Ahuja has published more than 70 papers in respected journals and conferences, including the Journal of Human-Computer Interaction and IEEE Access. She is a member of professional organizations such as IEEE and ACM, and holds certifications as an ISO-27001:2013 Lead Auditor.
