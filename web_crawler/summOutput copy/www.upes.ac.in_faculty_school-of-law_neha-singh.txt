Dr. Neha Singh is a distinguished academician in the legal field, transitioning from a successful legal career to academia. She has authored several significant publications, including books and research papers, focusing on reproductive rights, environmental law, corporate laws, and gender justice. Notable works include:

1. **Books:**
   - "The Ambit Of Force Majeure On Suspension of Rent in the Times of Pandemic" (2020), published in *The International Journal of Analytical and Experimental Modal Analysis*, ISSN 0886-9367.
   - "Reproductive Rights of Women in India" (2023), published by Bloomsbury Prime, ISBN 978-93-56404-74-8.
   - "Environmental Law Governance and Sustainable Development" (2023), ISBN 978-81-961751-1-5, published by Integrity Education.
   - "Comparative Legal Analysis of Adoption vs. Surrogacy in India" (2023), published by Routledge Taylor and Francis Group London, ISBN 978-1-032-70841-6.
   - "INTERSECTIONS OF INDIAN LAW, DEMOCRACY, AND SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOAL 16" in *Inclusive Democracy: A way Forward to peace and Justice* (2023), ISBN 9789391654399, published by EZ Education.

2. **Research Papers:**
   - "Abortion Rights in India - A critical review of the Medical Termination of Pregnancy (Amendment) Bill, 2020" (2021), published in *Shodh Sarita*.
   - "Reproductive Rights of Rural Women in India" (2019), published in the same journal as above.
   - "The Invisible Conflicts in Parenting Rights of LGBTQ Community: A Legal Study in India" (2021), published in *Kanpur Philosophers*, ISSN 23488301.

Dr. Singh has a robust legal background with six years of experience practicing law before the Supreme Court of India and various other judicial authorities. She has served as a legal advisor to several government bodies, including the National Highways Authority of India and the Delhi Urban Shelter Improvement Board. Dr. Singh holds an LL.M. in Intellectual Property Laws from the National Law University, Jodhpur, and a Ph.D. from Manav Rachna University, Faridabad, Haryana.

Her scholarly interests encompass a wide range of topics, including corporate law, intellectual property rights, international law, technology law, artificial intelligence, women's rights, gender justice, and comparative constitutional law.

In her role as an assistant professor, Dr. Singh emphasizes a teaching philosophy that aims to transform students into critical and innovative thinkers, encouraging them to engage with complex legal issues from a multidisciplinary perspective. She previously worked at K.R. Mangalam University, Gurugram, before joining the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES).

Dr. Singh is also noted for her patent titled "Titled for performance of crowdsourcing with donations to support micro-finance for women," classified on February 17, 2023.
