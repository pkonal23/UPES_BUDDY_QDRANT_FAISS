### Overview of JEE Mains and JEE Advanced
- **JEE Mains** serves as the first stage of the Joint Entrance Examination, conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA). It is crucial for qualifying for JEE Advanced and gaining admission to various engineering colleges, including NITs and IIITs.
- **JEE Advanced** is the second stage, exclusively for admission to the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs), known for its challenging nature and focus on in-depth knowledge and problem-solving skills.

### Exam Structure
- **JEE Mains 2025:**
  - **Number of Papers:** 2 (Paper 1 for B.E./B.Tech and Paper 2 for B.Arch/B.Planning)
  - **Duration:** 3 hours per paper
  - **Total Questions:** 90 (30 each in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics)
  - **Marking Scheme:** +4 for correct answers, -1 for incorrect answers; includes full, partial, and negative marking.

- **JEE Advanced 2025:**
  - **Number of Papers:** 2 (compulsory for all candidates)
  - **Duration:** 3 hours per paper
  - **Syllabus Focus:** Advanced topics in Physics (e.g., Wave Optics, Rigid Body Dynamics), Chemistry (e.g., Thermodynamics, Electrochemistry), and Mathematics (e.g., Calculus, Vectors).

### Preparation Tips
1. **Prioritize High-Weightage Topics:** Focus on subjects and topics that carry more marks.
2. **Simulate Exam Conditions:** Practice under timed conditions to build confidence.
3. **Regular Mock Tests:** Engage in solving previous years’ papers and mock tests to familiarize with the exam pattern.
4. **Conceptual Clarity:** Deepen understanding of fundamental principles across subjects.
5. **Effective Time Management:** Utilize the time between JEE Mains and Advanced (typically 40-50 days) to address weak areas and revise key materials.

### Eligibility Criteria
- **JEE Mains:** Open to students who have passed or are appearing for Class 12 exams, with no age restriction. Candidates can attempt the exam up to three consecutive years after completing Class 12.
- **JEE Advanced:** Only the top 2,50,000 JEE Mains qualifiers are eligible. Candidates born on or after October 1, 2000, can apply, with a 5-year age relaxation for PwD, ST, or SC categories.
