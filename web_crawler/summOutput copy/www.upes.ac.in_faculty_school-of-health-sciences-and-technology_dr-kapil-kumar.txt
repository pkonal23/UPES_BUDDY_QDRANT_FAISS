**Dr. Kapil Kumar - Profile Summary**

**Contact Information:**
- Email: kapil.kumar@ddn.upes.ac.in

**Current Position:**
- Associate Professor at the School of Health Sciences and Technology, UPES, joined in January 2024.

**Education:**
- M.S. (Pharm) and Ph.D. in Medicinal Chemistry from the National Institute of Pharmaceutical Education and Research (NIPER), Mohali, India.

**Previous Work Experience:**
- Faculty at Indo-Soviet Friendship College of Pharmacy (ISFCP), Moga, Punjab (Aug 2015 - Feb 2017).
- Assistant Professor at Apeejay Stya University, Gurugram, Haryana (Feb 2017 - July 2019; March 2022 - Jan 2024).
- Assistant Professor at Narsee Monjee Institute of Management Studies (NMIMS), Hyderabad (July 2019 - March 2022).

**Research Interests:**
- Focus on the synthesis of novel pharmacological molecules targeting cancer, diabetes, metabolic syndrome, and infectious diseases.
- Expertise in heterocyclic synthesis, methodology development, asymmetric synthesis, green chemistry, total synthesis, and computer-aided drug design.

**Publications and Scholarly Contributions:**
- Authored over 34 peer-reviewed articles in high-impact journals such as *European Journal of Medicinal Chemistry*, *Bioorganic Chemistry*, and *Journal of Organic Chemistry*.
- Contributed to seven edited textbooks and one book chapter (Wiley).
- Actively involved in the editorial boards of *Medicinal Chemistry* and *Current Computer-Aided Drug Design* since 2020.

**Awards and Grants:**
- Received a grant of Rs. 17 lac from Haryana State Council for Science, Innovation and Technology for a project on converting agricultural waste into pharmaceutical excipients.
- Awarded Rs. 3.2 lac SEED Grant from Apeejay Stya University for developing tryptanthrin derivatives as antioxidant agents.
- Recognized with travel grants for attending conferences, including a notable award for an oral presentation at IIT Guwahati.

**Teaching Philosophy:**
- Advocates for an active learning approach, emphasizing student engagement through interactive sessions, blended learning, group discussions, and gamification.
- Aims to inspire students to learn, analyze, and prepare for future challenges, recognizing the unique strengths of each student.

**Selected Publications:**
- Kaur, R.; Manjal, S. K.; Rawal, R. K.; Kumar, K. "Recent synthetic and medicinal perspective of tryptanthrin." *Wiley Publication*, 2021, pp. 205-238.
- Pawar, S.; Kumar, K.; Gupta, M. K.; Rawal, R. K. "Synthetic and medicinal perspective of fused-thiazoles as anticancer agents." *Chem. Biodivers*, 2017, pp. 195-202.
- Kumar, K.; Rawal, R. K. "CuI/DBU-mediated MBH reaction of isatins: A convenient synthesis of 3-substituted-3-hydroxy-2-oxindole." *Tetrahedron*, 2022.

**Editorial Roles:**
- Associate Editorial Board Member for *Medicinal Chemistry* and *Current Computer-Aided Drug Design*.

Dr. Kumar embodies a commitment to research excellence, innovative teaching, and mentorship in the field of medicinal chemistry, contributing significantly to both academic and practical advancements in pharmaceutical sciences.
