Dr. Samrat Dev is an accomplished Assistant Professor in the Department of Design, specializing in Ergonomics. He holds an undergraduate degree, a master's degree, and a PhD from the University of Calcutta. His professional background includes serving as a Senior Research Fellow on an INDO-US collaborative project funded by the Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) in New Delhi. Dr. Dev has a rich history of presenting research at both national and international conferences and has published extensively in reputable journals throughout his academic career.

His work experience includes positions at the National Institute of Design (NID) in Ahmedabad and as a faculty member at the Maharashtra Institute of Technology (MIT) in Pune. He has also contributed as a visiting professor at various institutions, including Kalyani University, Avantika University, and the Maharaja Ranjitsingh Gaekwad Institute of Design (MRID) in Baroda.

Dr. Dev's teaching philosophy emphasizes the importance of understanding the individualized nature of student learning. He believes in tailoring his teaching methods to accommodate different learning styles and paces, fostering an environment that encourages active participation and reflection. His courses cover essential topics such as Ergonomics, Human Factors, Visual Communication, Safety and Occupational Health, Physiology, and Interior and Space Design. He incorporates a "learning-by-doing" approach, promoting hands-on experiences that enhance practical understanding.

Throughout his career, Dr. Dev has received several accolades, including the Science Communicator Award from the Indian Science Congress in 2012 and the DST Young Scientist Travel Grant for attending an international conference in 2013. He has also coordinated the Research and PhD program at the School of Design and has mentored various university-funded student projects, leading many students to pursue doctoral research.
