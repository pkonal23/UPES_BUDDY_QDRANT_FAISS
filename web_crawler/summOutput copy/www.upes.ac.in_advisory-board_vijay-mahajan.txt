Vijay Mahajan is a distinguished alumnus with an impressive educational background, having graduated from IIT Delhi in 1975, IIM Ahmedabad in 1981, and being a Fellow of the Woodrow Wilson School at Princeton University in 1989. His extensive involvement in policy-making is highlighted by his participation in several significant committees, including the Rangarajan Committee on Financial Inclusion (2007-08), the Raghuram Rajan Committee on Financial Sector Reforms (2008-09), and his tenure with the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India from 2005 to 2010.

In addition to his policy work, Vijay has contributed to various non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and social enterprises, earning recognition as one of 60 Outstanding Social Entrepreneurs at the World Economic Forum in Davos in 2003. He was named an Ashoka Fellow in 2008 and became a Rockefeller Bellagio Centre Resident Fellow in 2017. Since 2018, he has held the position of CEO at the Rajiv Gandhi Foundation and serves as the Director of the Rajiv Gandhi Institute for Contemporary Studies in New Delhi.

Vijay is the founder of PRADAN, an NGO established in 1983, which has successfully promoted the livelihoods of over a million poor households. He transitioned from PRADAN in 1991 to establish the Basix Social Enterprise Group in 1996, which has since supported over five million poor households through various initiatives in microfinance and livelihood promotion. The BASIX Group encompasses seven entities focused on financial inclusion, agricultural services, vocational training, waste management, action research, training, and consulting for livelihood promotion. Vijay stepped down from his role at BASIX in 2017.
