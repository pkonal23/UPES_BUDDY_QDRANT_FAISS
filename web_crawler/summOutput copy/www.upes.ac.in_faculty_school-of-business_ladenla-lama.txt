**Profile Summary of Assistant Professor Ladenla Lama**

**Specialization:**  
Assistant Professor Ladenla Lama specializes in Development Economics and Industrial Economics, focusing primarily on Low Carbon Energy Industries in India. Her research investigates the Solar Photovoltaics and Wind Turbine Manufacturing Industries, analyzing the interplay between economic development, industrial factors, and the challenges faced by renewable energy technologies.

**Research Interests:**  
- **Low Carbon Energy Industries:** In-depth exploration of the Solar Photovoltaics and Wind Turbine sectors in India.
- **Gender and Development Economics:** Research on social inequalities, particularly asset holding disparities among Indian women, and variations in nutritional status across Indian states.
- **Agricultural Economics:** Study of regional trends in India during the globalization era.
- **Socio-economic Development:** Examination of the impact of government policies on rural primary education in northeastern India, along with the evolution of renewable energy industries.

**Teaching Philosophy:**  
Assistant Professor Lama emphasizes a balanced approach in teaching, combining complex theoretical concepts with practical applications. This method encourages independent thinking and equips students with critical skills for real-world application.

**Courses Taught:**

- Industrial Economics I
- Gender Economics

**Awards and Grants:**

**Professional Background:**  
Prior to her current role at UPES, Assistant Professor Lama was engaged in doctoral research at the Centre of Development Studies, Trivandrum, where she focused on the Low Carbon Energy Industries in India.
