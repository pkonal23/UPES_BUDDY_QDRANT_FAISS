The content outlines a comprehensive overview of the B.Tech. Civil Engineering program with a specialization in Transportation Engineering offered by UPES School of Advanced Engineering. The program is designed to equip students with a robust foundation in civil engineering principles while emphasizing the critical area of transportation systems.

### Program Structure and Curriculum:
- **Semesters and Courses:** The program spans eight semesters, covering various subjects such as:
  - **Core Subjects:** Physics, Chemistry, Engineering Mathematics, and Mechanics.
  - **Specialized Courses:** Transportation Planning, Traffic Engineering, Environmental Engineering, Building Information Modelling, and Design of Concrete Structures.
  - **Laboratories and Practical Work:** Includes labs for Concrete Technology, Structural Engineering, and Geotechnical Engineering, among others.
  - **Research Projects:** Students undertake Research Projects I and II, applying theoretical knowledge to practical challenges.
  - **Capstone Projects:** In the final semesters, students engage in Capstone projects that integrate their learning with real-world applications.
  
### Learning Approach:
- The curriculum emphasizes a multidisciplinary approach, incorporating insights from psychology, economics, ecology, and sociology alongside advanced engineering concepts. Students learn to apply scientific methods for problem-solving in transportation contexts.
- The program utilizes virtual reality to enhance learning experiences, allowing students to visualize complex transportation scenarios and deepen their understanding of the subject matter.

### Skills Development:
- Students gain expertise in artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) techniques, enhancing their problem-solving skills for real-world transportation challenges.
- The program prepares graduates to be adaptable and job-ready, aligning with industry demands for modern infrastructure, including metros, high-speed trains, and expressways.

### Career Opportunities:
- Graduates can pursue diverse roles in transportation planning, infrastructure development, and sustainability projects. Opportunities exist in government agencies, consulting firms, and research organizations.
- The increasing focus on eco-friendly and technologically advanced solutions in transportation engineering underscores the growing demand for skilled professionals in this field.

### Industry Trends:
- The program addresses the rising need for affordable and efficient high-speed transport systems and the integration of computational and AI skills in civil engineering.

### Admission and Eligibility:
- Interested candidates must meet the minimum eligibility criteria, which includes a minimum of 50% marks in Classes X and XII, with at least 50% in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics in Class XII.
- Admission is based on performance in UPESEAT, JEE, Board Merit, SAT, or CUET.

### Contact Information:
- For further inquiries, prospective students can reach out via the contact form provided in the program details.
