Dr. Manan Pathak's teaching philosophy emphasizes diversity and inclusion, social consciousness, and active participation. He employs various engagement techniques, including case studies, role play, perspective taking, sub-group activities, and experiential learning, to immerse participants in real-world challenges faced by organizational leaders and managers. This approach encourages critical thinking, reflection, and the practical application of diverse strategies.

**Research Interests and Experience:**
Dr. Manan has significant experience in social development and research. He worked full-time with Professional Assistance for Development Action (PRADAN) from 2016 to 2019, where he honed his skills in participatory action research, project management, corporate social responsibility (CSR), social entrepreneurship, and organizational development (OD). He is also involved in consulting on topics related to OD, human resource development (HRD), diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI). Dr. Manan is currently a PhD scholar at the Indian Institute of Management Indore, where he has been since 2019.

**Academic Achievements:**
Dr. Manan has received several prestigious awards, including:
- Institute Gold Medal for Academic Excellence from Tata Institute of Social Sciences (TISS).
- Organization Development Seminar Gold Medal for excellence in Industrial/Organizational Psychology.
- Janardan C. Mehta Memorial Gold Medal for excellence in Humanities and Social Sciences from The Maharaja Sayajirao University of Baroda.
He has also been awarded multiple doctoral student scholarships by the Academy of Management and participated in the Wharton Global Faculty Development Program at the University of Pennsylvania.

**Teaching and Research Contributions:**
As an Assistant Professor, Dr. Manan teaches courses on Organizational Behaviour and Human Resource Management to MBA and BBA students. His research interests focus on the intersection of organizational practices and social change, and he has presented at leading international conferences such as the Academy of Management and the British Academy of Management. His research paper has been accepted for publication in the *Nonprofit and Voluntary Sector Quarterly*, an 'A' category journal according to the Australian Business Deans Council.
