Shakuntala Marndi is a distinguished textile engineer and apparel design professional with a Master's in Design from the National Institute of Design (NID) Gandhinagar and a Bachelor of Technology from Biju Patnaik University, Rourkela, Odisha. She has made significant contributions to the field of sustainable design, textile research, and traditional crafts, with notable projects including the 2017 Craft Studies in Kutch and Odisha, a uniform design for a Gujarat Public Sector Undertaking (PSU) in 2023, and the development of multisensory learning materials for the National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT).

In her academic role as an Assistant Professor at the School of Design, she has taught various courses such as Environmental Perception, Basic Materials and Methods, Combination of Materials, Smart Textiles, and Pattern Making, among others, at both NID and MSU Baroda. Her teaching philosophy emphasizes the recognition of individual student talents, advocating for project-based learning and personalized instruction to enhance engagement and effectiveness. Marndi is dedicated to empowering her students to take ownership of their education, develop critical thinking skills, and become responsible citizens within their communities.

Marndi has also been actively involved in scholarly activities, focusing on the study of natural textile materials (both plant and animal protein-based), their morphology, properties, and the circularity of these materials. She has contributed to Santali Wikipedia, documenting tribal crafts and textiles, and has undertaken craft documentation of traditional textiles of the Santals of Mayurbhanj.

In addition to her academic pursuits, since 2014, she has operated her own venture, Shakuntala Clothing, where she manages design, pattern making, and production. Her previous professional experience includes roles at Serene Design (2016-2019) focusing on community research and accessory design, as an Apparel Designer at SEWA in Ahmedabad, and as a Production Engineer at Texport Syndicate from 2006 to 2008.

Marndi is multilingual, fluent in Santali, Odia, English, and Hindi, which enhances her ability to connect with diverse communities and contribute to her field.
