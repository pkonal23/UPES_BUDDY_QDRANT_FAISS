Dr. Tauheed Alam is an accomplished academic and legal professional with extensive experience in both the legal industry and higher education. He has taught a wide range of law courses, including Law of Crimes, Jurisprudence, Constitutional Law, Media Law, Family Law, and more, demonstrating his expertise in various legal disciplines. His teaching approach emphasizes a student-centered learning environment that encourages critical thinking, intellectual growth, and ethical development. Dr. Alam actively engages students through discussions, case studies, and real-world examples, fostering a supportive atmosphere that respects diverse perspectives.

Dr. Alam began his career as a Legal Associate at Thomson Reuters from February 2012 to September 2014, where he gained valuable insights into the legal industry. He then transitioned to academia, serving as Guest Faculty at Jamia Millia Islamia in New Delhi during the 2016-2017 academic year. In July 2018, he joined Shri Ram Swarup Memorial University (SRMU) in Lucknow as an Assistant Professor, contributing significantly to legal education until his resignation in January 2020. Currently, he is an Assistant Professor at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES), where he continues to teach and conduct research, particularly in criminal law.

Dr. Alam holds a Ph.D. in Criminal Law and specializes in the comparative study of capital punishment within the Islamic and Indian legal systems. His research aims to explore the legal, ethical, and human rights dimensions of capital punishment, contributing to ongoing discussions in legal scholarship and policy.
