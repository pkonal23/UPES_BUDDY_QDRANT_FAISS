Abhijit Kumar is an Assistant Professor-SG in the School of Computer Science at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES), Dehradun, Uttarakhand, India, since July 2016. He is currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Computer Science Engineering (CSE) from IIT Patna and holds an M.Tech in CSE from MANIT Bhopal, India. His research interests primarily lie in Natural Language Processing, Remote Sensing, and Bioinformatics, with a strong focus on Machine Learning applications.

Abhijit has a rich academic background spanning 12 years, during which he has taught a variety of subjects related to computer science engineering. His teaching portfolio includes core courses such as Data Structures, Algorithm Design and Analysis, Formal Languages and Automata Theory, Compiler Designing, and Discrete Mathematics. He also teaches programming courses like Object-Oriented Programming, Python Programming, and Principles of Programming Languages, as well as research-oriented courses including Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing.

In addition to his teaching responsibilities, Abhijit has been actively involved in research and training, securing two patents and publishing one patent under his name. He has presented expert talks on his areas of expertise at various institutions, including IIITT Bhopal, JNCT Bhopal, and KV Dehradun. His commitment to fostering student engagement is reflected in his organization of coding challenges and hackathons.

Before joining UPES, Abhijit served as an Assistant Professor at Amity School of Engineering and Technology in New Delhi from 2011 to 2016, and prior to that, he worked in software development at Infosys Technology Ltd in Hyderabad. Throughout his academic career, he has held several administrative roles, including Placement Coordinator, EDUSAT Coordinator, Spoken Tutorial Coordinator, and member of various project committees. He has also organized numerous academic events, including UHackathons and international conferences like NGCT and ICMLDE.

Abhijit has received recognition for his scholarly work, including a Best Paper award and a seed fund project under the MEITY research initiative. He is associated with professional bodies such as the International Association of Engineers (IAENG) and the Universal Association of Computer and Electronics Engineers (UACEE). 

For further inquiries, Abhijit Kumar can be contacted via email at abhijit.kumar@ddn.upes.ac.in. He is also recognized as a Unique Awardee under the Visvesvaraya PhD Scheme for Electronics and IT (VISPHD-MEITY-2527).
