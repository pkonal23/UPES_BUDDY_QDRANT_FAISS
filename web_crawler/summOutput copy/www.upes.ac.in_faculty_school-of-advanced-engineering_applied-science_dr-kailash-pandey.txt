Dr. Kailash Pandey is an accomplished nuclear physicist and Assistant Professor (SG) at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES). Prior to his current role, he was associated with APJ Abdul Kalam Technical University in Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, India, where he served as an Assistant Professor. His previous academic affiliations include Galgotiaâ€™s College of Engineering and Technology and Ajay Kumar Garg Engineering College in Ghaziabad. Notably, he has also worked as a Senior Research Fellow on a BRNS-sponsored project focused on neutron-induced cross-section calculations relevant to advanced nuclear technologies. Dr. Pandey is recognized as a Certified Nuclear Security Personnel by the World Institute of Nuclear Security (WINS) in Vienna, Austria.

With over 15 years of teaching and research experience, Dr. Pandey specializes in theoretical nuclear physics, particularly in calculating nuclear cross-sections. His extensive expertise encompasses both theoretical and experimental nuclear physics, material science, and solar energy. He has taught a variety of undergraduate and postgraduate courses, which include Engineering Physics, Basic Electronics Engineering, Laser Systems and Applications, Nuclear and Particle Physics, Interaction of Radiation with Matter, Radiation Safety, Classical Mechanics, and various laboratory courses.

Dr. Pandey's research endeavors have been supported by grants, including one from BRNS for developing Vacuum Photodiode Detectors for vacuum ultraviolet detection in the ADITYA-U tokamak at the Institute for Plasma Research (IPR) in Gandhinagar, Gujarat. He is an active member of international organizations like WINS and the International Nuclear Security Education Network (INSEN) and holds life memberships in the Indian Association of Physics Teachers and the Indian Physics Association. His scholarly contributions include numerous research papers published in refereed journals, the development of educational content for universities, and authorship of several book chapters across diverse fields.

In terms of teaching philosophy, Dr. Pandey advocates for learner-centric approaches, aiming to foster an educational environment that adapts to future-oriented changes in teaching methodologies.
