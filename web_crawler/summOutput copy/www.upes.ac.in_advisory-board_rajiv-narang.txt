Rajiv Narang serves as the Director of the Process Economics Program at S&P Global, specifically within IHS Markit, and is currently located in New Delhi, India. With over 35 years of experience in the energy sector, Narang has worked with notable companies and consultancies including Bechtel, Fluor, Petrofac, ONGC, and GAIL. His professional journey has taken him to various countries, enhancing his global perspective in the field.

In his role, Narang leads the Process Economics Program for the Asia region, focusing on Techno-Economic studies that address significant industry topics. His areas of expertise include Coal Gasification, options for stakeholders regarding IMO 2020, Sulfur and Sulfuric Acid production, Lubricating Base Oils, Natural Gas Processing, and advancements in Carbon Capture technologies.

Narang has held leadership positions within professional organizations, serving as Secretary and Vice Chairman for the Northern India Chapter of the Indian Institute of Chemical Engineers. He is also recognized as a senior member of the American Institute of Chemical Engineers (AIChE), a Chartered Engineer in the UK, and a Fellow of the Institute of Chemical Engineers (IChemE) in the UK.

Regarding his academic background, Narang completed his undergraduate degree in Chemical Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology, Delhi, and also holds an MBA. He has contributed to the field through numerous technical papers published in various conferences and international journals.
