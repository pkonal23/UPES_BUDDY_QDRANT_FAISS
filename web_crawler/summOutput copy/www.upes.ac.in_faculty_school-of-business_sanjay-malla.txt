Professor Sanjay Malla is a seasoned academic and industry professional with over 25 years of experience in the Semiconductor and Computer Hardware sectors. He specializes in Operations Management, International Business, Cross-functional Team Leadership, and Corporate Strategy. Currently, he serves as a Professor of Practice at the School of Business, where he teaches Strategic Management courses to MBA, BBA, and Senior Executive students.

Prof. Malla holds an MBA with a specialization in Strategy and Marketing from the Indian Institute of Management Kozhikode (IIM-K) and a doctoral degree in Strategic Management from S P Jain School of Global Management in Sydney. His expertise extends to advanced data analytics, utilizing tools such as SPSS, ADANCO, and SmartPLS for higher-order data analytics and structural equation modeling (SEM).

An active contributor to scholarly activities, Prof. Malla has several publications in esteemed international journals and has presented research papers at various international conferences. He is a member of the Strategic Management Forum of India and has participated in review committees for organizations such as IEEE, SMF, and Emerald, among others.

Prof. Malla's teaching philosophy is student-centric, emphasizing deep engagement and authentic learning experiences in the classroom. He leverages his extensive industry experience to create real-world scenarios that enhance the educational experience, fostering a mindset in students that prepares them for challenges beyond their academic pursuits. He aims to instill in his students the ability to think critically about broader opportunities and realities in the business world.

Before joining the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES), Prof. Malla was the CEO of Toradex Systems India, a Swiss multinational corporation specializing in Embedded Systems. He has also held leadership roles as Country Manager at various multinational semiconductor companies in India.

His research interests include Organizational Development, the Post-M&A impact on companies, Organizational Justice, Leadership, Employee Commitment, Trust, and Strategic Management.
