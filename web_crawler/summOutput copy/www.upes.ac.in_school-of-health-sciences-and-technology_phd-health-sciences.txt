The PhD program at the UPES School of Health Sciences and Technology is designed to cultivate advanced research skills and knowledge in various health-related disciplines. The program's comprehensive curriculum emphasizes interdisciplinary subjects, including biotechnology, biomedical engineering, healthcare informatics, pharmaceutical sciences, and health policy. Students engage in rigorous coursework, seminars, and hands-on research projects, mentored by experienced faculty and industry experts, which prepares them to address complex healthcare challenges and contribute to technological advancements in healthcare.

**Eligibility Criteria:**
To apply for the PhD program, candidates must hold a Master's degree or an equivalent professional degree recognized by the relevant regulatory body. A minimum of 55% overall marks (or a grade 'B' on the UGC 7-point scale) is required. There is a provision for a 5% relaxation for candidates from SC/ST/OBC (non-creamy layer), differently-abled categories, or those who completed their Masterâ€™s degree before September 19, 1991. Additionally, degrees from foreign institutions must be accredited by an authorized Assessment and Accreditation Agency.

**Program Fees:**
The program's semester fee is INR 1 lakh, with a 75% waiver available for full-time students, reducing the fee to INR 25,000. There are no hostel accommodations provided for PhD scholars.

**Career Opportunities:**
Graduates of the program can pursue diverse career paths in academia, research institutes, the pharmaceutical and biotechnology industries, healthcare organizations, government agencies, and consulting firms. They can engage in advanced research, collaborate with experts, publish their findings, and contribute to innovative healthcare solutions.

**Specializations Offered:**
The program includes various specializations, such as:
- **Microbiology:** Focuses on microorganisms and their impact on health, covering medical, industrial, and environmental microbiology.
- **Food and Nutrition Sciences:** Involves the scientific examination of food, its components, and their effects on health, preparing students for roles in food safety, public health policies, and dietary interventions.
