A. S. Kiran Kumar, born on October 22, 1952, in Karnataka, holds an M.Sc. in Physics (Electronics) from Bangalore University and an M.Tech. in Physical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Science (IISC). He has made significant contributions to the Indian Space Programme, serving as the Vikram Sarabhai Professor at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in Bangalore and as a Member of the Space Commission for the Government of India. 

From 2015 to 2018, he held the position of Secretary in the Department of Space and Chairman of ISRO, where he was pivotal in advancing various sectors such as earth observation, communication, navigation, meteorology, and space science. His leadership facilitated the development of indigenous launch vehicles, ensuring reliable access to space. Notably, he has played a crucial role in developing over 50 Electro-Optical Imaging Sensors for spaceborne platforms, starting with the Bhaskara satellite in 1979, and was instrumental in the success of India's Chandrayaan-1 and Mars Orbiter Missions.
