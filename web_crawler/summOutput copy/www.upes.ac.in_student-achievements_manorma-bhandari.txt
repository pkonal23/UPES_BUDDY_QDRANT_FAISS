Ms. Manorma Bhandari, a Ph.D. Scholar in the Department of Pharmaceutical Sciences from the 2021-2024 batch, has achieved notable recognition by winning the 2nd Prize for Best E-Poster Presentation at the International Conference on Nanopharmaceuticals and Neurological Disorders (ICNAND2023). This prestigious event took place from February 2-4, 2023, at Indira Gandhi National Tribal University (IGNTU) in Amarkantak, Madhya Pradesh, India.

Her presentation focused on the research titled "Identifying the potential natural compounds against mGluR5 using in silico approach," which is part of her broader investigation into the potential of herbal medicines in addressing autism, specifically targeting synaptogenic pathways. Ms. Bhandari's work encompasses various aspects of drug discovery, including in silico methodologies, natural compound isolation, and both in vitro and in vivo screening processes.

Dr. Shubham Dwivedi served as her mentor throughout this research endeavor.
