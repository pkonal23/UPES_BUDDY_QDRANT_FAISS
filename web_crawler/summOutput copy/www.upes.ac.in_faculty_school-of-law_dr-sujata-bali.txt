Dr. Sujata Bali is a Senior Associate Professor at UPES School of Law in Dehradun, India, with a rich background in teaching, research, and administrative roles. Her primary areas of specialization include Corporate Governance and Banking laws, alongside a strong research interest in women's and children's rights, environmental issues, and techno-legal matters.

**Research Interests:**
Dr. Bali's research encompasses several critical areas, including:
- E-banking and the law
- Environmental Law
- Women's and children's rights
- Legal aspects of technology, including cyber security and privacy

She has authored notable publications, such as:
- "Life-skill courses in the Indian Legal Education Framework"
- "Anticipating Legal Issues associated with Cyber Security and Privacy"
- "Interweaving Life Skills through NEP 2020"

**Work Experience:**
Prior to her tenure at UPES, Dr. Bali was a Senior Research Fellow (UGC) at Himachal Pradesh University, where she completed her Ph.D. thesis on "Banking Frauds in India: Emerging Trends and Legal Challenges in the 21st Century." She has also taught at the Himachal Pradesh University Institute of Legal Studies.

**Teaching Philosophy:**
Dr. Bali employs a Socratic method in her teaching, emphasizing that true knowledge resides within the learner. She believes in guiding and mentoring students rather than merely imparting knowledge, viewing law as a natural aspect of human society.

**Courses Taught:**
Dr. Bali teaches various subjects, including:
- Law & Justice in a Globalized World
- Banking & Negotiable Instruments Law
- Corporate Governance
- Law of Torts and Consumer Protection
- Law of Contract
- Insolvency & Bankruptcy Law
- Online Commercial Arbitration
- Legal Methods & Legal Reasoning

**Achievements:**
- Recipient of the UGC Senior Research Fellowship for her doctoral work.
- Awarded the Best Faculty - Excellence Award by UPES in 2019 for her contributions to education.
- Engaged in a consultancy project with the National Law Institute University, Bhopal in 2021.
- Contributor to the expert report "Fuel Blending in India: Learnings and Way Forward" (2016).
