Dr. Susmay Nandi is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics within the School of Engineering at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES). He completed his Ph.D. at the National Institute of Technology (NIT) Meghalaya, where his research focused on the analysis of Magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) flows, specifically examining MHD fluid flow past various geometries with heat and mass transfer considerations. Dr. Nandi also holds an M.Sc. in Mathematics & Computing from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Guwahati, a B.Sc. (Honours) in Mathematics from the University of North Bengal, and a B.Ed. degree from the same university.

Dr. Nandi has an impressive academic record, having published 21 research papers, which have contributed to his h-index and i10-index, both standing at 9. His scholarly achievements include receiving the Instituteâ€™s Best Researcher Award from NIT Meghalaya in 2022 for the highest number of SCI publications during his Ph.D. He was also honored with the Best Paper award at the International Conference on Mathematics and Computation in 2021 and secured third place in oral presentations at NIT Meghalaya's 2nd Research Conclave in the same year.

In terms of teaching, Dr. Nandi covers several mathematics courses for undergraduate students, including Differential Equations, Engineering Mathematics II, Mathematics III, and Discrete Mathematics. His teaching philosophy emphasizes creating an engaging and inclusive learning environment that encourages critical thinking and problem-solving. He adopts a student-centric approach, accommodating diverse learning styles and backgrounds, and incorporates real-world examples to enhance active learning through discussions and hands-on projects.

Dr. Nandi's research interests are diverse, encompassing Boundary Layer Theory, Magnetohydrodynamics, Heat and Mass Transfer, Nanofluids, Hybrid Nanofluids, and Fluid Flow through various geometries. He is actively involved in academic responsibilities and has supervised undergraduate students in their minor projects, in addition to delivering invited lectures as a resource person at VIT Chennai.
