Ms. Shradha Baranwal is an Assistant Professor at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) with a robust background in law and academia. Prior to her current role, she served as an Assistant Professor at GLA University in Mathura and worked with various law firms in Delhi, focusing on real estate, banking, and aviation law. She has experience appearing in multiple courts in Delhi, including the High Court, the National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (NCDRC), the National Green Tribunal (NGT), the Debt Recovery Tribunal (DRT), and the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA) Court.

In her academic career, Ms. Shradha has taken on significant administrative roles, such as Faculty Convenor for Legal Aid, head of the Internship and Career Services Committee, and coordinator for the Mentorship program. She is also actively involved in social service, participating in various Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) events at the school level.

Her research interests cover a wide range of legal subjects, including law and religion, human rights, gender issues, judicial processes, constitutional law, cyber law, and environmental issues. Ms. Shradha has published articles on topics such as Female Genital Mutilation, consent in rape cases, criminal liability for environmental crimes, and gender justice in the Indian Constitution. Recently, she co-edited a book titled ‘Cyber Crimes: Issues Policy Regulation & Development’, published by Satyam Law International.

Ms. Shradha teaches various law courses, including Constitutional Law, Contract Law, Jurisprudence, Family Law, Labour Law, Right to Information, Environmental Law, Pleading and Drafting, and Law and Justice in a Globalized World. She has published papers in UGC care-listed journals and has been involved in numerous training programs and faculty development programs (FDPs) to keep abreast of evolving legal education needs.

Her academic qualifications include a law degree from the University of Delhi and an LL.M. from the National Law School of India University, Bangalore. She is UGC NET qualified and is currently pursuing her doctorate at Rajiv Gandhi National University of Law, Patiala.

Ms. Shradha's teaching philosophy emphasizes the importance of developing a legal discourse environment among students through innovative teaching methods, regular assignments, pre-reading requirements, and the integration of technology into learning. She believes these approaches foster critical thinking essential for legal education.

For further inquiries, Ms. Shradha can be contacted via email at sbaranwal@ddn.upes.ac.in. Her research interests include law and religion, environmental liability mechanisms, legal history, and gender issues.
