**B.Tech. Food Technology (Food Biotechnology) Program**

**Program Overview**

The B.Tech. Food Technology (Food Biotechnology) program at UPES School of Health Sciences and Technology equips students with the knowledge and skills necessary to harness advancements in biotechnology within the food industry. The program delves into the application of biological techniques and tools to enhance various stages of food production, processing, and distribution.

**Curriculum Highlights**

* Focus on Food Biotechnology
* Practical learning opportunities
* Collaboration with Department of Biotechnology and UPES School of Health Sciences & Technology
* Access to cutting-edge equipment and instruments for hands-on experience
* Emphasis on industry collaboration and career preparation

**Future Scope and Industry Trends**

The B.Tech. Food Technology (Food Biotechnology) program prepares graduates for a promising future in the food and biotechnology industries. Key trends include:

* Genetically Modified Organisms (GMOs)
* Nutrigenomics and Personalized Nutrition
* Microbial Fermentation
* Cultured Meat
* Food Safety and Traceability
* Biofortification
* Synthetic Biology
* Bio-based Food Packaging
* Food Waste Utilization
* AI and Data Analytics

**Career Opportunities**

Graduates of the program are well-positioned for careers as:

* Food technologists
* Food processing specialists
* Researchers
* Regulatory personnel
* Biotechnology professionals
* Research and development experts

**Placements**

UPES has an impressive track record of placements for graduates of the B.Tech. Food Technology (Food Biotechnology) program. The university's strong industry connections and dedicated placement cell facilitate lucrative job opportunities for students.
