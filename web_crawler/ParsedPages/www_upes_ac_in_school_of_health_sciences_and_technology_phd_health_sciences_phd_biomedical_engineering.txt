Program details
The Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering at UPES School of Health Science & Technology is a comprehensive program designed to equip students with advanced knowledge and research skills in the dynamic field of biomedical engineering. This program, available in both full-time and part-time formats, offers flexibility to accommodate the diverse needs of aspiring scholars. The duration of the program ranges from 3 to 6 years, ensuring that candidates have ample time to delve deeply into their chosen research areas.
To be eligible for admission, candidates must hold a B.Tech./B.E. or M.Tech. in engineering disciplines, or an M.Sc. in relevant subjects with a minimum of 60% marks or a 6.00 CGPA on a 10-point scale. In addition to academic qualifications, candidates should possess qualifications in GATE/CSIR/UGC NET/ICAR/ICMR/DST-INSPIRE fellowship. This stringent eligibility criteria ensures that admitted students are well-prepared for the rigorous academic and research demands of the program.
The research focus areas of the program encompass cutting-edge fields such as Biomaterials, Microfluidics, Lab-on-Chip technology, Biosensors, Targeted Drug Delivery, and Soft Robotics. Through a combination of advanced coursework and hands-on research experience, students are encouraged to explore innovative solutions to real-world challenges in biomedical engineering. The goal of the Ph.D. program is to foster a new generation of scholars who will contribute significantly to the advancement of knowledge and technology in the biomedical engineering domain. Graduates emerge from the program with a deep understanding of their chosen research areas and the skills needed to make impactful contributions to the rapidly evolving field of biomedical engineering.
Program Highlights
Engage in cutting-edge research across various biomedical engineering domains, fostering innovation and contributing to advancements in healthcare technology.
The Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering program fosters a holistic understanding of biomedical engineering through interdisciplinary research, bringing together experts from engineering, biology, medicine, and materials science.
Benefit from a program led by a team of highly qualified faculty actively involved in research, providing strong industry connections and mentorship.
Access advanced laboratories, research equipment, and resources, including prototyping and analytical techniques in the Central Instrumentation Center and the School of Health Sciences and Technology.
Leverage partnerships with industry leaders such as GE Healthcare and collaborations with reputed national/international universities, providing exposure to real-world industrial challenges.
Collaborate with researchers worldwide, enhance your global perspective, and broaden research horizons through seminars, workshops, guest lectures, and opportunities to present at national and international conferences.
PEO / PSO
The Ph.D. program in Biomedical Engineering at UPES School of Health Science & Technology aims to cultivate scholars who will contribute significantly to the advancement of biomedical research and technology. The program's education objectives are multifaceted, seeking to foster a deep understanding of cutting-edge theories and methodologies in biomedical engineering while instilling a commitment to ethical and responsible research practices. Graduates are expected to demonstrate proficiency in interdisciplinary collaboration, critical thinking, and problem-solving, equipping them to address complex challenges in healthcare and technology. Furthermore, the program emphasizes effective communication skills, preparing scholars to disseminate their research findings and contribute to the broader scientific community. Ultimately, the education objectives aspire to produce Ph.D. graduates who are poised to make impactful contributions to the field, whether in academia, industry, or healthcare settings.
Future Scope / Industry Trends
A Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering from UPES Dehradun equips individuals with advanced knowledge at the intersection of engineering, biology, and medicine. Future prospects for graduates are promising, driven by key trends. Personalized medicine, emphasizing genetic-based diagnostics and therapies, continues to grow. Biomedical engineers are pivotal in the rise of microfluidics-enabled medical devices and wearables. Artificial intelligence and machine learning applications in healthcare, including diagnostics and drug discovery, create a demand for engineers skilled in these areas. Tissue engineering and regenerative medicine, biomechanics, neuroengineering, nanotechnology, and biopharmaceuticals are expanding fields. Addressing global health challenges, environmental health, bioinformatics, interdisciplinary collaboration, and expertise in policy and regulatory affairs are emerging opportunities. Biomedical engineers will play a crucial role in developing innovative solutions for healthcare access and outcomes globally. The interdisciplinary nature of the field, coupled with ongoing technological advancements, ensures diverse career opportunities in academia, research, healthcare, pharmaceuticals, government, and entrepreneurial ventures for Ph.D. graduates, driving innovation and enhancing healthcare outcomes.
Career Opportunities
A Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering from UPES opens doors to a myriad of career paths across various sectors due to the field's multidisciplinary nature. Graduates can embark on diverse roles such as academia, becoming professors or researchers in universities, research institutions, and colleges. They can also pursue opportunities as Biomedical Research Scientists, delving into R&D in pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, or medical devices. Clinical Engineers ensure the functionality of medical equipment in healthcare settings, while Regulatory Affairs Specialists navigate regulatory complexities. Graduates may also venture into entrepreneurship, biomechanics, data science, healthcare IT, quality assurance, patent examination, global health, government agencies, sales and marketing, and clinical trial management. The dynamic landscape of biomedical engineering promises impactful and rewarding careers tailored to individual research focus and expertise.

They can also pursue opportunities as Biomedical Research Scientists, delving into R&D in pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, or medical devices. Clinical Engineers ensure the functionality of medical equipment in healthcare settings, while Regulatory Affairs Specialists navigate regulatory complexities. Graduates may also venture into entrepreneurship, biomechanics, data science, healthcare IT, quality assurance, patent examination, global health, government agencies, sales and marketing, and clinical trial management. The dynamic landscape of biomedical engineering promises impactful and rewarding careers tailored to individual research focus and expertise.
