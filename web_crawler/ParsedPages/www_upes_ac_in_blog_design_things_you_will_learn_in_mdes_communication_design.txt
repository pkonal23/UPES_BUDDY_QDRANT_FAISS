Things You Will Learn In M.Des. Communication Design Course
UPES Editorial Team
Published 12/07/2024
Are you passionate about visual storytelling and eager to make your mark in the world of design? An M.Des. in Communication Design might be just what you're looking for! This exciting postgraduate program equips you with the skills and knowledge to become a master communicator through various visual mediums. Let's dive into the fascinating world of communication design and explore what you'll learn during this transformative course.
What is Communication Design?
Before we jump into the specifics, let's clarify what communication design actually means. Communication design is the art and science of crafting visual messages that inform, persuade, or inspire audiences. It's a multidisciplinary field that combines elements of graphic design, user experience (UX) design, branding, and more.
Key Areas You'll Master in an M.Des. Communication Design Program
Advanced Visual Design Techniques
You'll dive deep into:
Color theory and its psychological impact
Typography mastery for print and digital media
Layout principles for various platforms
Creating visual hierarchies that guide the viewer's eye
These skills form the foundation of effective visual communication, enabling you to create designs that not only look stunning but also convey messages clearly and efficiently.
User Experience (UX) Design
In today's digital-first world, understanding user experience is crucial. You'll learn:
User research methodologies
Creating user personas and journey maps
Wireframing and prototyping techniques
Usability testing and iterative design processes
This knowledge will empower you to create intuitive, user-friendly designs that enhance digital interactions and solve real-world problems.
Digital and Interactive Media
The digital realm offers endless possibilities for communication designers. Your coursework will cover:
Responsive web design principles
Mobile app interface design
Interactive storytelling techniques
Basic coding skills for designers
By mastering these digital tools, you'll be equipped to create engaging, interactive experiences across various platforms.
Animation and Motion Graphics
Bring your designs to life with animation skills:
2D and 3D animation techniques
Motion graphics for video and web
Character design and development
Storyboarding and visual storytelling
These skills will allow you to create dynamic content that captures attention and conveys complex ideas in engaging ways.
Design Thinking and Problem-Solving
At its core, communication design is about solving problems creatively. You'll learn:
Design thinking methodologies
Creative problem-solving techniques
Innovative ideation strategies
Client communication and project management skills
These abilities will help you approach design challenges with confidence and deliver solutions that meet both aesthetic and functional requirements.
Career Paths After Your M.Des. Communication Design
Wondering where this degree can take you? Here are some thrilling career opportunities you could pursue:
Graphic Designer
As a graphic designer, you'll create visual concepts using computer software or by hand to communicate ideas that inspire, inform, or captivate consumers. You might work on a variety of products, including:
Advertisements
Magazines
Corporate reports
Packaging design
UX Designer
UX designers focus on creating meaningful and relevant experiences for users. In this role, you might:
Conduct user research
Create wireframes and prototypes
Design user interfaces
Collaborate with developers to implement designs
Art Director
As an art director, you'll be responsible for the visual style and images in magazines, newspapers, product packaging, or movie and television productions. Your duties might include:
Determining the overall visual style of a project
Managing design teams
Reviewing and approving designs, artwork, photography, and graphics
Presenting designs to clients for approval
Animator
Animators create multiple images, known as frames, which when sequenced together create an illusion of movement called animation. As an animator, you might:
Create 2D or 3D animations for films, video games, or advertisements
Develop storyboards that map out key scenes in an animation
Use various animation and editing software to bring characters and stories to life
Is an M. Des. in Communication Design Right for You?
If you're passionate about visual communication, creativity, and using design to solve problems, this could be the perfect program for you. You'll graduate with a versatile skill set that's in high demand across industries, from tech startups to global media companies.
Take the Next Step in Your Design Journey
Ready to transform your creativity into a rewarding career? The University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) offers a comprehensive M. Des. Communication Design program that can help you achieve your goals.
Don't let this opportunity pass you by!

today to learn more about their M. Des. Communication Design program and
