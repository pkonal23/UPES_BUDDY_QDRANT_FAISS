Program details
The Ph.D. in Management program at UPES School of Business stands out with its focus on three distinct specialisations: Energy Management, General Management, and Transportation Management. This program caters to individuals aspiring to make substantial impacts in the management domain, with a specific emphasis on energy resources, general business practices, and transportation systems. The curriculum is meticulously crafted to blend advanced coursework, comprehensive research methodologies, and practical projects to provide students with a holistic understanding of the intricacies and advancements within their chosen specialisation.
Participants in the program will delve into a curriculum that combines theoretical knowledge with hands-on experience, allowing them to cultivate a profound expertise in their specific field of interest. The Energy Management specialisation equips students to navigate the dynamic landscape of energy resources, addressing contemporary challenges and fostering sustainable practices. General Management, on the other hand, provides a broad perspective on business practices, encouraging students to explore and innovate across various organizational facets. The Transportation Management specialisation focuses on the intricate dynamics of transportation systems, preparing scholars to contribute meaningfully to the efficiency and sustainability of global logistics.
The program's unique strength lies in its commitment to fostering both depth and breadth of knowledge, empowering candidates to become thought leaders and change-makers in their chosen management arena. Aspiring doctoral candidates will engage with renowned faculty members, cutting-edge research facilities, and a collaborative learning environment to further enhance their academic and professional journey. Through this Ph.D. in Management program, UPES School of Business aims to produce graduates who not only contribute significantly to the academic discourse but also bring valuable insights and solutions to real-world challenges in energy, business, and transportation management.
Program Highlights
The Ph.D. in Management program promotes interdisciplinary research, fostering a comprehensive understanding of the interconnected realms of economics, management, energy, and transportation, equipping scholars with a holistic perspective.
The Ph.D. in Management program collaborates with industry partners ensure that our research is not only academically rigorous but also relevant to contemporary business challenges, providing students with valuable insights and enhancing the practical applicability of their work.
UPES boasts state-of-the-art research facilities, including dedicated labs and extensive databases, empowering scholars to conduct cutting-edge research and contribute significantly to the advancement of knowledge in management.
Opportunities for international collaborations, conferences, and exchange programs enrich the academic experience, exposing scholars to global perspectives and best practices, preparing them for leadership roles in a diverse and interconnected business environment.
Our distinguished faculty, with a strong publication record in prestigious journals, including ABDC A*, ABDC A, and Scopus Q1, provides invaluable mentorship to Ph.D. candidates, enriching their research experience and ensuring academic excellence.
We actively support scholars in publishing their research in reputable journals, particularly those classified as ABDC A*, ABDC A, and Scopus Q1. Presenting findings at conferences enhances the visibility and impact of their research, contributing to the global academic discourse and establishing them as thought leaders in their field.
PEO / PSO
The Ph.D. in Management program at UPES School of Business aims to cultivate scholars and researchers who will contribute significantly to the field of management. The program's education objectives encompass the development of advanced expertise in theoretical and methodological approaches to management research, fostering critical thinking and analytical skills. Graduates are expected to demonstrate a deep understanding of contemporary management issues, engage in rigorous and impactful research, and contribute to the academic and practical advancements in the business domain. Additionally, the program emphasizes the cultivation of effective communication and leadership skills, preparing scholars to disseminate their findings and assume leadership roles in academia, industry, and policy-making. Overall, the Ph.D. program at UPES School of Business is designed to produce graduates who can make meaningful contributions to the academic discipline and address real-world challenges in the dynamic field of management.
Future Scope / Industry Trends
A Ph.D. in Management offers diverse career prospects in academia, corporate research, consulting, government, non-profit organizations, entrepreneurship, human resources, organizational development, operations, supply chain management, finance, investment, and technology. In academia, graduates can excel as professors, lecturers, or researchers, contributing to academic literature and mentoring future business leaders. Corporate research positions involve analyzing market trends and developing innovative management theories. Consulting firms seek Ph.D. holders for their analytical skills in areas like strategy and human resources. Opportunities also exist in government and non-profits for contributions to policymaking and economic development. Entrepreneurship allows for the application of research in innovative businesses, while roles in human resources focus on improving workplace dynamics. Supply chain optimization, financial analysis, and technology-driven industry management are other potential paths. The broad future scope of Ph.D. in Management reflects the increasing demand for professionals capable of navigating complex business environments and implementing effective management strategies, driven by their ability to conduct rigorous research and solve problems. This skill set remains highly valued across industries, ensuring continued demand in the evolving landscape of business and organizations.
Career Opportunities
The Ph.D. in Management program at UPES School of Business opens doors to a plethora of rewarding career opportunities. Graduates are equipped with advanced research and analytical skills, positioning them as thought leaders in academia and industry. With a focus on contemporary management issues, the program prepares scholars to contribute valuable insights to the business landscape. Alumni find opportunities in prestigious academic institutions as faculty members, driving research and shaping future business leaders. Additionally, the program nurtures entrepreneurship, empowering graduates to embark on ventures or consultancies. The industry relevance of the curriculum ensures that Ph.D. holders are sought after by corporate entities for leadership roles and strategic decision-making positions. Overall, the program not only fosters academic excellence but also cultivates professionals who can drive innovation, contribute to policy-making, and excel in various leadership capacities across diverse sectors.

Additionally, the program emphasizes the cultivation of effective communication and leadership skills, preparing scholars to disseminate their findings and assume leadership roles in academia, industry, and policy-making. Overall, the Ph.D. program at UPES School of Business is designed to produce graduates who can make meaningful contributions to the academic discipline and address real-world challenges in the dynamic field of management.

Future Scope / Industry Trends
A Ph.D. in Management offers diverse career prospects in academia, corporate research, consulting, government, non-profit organizations, entrepreneurship, human resources, organizational development, operations, supply chain management, finance, investment, and technology. In academia, graduates can excel as professors, lecturers, or researchers, contributing to academic literature and mentoring future business leaders. Corporate research positions involve analyzing market trends and developing innovative management theories. Consulting firms seek Ph.D. holders for their analytical skills in areas like strategy and human resources. Opportunities also exist in government and non-profits for contributions to policymaking and economic development. Entrepreneurship allows for the application of research in innovative businesses, while roles in human resources focus on improving workplace dynamics. Supply chain optimization, financial analysis, and technology-driven industry management are other potential paths. The broad future scope of Ph.D. in Management reflects the increasing demand for professionals capable of navigating complex business environments and implementing effective management strategies, driven by their ability to conduct rigorous research and solve problems. This skill set remains highly valued across industries, ensuring continued demand in the evolving landscape of business and organizations.
