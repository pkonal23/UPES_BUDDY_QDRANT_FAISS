Program details
The Ph.D. program in Aerospace Engineering at UPES School of Advanced Engineering is a comprehensive and dynamic academic experience designed to equip students with both theoretical knowledge and practical skills in the field. Spanning a duration of 3 to 4 years, the program immerses students in a journey of exploration and mastery. Core areas of study include computational fluid dynamics (CFD), materials science, and propulsion, ensuring a well-rounded understanding of aerospace engineering.
A key feature of the program is its emphasis on original research, culminating in the completion of a dissertation. This research component is a significant aspect of the Ph.D. journey, challenging students to contribute to the advancement of the aerospace field through innovative and impactful research. The curriculum is structured to facilitate this research process, providing students with the necessary tools and guidance to undertake in-depth investigations.
UPES is committed to providing a stimulating research environment, offering access to cutting-edge facilities where doctoral candidates collaborate with both seasoned experts and industry leaders. The program promotes active engagement with the wider aerospace community through participation in conferences and seminars. This approach not only enriches the academic experience but also ensures that scholars are well-prepared to emerge as leaders in aerospace engineering. Graduates of the program are poised to address the industry's most critical challenges and make meaningful contributions to technological advancements in the field. The Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering program at UPES is a gateway for individuals passionate about pushing the boundaries of aerospace engineering and making a lasting impact on the industry.
Program Highlights
The Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering program focuses on niche areas such as Computational Fluid Dynamics, Aerodynamics, Aeronautics, Astronautics, Space Technology, Defense, Military Aerospace, Aerospace Manufacturing, lightweight materials, composites, and propulsion systems.
The Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering program is designed for completion within 3 to 4 years, striking a balance between comprehensive coursework and independent research.
Students benefit from access to modern facilities, providing them with the tools and resources necessary for advanced aerospace research.
Renowned faculty members provide mentorship and guidance, ensuring scholars receive expert support throughout their research journey.
Students actively engage in contemporary aerospace projects, gaining hands-on experience and staying abreast of the latest industry developments.
Scholars are encouraged to present their research at industry conferences, fostering networking opportunities and elevating their research profile in the aerospace community.
PEO / PSO
The Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering program at UPES School of Advanced Engineering aims to cultivate highly skilled and innovative researchers in the field. The program's education objectives revolve around fostering advanced knowledge in aerospace engineering, instilling a deep understanding of cutting-edge technologies, and nurturing the ability to conduct independent and impactful research. Graduates of this program are expected to contribute significantly to the advancement of aerospace engineering through scholarly publications, technological innovations, and by addressing critical challenges in the industry. Furthermore, the program emphasizes the development of effective communication and leadership skills, ensuring that graduates are well-equipped to disseminate their research findings and lead in academic, research, and industrial settings. Overall, the Ph.D. program seeks to produce aerospace engineers with the expertise and leadership qualities needed to make substantial contributions to the ever-evolving field of aerospace engineering.
Future Scope / Industry Trends
The future for Ph.D. graduates in Aerospace Engineering is promising and diverse, driven by technological advancements and a rising demand for innovative solutions in air and space travel. Emerging trends include the development of sustainable aviation technologies, autonomous and unmanned aerial vehicles, and deep space exploration initiatives. To address environmental concerns, there is a notable shift towards electric propulsion systems, green fuels, and advanced lightweight materials. The integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning in aerospace systems is creating opportunities for smart navigation, predictive maintenance, and enhanced safety measures. As commercial spaceflight gains momentum, Ph.D. scholars specialising in cutting-edge aerospace research will be positioned at the forefront of an industry experiencing transformative growth. Opportunities abound in academia, private sector space enterprises, and government space agencies, making the Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering a key qualification for those seeking to contribute to and lead advancements in this dynamic field.
Career Opportunities
Ph.D. holders in Aerospace Engineering can explore diverse career paths spanning academia, industry, and government. In academia, they can excel as professors, researchers, or contributors to advanced research labs. Industry options include leadership roles in R&D departments, specialising in aircraft design, propulsion systems, or materials engineering for major aerospace corporations, or pioneering innovations in start-ups. Government agencies such as ISRO, CSIR, or DRDO offer roles in spacecraft development, policy-making, and national defense projects. Furthermore, the growing field of commercial spaceflight presents promising opportunities with companies like SpaceX or Blue Origin, focusing on spacecraft design, systems engineering, and mission planning and analysis.
Placements
The Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering program at UPES School of Advanced Engineering provides a cutting-edge platform for aspiring researchers to delve into the realms of aerospace technology and innovation. With a strong emphasis on interdisciplinary research and hands-on experience, the program equips candidates with the skills and knowledge necessary to address complex challenges in the aerospace industry. Leveraging state-of-the-art facilities and a faculty comprising seasoned experts, students engage in impactful research that spans aerodynamics, propulsion, materials, and space systems. The program's robust industry connect ensures that graduates are well-prepared for diverse career opportunities in academia, research institutions, and the thriving aerospace sector. UPES School of Advanced Engineering's commitment to fostering a dynamic learning environment underscores its reputation as a premier institution for those aspiring to make significant contributions to the field of aerospace engineering.

Emerging trends include the development of sustainable aviation technologies, autonomous and unmanned aerial vehicles, and deep space exploration initiatives. To address environmental concerns, there is a notable shift towards electric propulsion systems, green fuels, and advanced lightweight materials. The integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning in aerospace systems is creating opportunities for smart navigation, predictive maintenance, and enhanced safety measures. As commercial spaceflight gains momentum, Ph.D. scholars specialising in cutting-edge aerospace research will be positioned at the forefront of an industry experiencing transformative growth. Opportunities abound in academia, private sector space enterprises, and government space agencies, making the Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering a key qualification for those seeking to contribute to and lead advancements in this dynamic field.

Career Opportunities
Ph.D. holders in Aerospace Engineering can explore diverse career paths spanning academia, industry, and government. In academia, they can excel as professors, researchers, or contributors to advanced research labs. Industry options include leadership roles in R&D departments, specialising in aircraft design, propulsion systems, or materials engineering for major aerospace corporations, or pioneering innovations in start-ups. Government agencies such as ISRO, CSIR, or DRDO offer roles in spacecraft development, policy-making, and national defense projects. Furthermore, the growing field of commercial spaceflight presents promising opportunities with companies like SpaceX or Blue Origin, focusing on spacecraft design, systems engineering, and mission planning and analysis.

Placements
The Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering program at UPES School of Advanced Engineering provides a cutting-edge platform for aspiring researchers to delve into the realms of aerospace technology and innovation. With a strong emphasis on interdisciplinary research and hands-on experience, the program equips candidates with the skills and knowledge necessary to address complex challenges in the aerospace industry. Leveraging state-of-the-art facilities and a faculty comprising seasoned experts, students engage in impactful research that spans aerodynamics, propulsion, materials, and space systems. The program's robust industry connect ensures that graduates are well-prepared for diverse career opportunities in academia, research institutions, and the thriving aerospace sector. UPES School of Advanced Engineering's commitment to fostering a dynamic learning environment underscores its reputation as a premier institution for those aspiring to make significant contributions to the field of aerospace engineering.
