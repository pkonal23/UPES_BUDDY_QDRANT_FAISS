**Program Overview: Bachelor of Technology in Automotive Engineering (E-Mobility) at University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) School of Advanced Engineering**

University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) School of Advanced Engineering offers a specialized Bachelor of Technology program in Automotive Engineering with a focus on E-Mobility, equipping students to develop sustainable and eco-friendly mobility solutions. This program is designed to empower students with the knowledge and skills necessary to meet the evolving challenges faced by automotive engineers, including adhering to strict emission regulations, ensuring vehicle safety and performance, and optimizing mobility costs.

Throughout their academic journey, students will delve into various vehicle technologies, encompassing conventional, electric, and hybrid systems. They will explore the economic and sustainability aspects of electric vehicles, gaining insights into the transition towards a greener transportation ecosystem. The curriculum also covers certification and homologation processes to ensure compliance with industry standards and legal requirements.

A unique feature of this program is the integration of machine learning concepts applied to vehicles. This enables students to explore the emerging field of smart and autonomous transportation, paving the way for the future of e-mobility. With a dedicated focus on electric mobility, students will also study the infrastructure and technology supporting electric cars, buses, trains, and bikes. By developing expertise in these areas, graduates will contribute to reducing global dependence on fossil fuels and curbing emissions from the transportation sector, ultimately fostering a more sustainable and environmentally responsible future.

**Future Scope and Industry Trends:**

The Bachelor of Technology in Automotive Engineering (E-Mobility) program offers a promising future due to several significant trends shaping the global automotive industry. Skilled automotive engineers are in high demand to design, develop, and maintain innovative vehicles that align with evolving customer demands and regulatory requirements. The shift towards electric and autonomous vehicles is a key trend, necessitating expertise in battery technology, power electronics, electric drivetrain systems, sensors, artificial intelligence, and control systems. Connected car technology is also on the rise, requiring knowledge of embedded systems, software development, and cybersecurity. Furthermore, sustainability efforts are driving the exploration of lightweight materials and alternative fuels, such as carbon fiber composites, hydrogen, and biofuels. The integration of advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) is transforming vehicle safety, while smart manufacturing principles like robotics and data analytics are revolutionizing the automotive manufacturing process. Aspiring automotive engineers must continuously update their skills to succeed in this dynamic industry.

**Eligibility Criteria:**
* Minimum 50% marks in Classes X and XII, with 50% in PCM (Physics/Chemistry and Mathematics) in Class XII

**Selection Criteria:**
* Selection criteria include performance in University of Petroleum and Energy Studies Engineering Aptitude Test (UPESEAT)/Joint Entrance Examination (JEE)/Board Merit/Scholastic Assessment Test (SAT)/Common University Entrance Test (CUET)
