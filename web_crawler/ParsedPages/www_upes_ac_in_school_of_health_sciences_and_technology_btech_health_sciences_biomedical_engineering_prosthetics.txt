**Program Overview**

UPES School of Health Sciences and Technology's Bachelor of Technology in Biomedical Engineering (Prosthetics) program is an innovative and cutting-edge program focused on the design, development, and implementation of advanced prosthetic devices and implants. The program's goal is to equip students with a strong foundation in engineering principles, anatomy, physiology, and biomechanics, enabling them to create and improve prosthetic solutions for individuals with limb loss, disabilities, or injuries.

**Program Benefits**

Through a combination of theoretical knowledge and practical experience, students will gain skills in analyzing patient needs and developing personalized prosthetic solutions that mimic the form and function of natural body parts. The curriculum covers a wide range of topics, including material science, biomechanics, robotics, computer-aided design (CAD), and human-machine interfaces.

**Collaboration with Industry**

The program collaborates with GE Healthcare, providing students with real-world experience, access to industry expertise, and cutting-edge technology. This collaboration enhances problem-solving and critical thinking skills. Students have access to advanced tools and research opportunities, allowing them to contribute to advancements in biomedical engineering.

**Career Opportunities**

Upon graduation, students are well-prepared for careers in the biomedical industry, research institutions, or healthcare organizations, where they can contribute to the development of life-changing prosthetic devices and implants. Graduates can pursue roles as Prosthetics Engineers, Biomedical Engineers, Research Scientists, or Product Development Managers.

**Placements**

UPES's strong industry connections ensure access to top-notch companies and organizations for placements. Graduates from this program have an excellent track record of securing lucrative positions in reputed companies, reaffirming UPES's reputation for biomedical engineering education and job placements.

**Eligibility Criteria**

To be eligible for the Bachelor of Technology in Biomedical Engineering (Prosthetics) program, students must have a minimum of 50% marks in X & XII with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Mathematics, and English as a major subject in XII.

**Selection Criteria**

Selection for the program is based on the individual's performance in UPESEAT / JEE Mains / Board Merit / SAT/ CUET.
