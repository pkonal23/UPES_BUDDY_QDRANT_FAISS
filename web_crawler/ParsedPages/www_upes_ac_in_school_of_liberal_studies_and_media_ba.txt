BA - Course Overview

The UPES School of Liberal Studies and Media equips students with cutting-edge media literacy for the fast-paced media industry. Media studies 3 years BA course offers insights into content and digital marketing for those interested in mainstream media. Personal and public relations enthusiasts can take a course.
Our industry-aligned curriculum, state-of-the-art studios, and connections with renowned media specialists offer students an extensive curriculum they can pursue. Additionally, students can choose subject-specific specialisations alongside their main subject.

Specialisations
The UPES School of Liberal Studies and Media
BA in Journalism and Mass Communication (Digital)
course encompasses print, digital, and broadcast media. Students report, edit, and produce news. Critical media analysis is also covered.
Digital, radio, and television broadcasting comprise the core topics of the
BA in Digital and Mass Media (Broadcasting)
degree. Students learn to develop content for various media outlets and the industry's business and legal aspects. Modern studios and production facilities are also employed for practical instruction.
The initial two semesters of the program are standardised across all specialisations, whereas from the third semester onwards, the curriculum diverges based on the chosen field of specialisation.

Eligibility Criteria
A UPES Bachelor of Arts degree is an ideal start to college and a fulfilling arts career. UPES University was the first Indian university to obtain the 2020 QS Five Stars Global Rating for employability due to its top-notch faculty and facilities. UPES BA entrance is simple. Applicants must submit an online application with photographs. Shortlisted applicants are interviewed. Candidates can choose their personal interview locations from the collective list. Successful candidates in the interview round are notified and offered admission to the BA program at UPES.
Minimum 50% marks in class X and XII, any Stream.

Scope and Highlights
The Bachelor of Arts (BA) program in Journalism and Mass Communication (Digital) and Bachelor of Arts (BA) Digital and Mass Media (Broadcasting) at the UPES School of Liberal Studies and Media includes journalism, film studies, advertising, and public relations, among other media-related subjects. This program prepares students for careers in the journalism, advertising, public relations, film and television production industries, among others. Through internships, real-world projects, and partnerships with industry professionals, the curriculum emphasises practical experience. Seminars, workshops, and guest lectures offer students invaluable networking opportunities. There are courses in digital media, visual communication, media law and ethics, media management, and media research. In addition, students can choose electives in photography, radio, and event management based on their interests and career goals. Through study abroad programs and global internships, students cultivate a global perspective and sensitivity to other cultures.
Career Opportunities
The BA program at UPES School of Liberal Studies and Media prepares students for diverse careers in the media industry, including:
Journalist:
Graduates can work as reporters, editors, and writers in newspapers, magazines, TV, and online news portals.
Advertising and Public Relations:
Graduates can pursue roles as copywriters, media planners, and PR specialists in agencies, firms, and corporate communication departments.
Filmmaker and TV Producer:
Graduates can work as filmmakers, screenwriters, directors, producers, and editors in the film and TV industries.
Digital Media Specialist:
Graduates can excel as digital media specialists, social media managers, and content creators for websites, social media, and mobile apps.
Media Researcher:
Graduates can work as researchers, data analysts, and market researchers in media organisations, agencies, and research firms.
Media Educator:
Graduates can pursue careers as educators, trainers, and consultants in academic institutions, media organisations, and training centres.
Innovative Teaching and Learning
We at the School of Liberal Studies and Media believe in experiential teaching and learning and strive to imbibe creative skills in students. The following practices are in place to improve the quality of Teaching-Learning and student experience:
Design and Review of individual Course Plans at the beginning of session
Course Completion Report (CCR)
Academic Planning & Monitoring
Real-world/Project based assignments
Encouraging Advanced Learner
Slow Learners Support
ICT-enabled Classroom (sound system, mic and projector)
Guest lectures from industry experts
Certification Courses
Professional Software Training (PST) and Certification
NPTEL lectures
Participation in competitive events (In-house/National/International).
Participation in Conferences/Seminars/Workshops (National/International).
Continuous Assessment.

Scope and Highlights
The Bachelor of Arts (BA) program in Journalism and Mass Communication (Digital) and Bachelor of Arts (BA) Digital and Mass Media (Broadcasting) at the UPES School of Liberal Studies and Media includes journalism, film studies, advertising, and public relations, among other media-related subjects. This program prepares students for careers in the journalism, advertising, public relations, film and television production industries, among others. Through internships, real-world projects, and partnerships with industry professionals, the curriculum emphasises practical experience. Seminars, workshops, and guest lectures offer students invaluable networking opportunities. There are courses in digital media, visual communication, media law and ethics, media management, and media research. In addition, students can choose electives in photography, radio, and event management based on their interests and career goals. Through study abroad programs and global internships, students cultivate a global perspective and sensitivity to other cultures.

Career Opportunities
The BA program at UPES School of Liberal Studies and Media prepares students for diverse careers in the media industry, including:
Journalist:
Graduates can work as reporters, editors, and writers in newspapers, magazines, TV, and online news portals.
Advertising and Public Relations:
Graduates can pursue roles as copywriters, media planners, and PR specialists in agencies, firms, and corporate communication departments.
Filmmaker and TV Producer:
Graduates can work as filmmakers, screenwriters, directors, producers, and editors in the film and TV industries.
Digital Media Specialist:
Graduates can excel as digital media specialists, social media managers, and content creators for websites, social media, and mobile apps.
Media Researcher:
Graduates can work as researchers, data analysts, and market researchers in media organisations, agencies, and research firms.
Media Educator:
Graduates can pursue careers as educators, trainers, and consultants in academic institutions, media organisations, and training centres.

FAQs
What is BA Journalism and Mass Communication (Digital) and BA Digital and Mass Media (Broadcasting) Program?
At UPES School of Liberal Studies and Media, the BA programme in Journalism & Mass Communication and Digital & Mass Media (Broadcasting) incorporates news reporting, broadcasting, and digital media production. Students learn about journalism, broadcasting, advertising, public relations, and digital media vocations. Internships, projects, and industrial collaborations emphasise theory and practice in the curriculum. Professional guest lectures, workshops, and seminars give students networking possibilities.
What is the eligibility criteria for the BA Program?
Minimum 50% marks in class X and XII any Stream.

What are the career opportunities after doing a BA in Journalism and Mass Communication (Digital) and BA Digital and Mass Media (Broadcasting)Program?
UPES School of Liberal Studies and Media BA graduates in Journalism and Mass Communication and Digital and Mass Media (Broadcasting) benefit from numerous media employment opportunities. Students acquire the skills, information, and hands-on experience they need to succeed in journalism, radio, advertising, or public relations. Internships, initiatives, and industry collaborations offer networking and real-world experience, giving graduates an edge in the employment market. The program prepares graduates for success in the dynamic media sector and associated fields.
What is the average salary after the BA Journalism and Mass Communication (Digital) and BA Digital and Mass Media (Broadcasting) Program?
The average pay after completing the BA Programme in Journalism & Mass Communication and Digital & Mass Media (Broadcasting) at UPES School of Liberal Studies and Media depends on work type, location, and organisation. Mid-level journalism and digital media jobs can pay INR 50,000–80,000 per month, whereas entry-level jobs pay INR 20,000–30,000. However, high-level broadcasting and advertising jobs might pay INR 1,00,000 per month or more. Experience and certifications might increase salaries. These values are averages and might vary greatly depending on qualifications, work function, and other factors.
Are there hostel facilities for boys and girls getting admission for the course?
Yes, hostel facility is provided by the University. Residential blocks have been earmarked for limited on-campus accommodation (on first come, first serve basis), separately for boys and girls on triple sharing basis. Students wishing to avail hostel facility (on-campus) will need to deposit fee in advance.
