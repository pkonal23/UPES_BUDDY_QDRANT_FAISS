Course Overview
About LL.B. Courses
A Bachelor of Legislative Law (LL.B.) refers to a field of legal studies that deals with issues and conflicts related to society and morality. Students pursuing this course will gain essential and crucial knowledge about the law, which can be applied in the judiciary, legislative and executive institutions, bureaucracy, civil society, as well as the military and the police.

LL.B. Eligibility & Admission
Examination Pathway:
Examination Pathway admissions through ULSAT (UPES Legal Studies Aptitude Test)
The student need to meet the eligibility of minimum 50% marks in class X and XII.
Non-Examination Pathway:
CLAT Merit
Board Merit - Candidates with 75% marks in the 10th and 12th main five subjects (English as one of the compulsory subjects) are exempted from ULSAT.
Up to 20% of seats shall be filled through the Board Merit/ CLAT AIR Category. In case, any seat remaining vacant UPES has the right to fill such seats through ULSAT.

Legum Baccalaureus
in Latin, which translates to "Bachelor of Laws" in English. It is an undergraduate academic degree awarded to students who have completed their legal education and training in the field of law. The LL.B. course is the first step towards becoming a legal professional.
How many years is LL.B. after 12th?
If you wish to pursue a career in law immediately after completing your school education, you can opt for a BA LL.B. or BBA LL.B. It is a 5-year integrated degree that provides students with a comprehensive understanding of both the humanities subjects offered in the BA or BBA program and the legal principles and practices covered in the LL.B. program.
What is the scope after doing LL.B. Course?
After completing the 5-year LL.B. (Hons.) program in India, graduates can pursue a variety of employment opportunities. As advocates or legal consultants, they can practise law in various fields, including corporate law, criminal law, taxation law, intellectual property law, and environmental law. Graduates can also work in corporate legal departments, government agencies, and law firms as experts or advisers on legal matters.
