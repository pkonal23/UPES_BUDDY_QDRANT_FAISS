Program details
The
Banking and Insolvency Law (Hons.)
program at UPES School of Law offers students a comprehensive understanding of key topics related to banking and insolvency laws. Through this specialization, students will gain in-depth knowledge of various areas such as banking laws and practices, negotiable instruments laws including cheque bouncing, financial market regulation, regulatory compliance, anti-money laundering regulations, consumer protection laws, liability under Tort, debt recovery laws, insolvency and bankruptcy laws, laws on insurance, and international perspectives on banking and financial regulation.
The program is designed to enable students to critically examine and analyze these subjects, allowing them to develop a strong foundation in the field of banking and insolvency laws. Students will be exposed to theoretical concepts as well as practical aspects of the subject matter, preparing them for the real-world challenges they may encounter in the banking and financial industry.
The curriculum emphasizes the importance of staying updated with current developments in the field, ensuring that students are well-equipped to navigate the evolving landscape of banking and insolvency laws. Through a combination of classroom lectures, case studies, workshops, and interactive sessions, students will develop a range of skills, including legal research, analytical thinking, problem-solving, and effective communication.
Program Highlights
The
Banking and Insolvency Law (Hons.)
program offers specialized papers covering essential areas of Banking and Insolvency Laws, such as Banking Law and Practices, Financial Market Regulation, and Insolvency and Bankruptcy Law.
Students gain in-depth knowledge of Debt Recovery Laws, equipping them with the skills to effectively navigate the legal aspects of debt recovery in the banking sector.
The
Banking and Insolvency Law (Hons.)
program incorporates a study of International Banking and Finance, enabling students to understand the global dimensions of banking and finance regulations and practices.
Students delve into the Law on Insurance, gaining insights into the legal framework governing insurance operations and transactions.
The program is taught by experienced faculty members who possess a deep understanding of banking and insolvency laws, ensuring high-quality education and mentorship.
Students have opportunities for practical learning through case studies, moot courts, internships, and industry interactions, enhancing their practical understanding of the subject matter.
Future Scope / Industry Trends
The future scope of the
Banking and Insolvency Law (Hons.)
program is promising and relevant in the evolving landscape of the banking industry. Students of this program will gain a comprehensive understanding of the historical, political, and legal aspects of business, financial markets, banking regulations, and insolvency. They will acquire expertise in areas such as debt recovery, insurance laws, international banking, compliance, and customer relations. The integration of technology and finance, known as fintech, is reshaping the banking industry, and professionals in this field need to stay updated on the legal and regulatory aspects related to digital banking, data protection, cybersecurity, and consumer privacy. The resolution of non-performing assets and stressed assets is a key focus, with emphasis on debt restructuring, asset reconstruction, and insolvency proceedings. The implementation of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code has brought significant changes, and there is continuous evolution in the regulatory framework to address emerging challenges. Cross-border transactions and international activities require a strong understanding of international banking laws and compliance with global regulatory frameworks. The regulation and supervision of non-banking financial companies are crucial, and the industry is witnessing collaboration, consolidation efforts, and strategic alliances to enhance competitiveness and operational efficiency. Graduates of this program will be equipped to contribute to policy-making, research, teaching, and practice in the field of banking and insolvency law.
Career Opportunities
Completing the
Banking and Insolvency Law (Hons.)
program offered by UPES School of Law opens up a wide array of promising career opportunities. Graduates can embark on a fulfilling professional journey in various sectors, including banking and financial institutions, corporate law firms, regulatory bodies, and government agencies. With a deep understanding of banking laws, insolvency frameworks, and financial regulations, these graduates can excel as banking lawyers, legal consultants, compliance officers, or in-house counsels for banks and financial institutions. Additionally, they can pursue roles in insolvency and restructuring practices, handling complex matters such as debt recovery, liquidation, and bankruptcy proceedings. The comprehensive knowledge gained through this program equips graduates to navigate the dynamic landscape of banking and insolvency law and make significant contributions to the legal and financial sectors.
Placements
The
Banking and Insolvency Law (Hons.)
program offered by UPES School of Law provides students with a comprehensive understanding of the intricacies of the banking and insolvency sector. With a strong emphasis on practical knowledge and industry exposure, the program equips students with the necessary skills to excel in the field. Graduates of this program have excellent placement opportunities, with renowned banks, financial institutions, and law firms actively seeking their expertise. Whether it's working as legal advisors for banks, handling insolvency cases, or providing consultation on regulatory matters, the program prepares students for a successful career in the dynamic and ever-evolving field of banking and insolvency law.

program offered by UPES School of Law provides students with a comprehensive understanding of the intricacies of the banking and insolvency sector. With a strong emphasis on practical knowledge and industry exposure, the program equips students with the necessary skills to excel in the field. Graduates of this program have excellent placement opportunities, with renowned banks, financial institutions, and law firms actively seeking their expertise. Whether it's working as legal advisors for banks, handling insolvency cases, or providing consultation on regulatory matters, the program prepares students for a successful career in the dynamic and ever-evolving field of banking and insolvency law.

Eligibility
Interested students must meet the minimum eligibility criteria for
Banking and Insolvency Law (Hons.)
as outlined below: Minimum 50% Marks at Higher and Senior Secondary level (X and XII).
Selection Criteria
The selection criteria for students who wish to pursue a degree in
Banking and Insolvency Law (Hons.)
at UPES is determined by evaluating their individual performance in ULSAT / CLAT / Board Marks / CUET.

Program details
The
Banking and Insolvency Law (Hons.)
program at UPES School of Law offers students a comprehensive understanding of key topics related to banking and insolvency laws. Through this specialization, students will gain in-depth knowledge of various areas such as banking laws and practices, negotiable instruments laws including cheque bouncing, financial market regulation, regulatory compliance, anti-money laundering regulations, consumer protection laws, liability under Tort, debt recovery laws, insolvency and bankruptcy laws, laws on insurance, and international perspectives on banking and financial regulation.
The program is designed to enable students to critically examine and analyze these subjects, allowing them to develop a strong foundation in the field of banking and insolvency laws. Students will be exposed to theoretical concepts as well as practical aspects of the subject matter, preparing them for the real-world challenges they may encounter in the banking and financial industry.
The curriculum emphasizes the importance of staying updated with current developments in the field, ensuring that students are well-equipped to navigate the evolving landscape of banking and insolvency laws. Through a combination of classroom lectures, case studies, workshops, and interactive sessions, students will develop a range of skills, including legal research, analytical thinking, problem-solving, and effective communication.
Program Highlights
The
Banking and Insolvency Law (Hons.)
program offers specialized papers covering essential areas of Banking and Insolvency Laws, such as Banking Law and Practices, Financial Market Regulation, and Insolvency and Bankruptcy Law.
Students gain in-depth knowledge of Debt Recovery Laws, equipping them with the skills to effectively navigate the legal aspects of debt recovery in the banking sector.
The
Banking and Insolvency Law (Hons.)
program incorporates a study of International Banking and Finance, enabling students to understand the global dimensions of banking and finance regulations and practices.
Students delve into the Law on Insurance, gaining insights into the legal framework governing insurance operations and transactions.
The program is taught by experienced faculty members who possess a deep understanding of banking and insolvency laws, ensuring high-quality education and mentorship.
Students have opportunities for practical learning through case studies, moot courts, internships, and industry interactions, enhancing their practical understanding of the subject matter.
Future Scope / Industry Trends
The future scope of the
Banking and Insolvency Law (Hons.)
program is promising and relevant in the evolving landscape of the banking industry. Students of this program will gain a comprehensive understanding of the historical, political, and legal aspects of business, financial markets, banking regulations, and insolvency. They will acquire expertise in areas such as debt recovery, insurance laws, international banking, compliance, and customer relations. The integration of technology and finance, known as fintech, is reshaping the banking industry, and professionals in this field need to stay updated on the legal and regulatory aspects related to digital banking, data protection, cybersecurity, and consumer privacy. The resolution of non-performing assets and stressed assets is a key focus, with emphasis on debt restructuring, asset reconstruction, and insolvency proceedings. The implementation of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code has brought significant changes, and there is continuous evolution in the regulatory framework to address emerging challenges. Cross-border transactions and international activities require a strong understanding of international banking laws and compliance with global regulatory frameworks. The regulation and supervision of non-banking financial companies are crucial, and the industry is witnessing collaboration, consolidation efforts, and strategic alliances to enhance competitiveness and operational efficiency. Graduates of this program will be equipped to contribute to policy-making, research, teaching, and practice in the field of banking and insolvency law.
Career Opportunities
Completing the
Banking and Insolvency Law (Hons.)
program offered by UPES School of Law opens up a wide array of promising career opportunities. Graduates can embark on a fulfilling professional journey in various sectors, including banking and financial institutions, corporate law firms, regulatory bodies, and government agencies. With a deep understanding of banking laws, insolvency frameworks, and financial regulations, these graduates can excel as banking lawyers, legal consultants, compliance officers, or in-house counsels for banks and financial institutions. Additionally, they can pursue roles in insolvency and restructuring practices, handling complex matters such as debt recovery, liquidation, and bankruptcy proceedings. The comprehensive knowledge gained through this program equips graduates to navigate the dynamic landscape of banking and insolvency law and make significant contributions to the legal and financial sectors.
