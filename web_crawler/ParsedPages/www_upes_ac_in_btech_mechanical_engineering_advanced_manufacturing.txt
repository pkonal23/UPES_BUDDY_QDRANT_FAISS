**University of Petroleum and Energy Studies (UPES) - B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering (Advanced Manufacturing)**

**Program Overview**

University of Petroleum and Energy Studies offers a Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) program in Mechanical Engineering with a specialization in Advanced Manufacturing. This program focuses on providing students with a comprehensive foundation in the latest manufacturing processes and technologies.

Through hands-on training, laboratory demonstrations, and industry-focused projects, students gain practical experience in areas such as mechatronics, microcontrollers, industrial robotics, and advanced materials.

**Program Highlights**

* Emphasis on practical learning through well-equipped labs
* Collaboration with industry giants like Bosch Rexroth and Jindal Steel for practical exposure
* Student exchange programs with renowned foreign universities
* Focus on advanced manufacturing techniques and technologies
* Opportunities to work on live industry-focused projects
* Strong research and innovation emphasis, with opportunities to participate in conferences and workshops

**Career Opportunities**

Graduates of the Bachelor of Technology in Mechanical Engineering (Advanced Manufacturing) program possess the skills and knowledge necessary for a wide range of career opportunities in industries such as automotive, manufacturing, power, pharmaceuticals, 3-D printing, robotics, aerospace, and renewable energy.

Possible career paths include:

* Design and Development
* Maintenance and Quality Assurance
* Research and Development
* Manufacturing and Quality Control
* Design and Project Engineering
* Operations and Maintenance Engineering

**Placements**

The Bachelor of Technology in Mechanical Engineering (Advanced Manufacturing) program has a strong placement record, with graduates securing roles in companies such as Maruti Suzuki, Royal Enfield, Mahindra and Mahindra, Tata Projects, Shell, JSL, Godrej, IFB Industries, Larsen & Toubro, and Ambuja Cement.

**Eligibility Criteria**

* Minimum 50% marks in Class X and XII with 50% in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) in Class XII

**Admission Process**

Selection is based on performance in UPESEAT, JEE, Board Merit, SAT, or CUET.
