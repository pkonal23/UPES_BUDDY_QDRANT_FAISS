Nikhilesh Nawani

Assistant Professor

Profile Summary
Assistant Professor Nikhilesh Nawani holds an MBA in Logistics and Supply Chain Management, bringing over 8 years of comprehensive experience in consulting, academia, and entrepreneurship. As a consultant, Nikhilesh has guided organizations in optimizing supply chain processes and implementing strategic solutions to improve efficiency and effectiveness.
Nikhilesh's entrepreneurial journey has provided him with hands-on experience in managing business operations, giving him a unique perspective on the logistics and supply chain challenges faced by companies. His expertise spans across various facets of supply chain management, making him a valuable asset in both academic and business environments. Passionate about advancing the field, he continues to contribute to the industry through research, teaching, and practical applications.
Work Experience
With an MBA in Logistics and Supply Chain Management, Nikhilesh Nawani brings over 8 years of diverse experience. As a business owner, he managed operations, gaining practical insights into logistics challenges. His comprehensive background blends academic knowledge with real-world experience, making him a valuable asset in both teaching and consulting roles.
Research Interests
Research interests focus on optimizing supply chain and logistics systems, with a particular emphasis on unique challenges in hill and rural areas. He explores innovative solutions for improving transportation and supply chain efficiency in these regions, addressing issues such as infrastructure limitations, accessibility, and cost-effective distribution. His work aims to develop sustainable and practical strategies that enhance connectivity and economic development in underserved areas, contributing to the broader field of supply chain management and rural logistics.
Teaching Philosophy
Assistant Professor Nikhilesh Nawani's teaching philosophy centers on a practical, industry-based approach that integrates multidisciplinary issues and real-world scenarios. He emphasizes the importance of experiential learning by incorporating live case studies and current industry practices into the curriculum. This approach not only helps students grasp theoretical concepts but also prepares them for the complexities of the professional world. By bridging academic learning with practical applications, he aims to cultivate critical thinking, problem-solving skills, and a deep understanding of the dynamic landscape of supply chain and logistics management.
Courses Taught
Assistant Professor Nikhilesh Nawani has taught a range of courses that equip students with essential skills in the field of business and supply chain management. His courses include Supply Chain Management, focusing on logistics, operations, and strategy; Lean Management, which emphasizes efficiency and waste reduction; Finance, covering financial principles and decision-making; Business Laws, providing insights into legal aspects of business operations; and Cost Accounting, which teaches cost control and analysis for effective financial management. Through these courses, he aims to provide a comprehensive and practical education that prepares students for diverse challenges in the business world.
Contact
nikhilesh.nawani@ddn.upes.ac.in

Research interests focus on optimizing supply chain and logistics systems, with a particular emphasis on unique challenges in hill and rural areas. He explores innovative solutions for improving transportation and supply chain efficiency in these regions, addressing issues such as infrastructure limitations, accessibility, and cost-effective distribution. His work aims to develop sustainable and practical strategies that enhance connectivity and economic development in underserved areas, contributing to the broader field of supply chain management and rural logistics.

Teaching Philosophy
Assistant Professor Nikhilesh Nawani's teaching philosophy centers on a practical, industry-based approach that integrates multidisciplinary issues and real-world scenarios. He emphasizes the importance of experiential learning by incorporating live case studies and current industry practices into the curriculum. This approach not only helps students grasp theoretical concepts but also prepares them for the complexities of the professional world. By bridging academic learning with practical applications, he aims to cultivate critical thinking, problem-solving skills, and a deep understanding of the dynamic landscape of supply chain and logistics management.

Courses Taught

Through these courses, he aims to provide a comprehensive and practical education that prepares students for diverse challenges in the business world.
