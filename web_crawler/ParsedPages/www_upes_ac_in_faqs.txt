How does UPES ensure global exposure for its students?
UPES offers diverse global opportunities, including student and faculty exchange programs, progression pathways, study abroad programs at prestigious institutions like Berkeley Global, and international programs in collaboration with The University of Queensland.
Our dedicated Department of International Affairs (DIA) supports international mobility from and to UPES. The university has collaborated with top-ranking universities worldwide with more than 50 partner universities across the globe and 7 active associations with international bodies.
Under their Global Pathway Program, students complete half of their course at UPES, and the other half at partner universities in the UK, Germany, Australia, Canada, and the USA. Upon successful completion of the course at the Partner University and complying with all academic criteria, the student receives a degree from the international partner university.
Student Exchange happens between UPES and partner universities twice a year. The university also provides various internship and research opportunities to students from both UPES and partner universities.

Does UPES offer incubation for start-ups?
Yes! UPES is dedicated to fostering innovation and entrepreneurship. We offer comprehensive incubation support for startups through our mentor-driven initiative, Runway. This dynamic program serves as a catalyst for innovators across the nation to establish and grow their businesses.
Runway provides invaluable mentorship to guide startups on their journey, helping them navigate challenges and make informed decisions through a mix of grants and investments, legal and incorporation support, and allocation of dedicated workspace. The team comprises entrepreneurs, investors, mentors, and subject matter experts (SMEs) who excel in building solutions that resonate with people’s needs.
If you're an aspiring entrepreneur or have a groundbreaking idea,
Runway
is here to propel your startup journey to new heights.
What is the Runway initiative at UPES? How is it supporting start-ups?
At UPES, innovation takes flight through Runway, a dedicated start-up incubator that nurtures and supports both idea-stage and early-stage start-ups through an open application process, offering opportunities to entrepreneurs across India, regardless of their field of expertise.
The selected start-ups participate in a blended (online and offline) 10-week incubation program, which includes refining their business ideas, preparing them for the market and connecting them with potential investors.
Runway also provides peripheral support like incorporation services, logo design, etc. to help establish a solid foundation. Each startup is paired with a dedicated mentor who provides guidance throughout the incubation process and beyond.
To facilitate innovation, Runway provides access to well-equipped labs and a dedicated workspace for startups, fostering a collaborative environment. The start-ups get a chance to interact with industry experts through online and offline events and workshops, gaining valuable insights and building vital connections.
Whether you're just starting or already on your journey,
Runway
can help your startup take flight with the support and resources it deserves.
Does the UPES School of Liberal Studies offer dual majors?
Yes! UPES School of Liberal Studies is one of the first institutions in India to implement the Choice Based Credit System (CBCS). This enables students to explore their preferred discipline in-depth (major) while combining it with related disciplines (minors). For example, you can major in economics with a minor in applied mathematics or major in politics with a minor in history. Ambitious students can pursue a double major, such as History and Politics.
Our comprehensive framework supports dual majors, multiple minors, and a credit bank, allowing students to choose multidisciplinary training. This provides a meaningful academic and learning engagement with an option of exploring beyond and across disciplines. Our globally-trained faculty members guide students in their dual major journeys, providing depth, breadth, and robustness to their undergraduate programs.
The school also offers a flagship B.Sc. Research Hons. program in History and Politics, which provides students with a thorough grounding in the core disciplines of Political Science and History. Graduates of this program will double major in Political Science and History and have the option of completing a minor in Economics with Data Science and another in Environmental Sciences. In this unique transdisciplinary program, the faculty draws upon the complementarities of Political Science, History, and International Relations, towards shaping an enriching and coherent global program. The program, individual courses, and curriculum design are commensurate with those practiced in International BA/B.Sc. History and Politics programs.

How is the faculty at UPES School of Liberal Studies?
UPES has partnered with esteemed foreign universities such as the University of Bologna (Italy), University of Gothenburg (Sweden) and HEIP (France). These partnerships facilitate faculty exchange programs, enriching the academic environment. The faculty members of India also have a rich exposure to foreign universities which broadens their horizons and brings diverse perspectives to the classroom.
The dean of UPES School of Liberal Studies Professor Shubhashis Gangopadhyay is an eminent economist. He has been an advisor to the Finance Minister at the rank of Secretary in the Government of India. He has been an Advisor to the Competition Commission of India, held several high-profile public policy advisory roles, and served as a consultant on policies to various ministries including Finance, Planning, Industry, and Rural Development. He encourages his students to holistically address global problems through a combination of technology to provide a technical fix and the insights of liberal studies to bring in a cultural, political, social, and human perspective.

Is the School of Design at UPES good?
Absolutely! UPES School of Design is one of the best institutions for design in India. Here’s why:
International Collaboration:
Our partnership with IED (The Istituto Europeo di Design), Italy, enhances our global perspective and enriches the learning experience for our students.
Cutting-Edge Facilities:
At our school, you'll have access to world-class labs and studios equipped with state-of-the-art technology including 10 technology and art studios such as AR/VR, MAC, CAD, Cintiq and design studio, 3D printing machines, laser cutting machines, high-end computer workstations, vacuum-forming machines, spray-painting booths, among other creative learning aids.
Global Recognition:
We are proud members of the Cumulus Association and the World Design Organisation (WDO), a prestigious non-profit organisation based in Canada. WDO is renowned worldwide for its contributions to industrial design.
Industry Partnerships:
Our collaborations with industry giants like Tech Mahindra, Desmania, and Mosaic ensure that your education is aligned with real-world requirements, giving you a competitive edge.
Eminent Leadership:
Led by Professor Phani Tetali, an accomplished animator, educator, and cartoonist with over 30 years of experience, our school benefits from his wealth of knowledge. Professor Tetali, formerly the Head of IDC School of Design at IIT Bombay, completed his B.Tech. from IIT, BHU, Varanasi, and his M.Des. in Visual Communication from IDC School of Design, IIT Bombay.
For detailed information, please visit -

Does the faculty at UPES have good credentials?
We have distinguished faculty members comprising over 700 academicians from world-renowned institutions, including Rhodes, Fulbright, Commonwealth, Chevening, DAAD, and Erasmus scholars, along with a Fox Fellow from Yale University.
Forty-two UPES faculty members are in the list of top 2% researchers in the world, according to a study led by eminent scientists from Stanford University.
What is the research environment like at UPES?
Our faculty members at UPES are at the forefront of groundbreaking research, driving innovation, and addressing both local and global challenges. Our university has emerged as a vibrant hub of exploration and discovery, fostering a dynamic research environment.
Remarkable Research Output
: UPES has witnessed a remarkable leap in research productivity, with our faculty now producing an average of three research papers each—a substantial increase from 0.7 papers per faculty in a single year. This achievement is three times higher than the global average of one paper per faculty annually.
World-Class Publications
: Our research output is internationally recognised, with over 1,400 Scopus-indexed research papers in 2022, up from 1,100+ in 2021. Scopus is the world’s largest abstract and citation database of scientific literature. The university has grown twelve times in research output over the last seven years.
Global Recognition
: Twenty-five UPES faculty members are among the top 2% researchers in the world, according to Stanford list and eight of these researchers have found a place in the one of most coveted ‘Highly Cited Researchers’ list published by Clarivate.
Extensive Knowledge Resources
: To support our research endeavours, UPES Library provides a comprehensive collection of knowledge resources and innovative information services, ensuring that our faculty and students have access to the best tools for their scholarly pursuits.
At UPES, we're proud to offer an environment where research thrives, driving us to new heights of academic excellence.
Are life skills a part of the curriculum at UPES?
At UPES Dehradun, we believe that education goes beyond textbooks and classrooms. That's why we've introduced the School for Life that provides a well-rounded learning experience for every student, regardless of their chosen specialisation among our eight schools. Through its bespoke curricular structure, the School for Life aims to deliver a broad base of curricular, co-curricular and extra-curricular learning experience that is the unique signature of an UPES graduate and contributes towards his/her development as a 21st Century learner with strong metacognitive abilities.
Social Internships for Impact
: As a mandatory part of our holistic education, every UPES student undergoes Srijan Social Internships. These internships are immersive, full-time experiences lasting eight weeks, partnering with social sector organisations. They are a vital component of our School for Life program. These internships are not limited to a select few; they are offered to all first-year students at UPES.
Through these internships, students engage with real-world social challenges, actively seeking solutions to address them. It's an opportunity to make a tangible impact on society while developing invaluable life skills.
At UPES, we are committed to nurturing well-rounded individuals who not only excel academically but also contribute meaningfully to society.
To be part of a transformative educational journey at UPES, visit
https://www.upes.ac.in/school-for-life
What does Professor of Practice mean for a law school?
The title of "Professor of Practice" at UPES School of Law carries immense significance in our pursuit of excellence in legal education. The position is not based on academic merit and no assessment procedure is used in the appointment process.
Key Criteria
: To hold this esteemed title, candidates must possess a minimum of 10 years of relevant industry experience. They are revered experts in their specific practice areas or technical domains. Our Professors of Practice include esteemed individuals such as senior partners at top Indian law firms, Directors/General Counsels/India Heads (Legal) of leading MNCs and Indian Corporates, Retired Supreme Court/High Court Judges, and distinguished legal practitioners actively engaged in High Courts and the Supreme Court.
Teaching Excellence
: Professors of Practice are appointed for one year, with the option for renewal by mutual consent. While they are not considered professorial staff, they play a pivotal role in imparting knowledge. Their contributions extend beyond ad hoc or one-off masterclasses; they teach entire semester courses, providing students with invaluable insights into the practical nuances and real-world applications of legal principles. It is a perfect blend of practical knowledge and theoretical learning.
Professional programs
: UPES School of Law runs professional programs leading to degrees that entitle the degree holder to appear for All India Bar Examination (AIBE) to qualify as an Advocate in India. Degree holders in law may also choose to enter various other professional jobs, like in-house counsel in companies, join higher education as teachers, become an entrepreneur, etc.
Balancing Theory and Practice
: At UPES School of Law, we emphasise clinical legal education, equipping our students with practical skills in research, court craft, advocacy, counselling, and legal drafting. Our approach aligns with the recognition by the Bar Council of India and UGC of the invaluable role played by practicing advocates and seasoned professionals in the teaching and learning process of law schools.
International Best Practices
: Our policy on the "Appointment of Professors of Practice" is inspired by international best practices adopted by leading foreign universities and law schools. It represents our commitment to bridging the gap between legal theory and practice, preparing our students for success in the dynamic legal landscape.

What does Professor of Practice mean for a law school?
The title of "Professor of Practice" at UPES School of Law carries immense significance in our pursuit of excellence in legal education. The position is not based on academic merit and no assessment procedure is used in the appointment process.
Key Criteria
: To hold this esteemed title, candidates must possess a minimum of 10 years of relevant industry experience. They are revered experts in their specific practice areas or technical domains. Our Professors of Practice include esteemed individuals such as senior partners at top Indian law firms, Directors/General Counsels/India Heads (Legal) of leading MNCs and Indian Corporates, Retired Supreme Court/High Court Judges, and distinguished legal practitioners actively engaged in High Courts and the Supreme Court.
Teaching Excellence
: Professors of Practice are appointed for one year, with the option for renewal by mutual consent. While they are not considered professorial staff, they play a pivotal role in imparting knowledge. Their contributions extend beyond ad hoc or one-off masterclasses; they teach entire semester courses, providing students with invaluable insights into the practical nuances and real-world applications of legal principles. It is a perfect blend of practical knowledge and theoretical learning.
Professional programs
: UPES School of Law runs professional programs leading to degrees that entitle the degree holder to appear for All India Bar Examination (AIBE) to qualify as an Advocate in India. Degree holders in law may also choose to enter various other professional jobs, like in-house counsel in companies, join higher education as teachers, become an entrepreneur, etc.
Balancing Theory and Practice
: At UPES School of Law, we emphasise clinical legal education, equipping our students with practical skills in research, court craft, advocacy, counselling, and legal drafting. Our approach aligns with the recognition by the Bar Council of India and UGC of the invaluable role played by practicing advocates and seasoned professionals in the teaching and learning process of law schools.
International Best Practices
: Our policy on the "Appointment of Professors of Practice" is inspired by international best practices adopted by leading foreign universities and law schools. It represents our commitment to bridging the gap between legal theory and practice, preparing our students for success in the dynamic legal landscape.

What is the research environment like at UPES?

Remarkable Research Output
: UPES has witnessed a remarkable leap in research productivity, with our faculty now producing an average of three research papers each—a substantial increase from 0.7 papers per faculty in a single year. This achievement is three times higher than the global average of one paper per faculty annually.
World-Class Publications
: Our research output is internationally recognised, with over 1,400 Scopus-indexed research papers in 2022, up from 1,100+ in 2021. At UPES, we're proud to offer an environment where research thrives, driving us to new heights of academic excellence.

What does Professor of Practice mean for a law school?
The title of "Professor of Practice" at UPES School of Law carries immense significance in our pursuit of excellence in legal education. The position is not based on academic merit and no assessment procedure is used in the appointment process.
Key Criteria
: To hold this esteemed title, candidates must possess a minimum of 10 years of relevant industry experience. They are revered experts in their specific practice areas or technical domains. Our Professors of Practice include esteemed individuals such as senior partners at top Indian law firms, Directors/General Counsels/India Heads (Legal) of leading MNCs and Indian Corporates, Retired Supreme Court/High Court Judges, and distinguished legal practitioners actively engaged in High Courts and the Supreme Court.
Teaching Excellence
: Professors of Practice are appointed for one year, with the option for renewal by mutual consent. While they are not considered professorial staff, they play a pivotal role in imparting knowledge. Their contributions extend beyond ad hoc or one-off masterclasses; they teach entire semester courses, providing students with invaluable insights into the practical nuances and real-world applications of legal principles. It is a perfect blend of practical knowledge and theoretical learning.
Professional programs
: UPES School of Law runs professional programs leading to degrees that entitle the degree holder to appear for All India Bar Examination (AIBE) to qualify as an Advocate in India. Degree holders in law may also choose to enter various other professional jobs, like in-house counsel in companies, join higher education as teachers, become an entrepreneur, etc.
Balancing Theory and Practice
: At UPES School of Law, we emphasise clinical legal education, equipping our students with practical skills in research, court craft, advocacy, counselling, and legal drafting. Our approach aligns with the recognition by the Bar Council of India and UGC of the invaluable role played by practicing advocates and seasoned professionals in the teaching and learning process of law schools.
International Best Practices
: Our policy on the "Appointment of Professors of Practice" is inspired by international best practices adopted by leading foreign universities and law schools. It represents our commitment to bridging the gap between legal theory and practice, preparing our students for success in the dynamic legal landscape.
